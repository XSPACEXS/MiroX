# Variations — Team & HR Boards

## Overview

Team and HR boards must adapt to wildly different organizational contexts: a 10-person startup has different needs than a 10,000-person enterprise. A remote-first company designs team building differently than a co-located one. A flat organization structures org charts differently than a traditional hierarchy. This guide covers how to adapt Team/HR board templates for different company sizes, organizational structures, work modalities, and special use cases.

---

## Company Size Variations

### Startup (5-30 Employees)

**Org chart adaptations**:

- Only 2-3 levels needed: Founders → Team leads → ICs
- Skip the sidebar — with 30 people, the chart itself is the summary
- Include dual roles: "CTO / Lead Backend Engineer" is common at this stage
- Show founding date and current stage (Seed, Series A) in the header
- Include advisory board or key investors if relevant to team context

**Onboarding adaptations**:

- Shorter onboarding (2-3 weeks, not 90 days) — everyone wears multiple hats
- Replace "buddy system" with "everyone is your buddy" — small team, direct access to founders
- Include "company story" section — startups have founding narratives worth sharing
- Tasks emphasize breadth: "Shadow every team member for 30 minutes"
- 30-60-90 goals are more aggressive (shipping within first 2 weeks is common)

**Team building adaptations**:

- The whole company IS the team — no department-specific sessions needed
- Include founder values discussion directly (not just corporate values posted on a wall)
- Strengths map is particularly valuable — in small teams, every person's skill set matters disproportionately
- Working agreements cover the entire company, not just one team

**Performance review adaptations**:

- Simplified: 3 frames instead of 6 (Goals, Feedback, Development)
- No formal competency matrix — roles are too fluid
- Simple rating scale (Exceeds / Meets / Below) instead of 5-point
- Career path is less defined — "What do you want to learn next?" replaces a formal ladder

### Scale-Up (30-200 Employees)

**Org chart adaptations**:

- 3-4 levels: CEO → Department Heads → Team Leads → ICs
- Sidebar becomes important — headcount tracking, hiring plan, open roles
- Start introducing department color coding
- Include "dotted-line" for cross-functional product teams
- Show org changes in progress: "PLANNED" badges for roles being hired

**Onboarding adaptations**:

- Full 90-day structure is appropriate
- Role-specific customization becomes critical (engineering vs sales vs design)
- Buddy system is essential — the team is too large for everyone-knows-everyone
- Include explicit department context: "Here is how Engineering works with Product"
- Add tech stack and tool setup checklists

**Team building adaptations**:

- Department-level team building (not company-wide)
- Cross-functional team building for product squads
- Include RACI or responsibility mapping when team boundaries are still forming
- Working agreements per team, with cross-team escalation agreements

### Mid-Market (200-2,000 Employees)

**Org chart adaptations**:

- 4-5 levels with sub-department detail
- Multiple org chart boards: company-wide (high-level) + department-specific (detailed)
- Sidebar includes org health analytics, not just headcount
- Include HR business partner contacts per department
- Show functional vs product matrix overlays

**Onboarding adaptations**:

- Company-wide onboarding (Day 1-3) + department-specific onboarding (Week 1+)
- Include "cohort" onboarding — multiple new hires start together
- Add compliance and regulatory training blocks
- Include executive meet-and-greet schedule
- Longer ramp time for complex roles (6-month milestones for senior leaders)

**Performance review adaptations**:

- Full 6-frame format with competency matrix and 360 feedback
- Calibration sessions across departments (separate board type)
- Promotion panels with evidence packages
- Include calibration notes and comparative context
- Career ladder documentation is formal and published

### Enterprise (2,000+ Employees)

**Org chart adaptations**:

- Org chart is hierarchical with drill-down boards
- Top level: CEO → Business Units → SVPs (company-wide board)
- Department level: SVP → VPs → Directors → Managers → Teams (department board)
- Team level: Manager → ICs with project assignments (team board)
- Include geographic/regional overlays
- Show P&L ownership per business unit

**Onboarding adaptations**:

- Centralized HR onboarding (compliance, benefits, systems) + local team onboarding
- Multi-track onboarding: IC track, manager track, executive track
- Include virtual onboarding centers and learning management system (LMS) integration
- Global considerations: timezone-aware scheduling, language-specific resources
- 6-12 month executive onboarding with board-level integration milestones

## Organizational Structure Variations

### Flat Organization

**Org chart adaptations**:

- Minimal levels: 1-2 levels (Founders/CEO → Everyone else)
- Replace hierarchical tree with a circular or cluster layout
- Group by project, skill, or pod rather than department
- Include decision-making framework: "How do decisions get made without a management chain?"
- Show elected or rotating leadership roles (e.g., "Sprint captain this quarter: Jordan")

### Matrix Organization

**Org chart adaptations**:

- Two overlapping trees: functional (solid lines) + project (dashed lines)
- Each person may appear in two locations: their functional home and their product squad
- Include a legend explaining solid vs dashed reporting lines
- Add a RACI matrix for key processes to clarify who decides what
- Consider a "dual-home" card design showing both reporting relationships on a single card

### Holacracy / Self-Managing Teams

**Org chart adaptations**:

- Replace hierarchical tree with a circle diagram
- Each circle = a role, not a person (one person may hold multiple roles)
- Show role tensions and proposals for new roles
- Include governance meeting outputs
- De-emphasize individual names; emphasize role accountability

### Remote-First / Distributed

**Org chart adaptations**:

- Include timezone indicators on every card (e.g., "PST," "CET," "IST")
- Group by timezone overlap bands, not just department
- Show core collaboration hours (when most time zones overlap)
- Include communication tool preferences per team (Slack vs Teams vs async-first)
- Add "hub" or "office" labels for people who are co-located

**Onboarding adaptations**:

- Replace in-office activities with virtual equivalents
- Add "Home Office Setup" checklist (equipment stipend, ergonomic assessment, internet requirements)
- Schedule virtual coffee chats with rotating team members (not just key contacts)
- Include asynchronous onboarding paths (recorded video welcome, self-paced learning)
- Add timezone-aware meeting scheduling guidance
- Include tips for combating remote isolation in the first 90 days

**Team building adaptations**:

- Async activities spread over 1-2 weeks instead of a single workshop session
- Include timezone-friendly scheduling (rotate meeting times across sessions)
- Add a "Virtual Workspace" frame: photos of home offices, local coffee shop recommendations
- Donut/random pairing for social connection
- Async fun facts wall that stays open for a week

## Industry Variations

### Technology / SaaS

- Org charts include product squads alongside functional departments
- Onboarding includes dev environment setup, CI/CD walkthrough, first PR
- Performance reviews include code review quality, on-call participation, incident response
- Career ladders include IC track and management track with explicit fork

### Professional Services / Consulting

- Org charts are project-centric: show current client engagements and team assignments
- Onboarding includes client-facing protocol, methodology certification, billing system training
- Performance reviews include utilization rate, client satisfaction scores, business development
- Career ladders: Analyst → Associate → Consultant → Senior → Manager → Director → Partner

### Healthcare / Regulated Industries

- Org charts include credentialing status and license requirements
- Onboarding includes extensive compliance training (HIPAA, patient safety, clinical protocols)
- Performance reviews include quality metrics, safety incidents, peer review committee input
- Working agreements include patient safety protocols and clinical documentation standards

### Financial Services

- Org charts include regulatory reporting lines (compliance officer reports to board, not CEO)
- Onboarding includes regulatory training (KYC, AML, Dodd-Frank, SOX)
- Performance reviews include risk management and compliance adherence
- Confidentiality requirements are extreme — boards may need to be view-only with no export

### Education / Non-Profit

- Org charts include board of directors and advisory committees
- Onboarding includes mission orientation, stakeholder mapping, grant awareness
- Performance reviews include mission impact alongside operational metrics
- Team building emphasizes shared purpose and volunteer coordination

## Complexity Variations

### Simple (1-2 Hours to Build)

**Board type**: Basic org chart or simple onboarding checklist
**Scope**: Flat team of 10-30 people, or 2-week onboarding plan
**Content**: Names, titles, reporting lines, basic checklist items
**Best for**: Startups, new teams, simple HR processes

### Medium (3-6 Hours to Build)

**Board type**: Detailed org chart with sidebar, or full 90-day onboarding board
**Scope**: 50-200 person org chart, or personalized onboarding with 30-60-90 goals
**Content**: Department colors, headcount metrics, role-specific tasks, cultural context
**Best for**: Growing companies, new hire onboarding, team kickoffs

### Advanced (8-16 Hours to Build)

**Board type**: Full performance review board, or team building workshop with all 6 frames
**Scope**: Complete review cycle with goals, competencies, 360 feedback, development plan, career path
**Content**: Comprehensive assessment data, synthesized feedback, promotion readiness, compensation
**Best for**: Semi-annual reviews, team offsites, leadership development planning

### Expert (20+ Hours to Build)

**Board type**: Enterprise org chart with drill-down boards, or multi-team calibration board
**Scope**: 500+ person organization with multiple levels, or calibration across an entire department
**Content**: Full hierarchy with health analytics, cross-team performance comparison, succession planning
**Best for**: CHRO presentations, board of directors reporting, organizational transformation planning

## Special Use Cases

### Reorg Planning Board

A dedicated board for planning and communicating organizational restructuring:

```
+----------------------------------------------+
| CURRENT STATE (org chart as-is)              |
+----------------------------------------------+
| PROPOSED STATE (org chart to-be)             |
+----------------------------------------------+
| IMPACT ANALYSIS                              |
| - People affected (moves, role changes)      |
| - Reporting changes (who reports to whom)    |
| - Timeline (announce → transition → stable)  |
+----------------------------------------------+
| COMMUNICATION PLAN                           |
| - Who hears first, who hears last            |
| - Key messages per audience                  |
| - FAQ document                               |
+----------------------------------------------+
```

### Succession Planning Board

A confidential board mapping potential successors for key leadership roles:

- Show each key role with current holder and 2-3 potential successors
- Readiness assessment: Ready Now, Ready in 1-2 Years, Long-Term Potential
- Development actions needed for each successor candidate
- Risk assessment: what happens if this role is suddenly vacant?

### Employee Journey Map

Adapting the customer journey map format for the employee lifecycle:

- Stages: Recruit → Interview → Offer → Onboard → Ramp → Perform → Grow → Retain → Offboard
- For each stage: touchpoints, emotional highs/lows, tools/systems, key moments that matter
- Identify pain points in the employee experience and opportunities to improve retention

### PIP (Performance Improvement Plan) Board

A structured board for managing a performance improvement plan:

- Current assessment: where the employee falls short (specific, documented)
- Target performance: what "meeting expectations" looks like (measurable criteria)
- Support plan: what the manager and company will provide
- Timeline: weekly check-ins over 30-60 days with clear milestones
- Outcomes: what happens if targets are met vs. not met
- Important: this board must be built with HR guidance and legal review

### Team Health Check Board

A recurring assessment of team dynamics:

```
+----------------------------------------------+
| HEALTH DIMENSIONS (8 categories)             |
|                                              |
| Delivering Value:     [Green / Amber / Red]  |
| Team Morale:          [Green / Amber / Red]  |
| Code/Design Quality:  [Green / Amber / Red]  |
| Speed:                [Green / Amber / Red]  |
| Learning:             [Green / Amber / Red]  |
| Fun:                  [Green / Amber / Red]  |
| Stakeholder Trust:    [Green / Amber / Red]  |
| Support:              [Green / Amber / Red]  |
+----------------------------------------------+
| TREND (last 3 quarters compared)             |
+----------------------------------------------+
| ACTION ITEMS (what we will improve this Q)   |
+----------------------------------------------+
```
