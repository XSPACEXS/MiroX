# New Hire Onboarding Board

## Overview

- **Purpose**: A structured onboarding board that guides new hires through their first 90 days — with a week-by-week task checklist, key contacts, tools setup instructions, culture introduction, and 30-60-90 day milestone goals to ensure a smooth and productive ramp-up.
- **Best For**: HR teams, hiring managers, onboarding coordinators, and team leads preparing for a new team member's arrival.
- **Complexity**: Medium
- **Board Size**: 4600 x 3200px

The board flows left to right through time: pre-boarding and Week 1 on the left, Weeks 2-4 in the center, and Month 2-3 goals on the right. Key contacts and culture sections sit along the bottom.

## Variations

1. **Engineering Onboarding**: Replace design-specific tasks with engineering setup (Git access, CI/CD pipeline walkthrough, first PR, code review participation). Add a "Codebase Architecture" frame and "Dev Environment Setup" checklist.
2. **Remote Onboarding**: Add a "Home Office Setup" checklist (stipend, ergonomic assessment), replace in-office events with virtual coffee chats, add timezone considerations and async communication norms.
3. **Executive Onboarding**: Replace tactical tasks with strategic orientation (board deck review, investor relationships, key customer meetings, strategy offsites). Extend timeline to 6 months with quarterly milestones.

## Tips & Best Practices

- **Personalize every board**: Generic onboarding is forgettable. Use the new hire's name, specific team details, and their manager's voice in the sticky notes.
- **Make it interactive**: The new hire should check off items themselves. This gives them ownership and a visible sense of progress.
- **Assign a buddy, not just a manager**: The buddy handles the "dumb questions" and social integration. The manager handles goals and performance. Both are essential.
- **Front-load human connection**: The first 3 days should be dominated by people, not paperwork. Move compliance training to end of Week 1.
- **Set explicit expectations for each phase**: "Learn and observe" in Week 1 removes the pressure to deliver immediately. "Own and deliver" in Weeks 3-4 sets a clear ramp-up expectation.
- **Follow up at 30, 60, and 90 days**: Schedule these check-ins before the new hire starts. Onboarding does not end after Week 1.
