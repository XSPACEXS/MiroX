# Research Cluster Map

## Overview

- **Purpose**: A research synthesis mind map that takes raw qualitative and quantitative findings and organizes them into thematic clusters with evidence cards, insight nodes, and recommendation branches. Designed for the "synthesis" phase of user research, market research, or any data-heavy investigation. Raw data (interview quotes, survey responses, observations) feeds into theme clusters, which generate insights, which drive recommendations. The visual clustering makes patterns visible that spreadsheets and documents hide.
- **Best For**: UX researchers synthesizing interview data, product teams analyzing customer feedback, market researchers organizing competitive findings, academic researchers clustering literature review themes, strategy teams making sense of complex data landscapes.
- **Complexity**: Advanced
- **Board Size**: 5000 x 4000px

3. **Customer Feedback Map**: Clusters are product areas, evidence cards are support tickets and NPS comments
4. **Market Research Map**: Clusters are market segments, evidence cards are industry data points, insights are opportunities

## Build Instructions

1. Create board at 5000x4000px with Warm White background
2. Place Research Header at top
3. Create Raw Data Wall below header with unprocessed evidence
4. Arrange 5 cluster frames in a row below the data wall
5. Within each cluster, stack evidence cards vertically with the insight node at the bottom
6. Place Insights Summary frame below the clusters
7. Add Recommendations & Action Plan as a wide footer
8. Draw evidence flow lines from raw data to clusters
9. Draw convergence lines from evidence to insights within clusters
10. Connect all insights to the summary, then to recommendations

## Tips & Best Practices

- Start by dumping ALL raw data onto the Raw Data Wall — do not pre-filter
- Use physical affinity mapping gestures: drag stickies into clusters, discuss why they belong
- If an evidence card fits two clusters, duplicate it and place it in both — note the overlap
- Color-code evidence types: blue border for interviews, green for quantitative, orange for observations
- The insight statement should be a complete sentence that a stakeholder can understand without context
- Add confidence ratings to insights — not all clusters have equal evidence strength
- Revisit the "unassigned" evidence pile — it often reveals a missing cluster
- Share the Recommendations frame as a standalone artifact with leadership
- Archive the full board — it is the "proof" behind your recommendations
