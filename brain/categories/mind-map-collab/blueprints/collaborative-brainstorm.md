# Collaborative Brainstorm

## Overview

- **Purpose**: A multi-user brainstorming mind map designed for real-time collaboration, where each participant has a personal ideation zone that feeds into a shared synthesis area. The board combines individual creativity with collective intelligence — personal sticky note clusters converge into a central mind map with voting, grouping, and prioritization. Live collaboration indicators (cursors, avatars, comments) make the process feel alive and social. The result is a structured brainstorm output that captures both divergent thinking (many ideas) and convergent thinking (prioritized themes).
- **Best For**: Product teams running ideation sprints, design teams exploring concepts collaboratively, leadership offsites generating strategic initiatives, cross-functional teams solving complex problems together, remote workshops where every voice needs to be heard.
- **Complexity**: Advanced
- **Board Size**: 5000 x 3500px

## Connectors & Flow

1. Each Personal Zone --> Synthesis Center (dashed lines in participant's zone color, labeled "Contribute")
2. Synthesis Center --> Voting Results (solid Deep Violet line, labeled "Vote")
3. Voting Results --> Priority Matrix (solid Deep Violet line, labeled "Prioritize")
4. Priority Matrix (Quick Wins + Strategic Bets) --> Action Items (solid Aqua Teal line, labeled "Commit")

## Example Content

A product team brainstorm at a B2B SaaS company tackling the problem "How might we reduce customer churn by 30% in two quarters?" with 6 participants each contributing 6 ideas (36 total), synthesized into 6 theme clusters, voted down to top priorities, and converted to 5 committed action items.

## Variations

1. **Solo Brainstorm**: Remove participant zones, use a single large ideation area with color-coded sticky categories
2. **Lightning Round**: 3 participants, 3-minute timer per person, rapid-fire sticky notes, immediate voting
3. **Async Brainstorm**: Zones labeled by day instead of person — participants contribute over a week
4. **Strategy Brainstorm**: Replace stickies with structured strategy cards (Hypothesis, Evidence, Risk, Effort)

## Build Instructions

1. Create board at 5000x3500px with Soft Lavender background
2. Place title banner with problem statement at top
3. Create 6 personal ideation zones (3x2 grid) on the left side, each color-coded
4. Place the Synthesis Center mind map on the right side, connected to all zones
5. Create the Voting Results panel below the zones
6. Add the Priority Matrix (2x2) below the synthesis center
7. Place Action Items as a wide footer strip
8. Draw connector flows showing the ideation-to-action pipeline
9. Add live collaboration indicators: cursor icons, comment bubbles, reaction emojis as decorative elements

## Tips & Best Practices

- Start with everyone in their personal zone with cameras off and music playing — reduces groupthink
- Use Miro's built-in timer feature to enforce phase transitions
- The facilitator should NOT contribute ideas during Phase 1 — focus on managing the process
- During synthesis, physically drag stickies from personal zones to the shared mind map — this movement is satisfying and builds ownership
- Use Miro's voting plugin for dot voting — much faster than manual counting
- After the session, duplicate the board and archive it — the mess is part of the value
- Share the Action Items frame as a standalone view with stakeholders who did not attend
- Run a 5-minute retrospective on the brainstorm process itself — "What made this session effective?"
