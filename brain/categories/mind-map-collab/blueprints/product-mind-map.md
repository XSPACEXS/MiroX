# Product Mind Map

## Overview

- **Purpose**: A product-centric mind map that radiates outward from a central product vision, with branches for features, user personas, integrations, platform mockups, FAQ sections, and collaboration zones. Unlike a simple feature list, this mind map shows the interconnected nature of product decisions — how a feature connects to a persona, which integration enables it, and which platform it lives on. Embedded mockup frames (mobile and desktop) bring the product to life visually. FAQ and support sections ensure the map serves as a living product bible.
- **Best For**: Product managers maintaining a holistic product view, founders communicating product vision to investors and teams, product marketers understanding the full feature landscape, new team members onboarding onto a complex product, cross-functional alignment meetings.
- **Complexity**: Advanced
- **Board Size**: 5500 x 4000px

## Example Content

FlowBoard — a collaborative whiteboard SaaS product competing in the visual collaboration space. The mind map captures the complete product ecosystem: 12 core features across 3 tiers, 4 detailed personas, 20+ integrations across 6 categories, 4 platform surfaces with mockups, 6 FAQ entries, and 7 collaboration capabilities.

## Variations

1. **Startup Product Map**: 3 branches only (Features, Personas, MVP Platform). Smaller board, fewer nodes, more "coming soon" labels.
2. **API Product Map**: Center on the API, branches for Endpoints, SDKs, Authentication, Rate Limits, Changelog, Developer Personas.
3. **Hardware Product Map**: Branches for Components, Manufacturing, Supply Chain, Firmware, Packaging, Certifications.
4. **Platform Ecosystem Map**: Center on the platform, branches for 1st-party apps, 3rd-party marketplace, developer tools, partner program.

## Build Instructions

1. Create board at 5500x4000px with Soft Gray background
2. Place the central Product Vision node dead center
3. Create 6 branches radiating outward: Features (left), Personas (top), Integrations (right), Platform (bottom-right), FAQ (bottom), Collaboration (bottom-left)
4. Build each branch as its own frame with sub-nodes
5. Add the Mockup Showcase strip at the bottom
6. Draw thick colored connector lines from center to each branch
7. Add thin dashed cross-connections between related nodes across branches
8. Place decorative elements: small collaboration cursor icons, comment bubbles, reaction emojis scattered around the board

## Tips & Best Practices

- Update this mind map monthly — assign each branch to a team owner
- Use Miro links on feature nodes that jump to detailed feature spec boards
- During product reviews, use presentation mode to walk through branches sequentially
- Collapse branches you are not discussing to keep focus
- Add new nodes with a bright highlight color for 2 weeks, then normalize — this shows "what's new"
- Share the Personas branch with sales and marketing teams — they will reference it constantly
- Print the full map as a poster for the product team's wall — spatial memory helps retention
- The FAQ branch should mirror your actual help center — keep them in sync
