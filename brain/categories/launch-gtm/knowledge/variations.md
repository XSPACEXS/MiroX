# Project Overview & Launch GTM — Variations

## Introduction

Launch GTM boards must adapt to wildly different contexts: a startup launching their first feature with 3 people is different from an enterprise rolling out a platform update across 50 teams. A marketing campaign launch has different requirements than a technical infrastructure migration. This guide covers specific adaptations for different launch types, team sizes, industry contexts, and complexity levels.

---

## Launch Type Variations

### Feature Launch (Single Feature, Existing Product)

**Focus**: Incremental improvement, minimal disruption, targeted messaging
**Adaptations**:

- Simplified 2x2 board — may only need Tasks + Design quadrants for small features
- Insights quadrant focuses on the specific user pain point being addressed
- Mapping quadrant shows the specific journey stage being improved (not the full journey)
- Tasks quadrant is compact: 10-20 tasks, 2-3 teams
- Launch readiness can be a simple checklist rather than a full dashboard
- Board size: 4500 x 3000px (compact)

### Product Launch (New Product or Major Version)

**Focus**: Full cross-functional coordination, market positioning, comprehensive preparation
**Adaptations**:

- Full 2x2 Launch Command Center or Cross-Functional Swim Lane
- All four quadrants fully populated
- Insights quadrant includes market research, competitive analysis, and pricing validation
- Mapping quadrant shows the complete customer journey from awareness to expansion
- Tasks quadrant uses epic-level grouping with 30-50+ tasks across 5+ teams
- Include a competitive positioning section
- Board size: 6000 x 3800px (standard) or larger

### Market Entry / Geographic Expansion

**Focus**: Local market understanding, regulatory compliance, channel strategy
**Adaptations**:

- Insights quadrant emphasizes local market research and regulatory requirements
- Mapping quadrant shows the localized customer journey (different channels, different expectations)
- Tasks quadrant includes localization tasks: translation, compliance, local partnerships
- Add a "Regulatory & Compliance" section adjacent to the readiness dashboard
- Include market-specific pricing and positioning
- Readiness indicators should include LEGAL and COMPLIANCE as separate team lanes

### Campaign Launch (Marketing Campaign)

**Focus**: Creative execution, channel coordination, audience targeting
**Adaptations**:

- Insights quadrant focuses on audience research, segmentation, and messaging testing
- Mapping quadrant shows the campaign funnel: Awareness → Interest → Consideration → Conversion → Retention
- Tasks quadrant organized by channel: Email, Social, Paid Ads, Content, Events, PR
- Design quadrant holds creative assets, ad variations, landing pages
- Add performance tracking section with channel-level KPIs
- Board size: 5500 x 3500px

### Technical Migration / Infrastructure Launch

**Focus**: Risk management, rollback planning, zero-downtime execution
**Adaptations**:

- Insights quadrant focuses on technical risk assessment and performance benchmarks
- Mapping quadrant shows the migration flow: Current State → Migration Steps → Target State → Validation
- Tasks quadrant emphasizes sequence and rollback points
- Design quadrant replaced with "Architecture" quadrant showing system diagrams
- Add a prominent "Rollback Plan" section
- Readiness indicators focus on technical readiness: testing, monitoring, alerting, capacity
- Include an incident response plan in the footer

### Event Launch (Conference, Webinar, Workshop)

**Focus**: Attendee experience, logistics coordination, content preparation
**Adaptations**:

- Insights quadrant includes attendee research and past-event feedback
- Mapping quadrant shows the attendee journey: Pre-event → Arrival → Sessions → Networking → Post-event
- Tasks quadrant organized by workstream: Content, Logistics, Marketing, Sales, AV/Tech, Catering
- Design quadrant includes environmental design: stage layout, signage, swag, digital displays
- Add a "Day-of Run Sheet" section with minute-by-minute schedule
- Post-event follow-up plan included in the Mapping quadrant

## Team Size Variations

### Small Team (2-5 people)

**Adaptations**:

- Simplified board — may collapse to 2 panels instead of 4 quadrants
- One person may own multiple quadrants
- Less formal readiness dashboard — use a simple checklist
- Tasks quadrant can be a flat list rather than kanban columns
- No need for team color coding (too few people)
- Board size: 4000 x 2500px
- Update cadence: Twice weekly

### Mid-Size Team (6-15 people)

**Adaptations**:

- Standard 2x2 board with all four quadrants
- Team color coding becomes useful (3-4 teams)
- Readiness dashboard with per-team indicators
- Weekly standup uses the board as the agenda
- Assign section owners for each quadrant
- Board size: 5500 x 3500px
- Update cadence: Weekly, daily in final 2 weeks

### Large Team (15-30 people)

**Adaptations**:

- Full 2x2 or Cross-Functional Swim Lane board
- Team color coding essential (5+ teams)
- Readiness dashboard is the primary coordination tool
- Daily standup updates required during build phase
- Consider separate sub-boards for complex quadrants (linked from main board)
- Board size: 6000 x 3800px
- Update cadence: Daily

### Enterprise Team (30+ people, multiple workstreams)

**Adaptations**:

- Hub board linking to per-workstream sub-boards
- Main board shows only the readiness dashboard, timeline, and high-level status
- Each workstream has its own detailed board (feature team board, marketing campaign board, etc.)
- Executive summary view optimized for leadership review
- Formal governance: weekly readiness review meeting using the board
- Board size: Main hub 4000 x 2500px, sub-boards at standard sizes
- Update cadence: Sub-boards daily, hub board aggregated weekly

## Industry Variations

### SaaS / Software

**Focus**: Feature adoption, activation rates, churn impact
**Adaptations**:

- Metrics in Insights quadrant: activation rate, feature adoption, churn impact forecast
- Mapping shows the in-product user journey (not just the marketing funnel)
- Tasks quadrant links to Jira/Linear for engineering detail
- Design quadrant includes product UI screenshots and in-app messaging

### E-Commerce / Retail

**Focus**: Conversion funnel, seasonal timing, inventory coordination
**Adaptations**:

- Insights quadrant includes seasonal data and promotional calendar
- Mapping shows the shopping journey: Browse → Cart → Checkout → Delivery → Return
- Tasks quadrant includes inventory and supply chain tasks alongside digital
- Design quadrant includes product photography, email templates, and promotional banners
- Timing is critical: boards must account for seasonal windows

### Healthcare / Life Sciences

**Focus**: Regulatory compliance, clinical validation, patient safety
**Adaptations**:

- Add a "Regulatory" quadrant or section (FDA, HIPAA, IRB as applicable)
- Insights must include clinical evidence and compliance requirements
- Mapping shows the patient journey with compliance checkpoints
- Tasks must include regulatory submission milestones
- Readiness indicators include REGULATORY and COMPLIANCE as first-class teams
- No PII or PHI on the board

### Financial Services

**Focus**: Compliance, security, audit trail
**Adaptations**:

- Add COMPLIANCE and SECURITY as readiness indicator teams
- Insights include regulatory requirements and risk assessments
- Tasks must include security review, penetration testing, and audit documentation
- Design quadrant includes compliance-reviewed copy and disclosures
- Version control is critical for audit trail purposes

### Media / Content

**Focus**: Content production, editorial calendar, audience engagement
**Adaptations**:

- Insights quadrant focuses on audience analytics and content performance data
- Mapping shows the content consumption journey
- Tasks organized around the editorial pipeline: Ideation → Draft → Edit → Publish → Promote
- Design quadrant includes content layouts, thumbnails, and promotional creative
- Add an editorial calendar section

## Complexity Variations

### Simple Launch (1-2 teams, 2-4 weeks)

- Two-panel board: Tasks + Design only
- No formal insights or mapping quadrants (context provided in header description)
- Simple checklist-style readiness (not per-team dashboard)
- Board size: 3500 x 2000px
- Build time: 30 minutes
- Maintenance: 10 min/week

### Standard Launch (3-4 teams, 6-12 weeks)

- Full 2x2 Launch Command Center
- All four quadrants populated
- Per-team readiness indicators
- Cross-quadrant connectors
- Board size: 5500 x 3500px
- Build time: 2-3 hours
- Maintenance: 30 min/week, daily in final 2 weeks

### Complex Launch (5+ teams, 12+ weeks)

- Cross-Functional Swim Lane board OR 2x2 with linked sub-boards
- Dependency mapping is critical
- Formal risk register
- Weekly readiness review meetings
- Board size: 6000 x 3800px (main), plus sub-boards
- Build time: 4-6 hours (main board), 1-2 hours per sub-board
- Maintenance: 1 hour/week, daily in final month

### Multi-Launch Portfolio (Multiple concurrent launches)

- Hub board showing all active launches
- Each launch has its own detailed board
- Hub shows: launch name, date, RAG status, owner, next milestone
- Portfolio-level risk and resource allocation view
- Board size: Hub 4000 x 3000px, individual boards at standard sizes
- Build time: 2 hours (hub), standard per individual board
- Maintenance: Hub updated weekly from individual boards

## Cadence Variations

### Agile Sprint-Based

- Board aligned to sprint cadence (2-week sprints)
- Tasks quadrant updated at sprint planning and daily standup
- Design quadrant updated at design review
- Insights quadrant updated at sprint retrospective
- Readiness assessed at each sprint review

### Waterfall / Stage-Gate

- Board aligned to project phases with formal gate reviews
- Each phase has a readiness assessment before progressing
- Tasks quadrant organized by phase rather than kanban
- Formal sign-off documented on the board at each gate

### Continuous Deployment

- Board focused on deployment readiness rather than launch date
- Feature flags and gradual rollout tracked in Tasks quadrant
- Monitoring and metrics update continuously
- "Launch" is the moment the feature flag is toggled to 100%

## Presentation Mode Variations

### Executive Review (15 minutes)

Focus on readiness, metrics, and risks:

- Frame 1: Readiness Dashboard (2 min)
- Frame 2: Key Insights (3 min)
- Frame 3: Timeline & Milestones (3 min)
- Frame 4: Blockers & Risks (3 min)
- Frame 5: Decision / Next Steps (4 min)

### Team Standup (10 minutes)

Focus on status and blockers:

- Frame 1: Tasks Quadrant — Blocked items first (3 min)
- Frame 2: Tasks Quadrant — In Progress items (3 min)
- Frame 3: Dependencies due this week (2 min)
- Frame 4: Readiness indicators update (2 min)

### Quarterly Review (30 minutes)

Focus on outcomes and learning:

- Frame 1: OKR Scorecard (5 min)
- Frame 2: Launch Outcomes with impact metrics (5 min)
- Frame 3: Key Metrics — quarter-over-quarter (5 min)
- Frame 4: Retrospective — What Worked (5 min)
- Frame 5: Retrospective — What Didn't Work (5 min)
- Frame 6: Next Quarter Plan (5 min)
