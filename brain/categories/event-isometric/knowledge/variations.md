# Event Guide Immersive Isometric — Variations

## Overview

An Immersive Isometric Event Guide is not a one-size-fits-all artifact. The same underlying principles — isometric world-building, zone architecture, golden pathways, and live companion functionality — must adapt to different event types, sizes, durations, formats, and audiences. This guide covers the key variations and how to customize the board for each.

---

## Variation by Event Type

### Technology Conference

**Visual theme**: Daytime/bright or nighttime/tech depending on brand.

**Zone architecture**:

- Main Stage (keynotes, featured talks)
- Breakout Rooms (tracks: Frontend, Backend, DevOps, AI/ML, Security)
- Expo Hall (sponsor booths, demo theater)
- Workshop Labs (hands-on coding sessions)
- Hackathon Space (if applicable)
- Developer Lounge (networking, open-source corner)
- Registration and Info

**Unique elements**:

- WiFi details prominently placed (developers need reliable WiFi)
- Power outlet locations marked on the map
- GitHub/Discord/Slack community links in companion panel
- Live coding stage with screen-share mockup
- "Office Hours" schedule for speakers and experts
- Workshop pre-requisites listed (bring laptop, install X, clone Y)

**Decorative emphasis**:

- Code brackets or terminal symbols as ambient elements
- Digital/pixelated tree variations
- Robot or drone figures alongside human figures
- Holographic screen shapes near stages

### Corporate Summit / Company All-Hands

**Visual theme**: Light/corporate, aligned with company brand colors.

**Zone architecture**:

- Main Auditorium (executive presentations)
- Department Showcases (one zone per department or business unit)
- Town Hall / Q&A Space
- Awards Ceremony Stage
- Team Building Activities
- Executive Lounge
- Catering and Social

**Unique elements**:

- Company logo and brand colors throughout
- Department-specific color coding matching org chart
- Employee count and achievement stats in title banner
- Confidentiality notice ("Internal Only — Do Not Share Externally")
- Feedback submission mechanism in companion panel
- Anonymous Q&A queue linked from companion panel

**Decorative emphasis**:

- Corporate-appropriate: no playful elements, clean professional aesthetic
- Company mascot or symbol as a subtle repeating motif
- Formal human figure variants (suits, business attire)

### Trade Show / Expo

**Visual theme**: Bright, commercial, high-energy.

**Zone architecture**:

- Entrance and Registration (badge scan, welcome)
- Expo Floor (booth grid — the primary zone, largest area)
- Theater Stage (product demos, flash talks)
- Meeting Rooms (pre-scheduled buyer-seller meetings)
- Networking Lounge (casual connections)
- Press Room (media briefings)
- VIP Suite (premium access area)

**Unique elements**:

- Booth directory is the primary content: company name, booth number, category, product focus
- Booth map with numbered positions matching the directory
- "Don't Miss" flags on featured exhibitors
- Lead scanning info and badge types explained
- Meeting scheduling tool link in companion panel
- Scavenger hunt or passport challenge map overlay

**Decorative emphasis**:

- Brighter, more commercial aesthetics
- Banner and flag decorations on booth buildings
- Award ribbon shapes for "Best of Show" winners
- Larger-than-life product shapes near demo areas

### Workshop / Training Day

**Visual theme**: Warm, inviting, learning-focused.

**Zone architecture**:

- Training Rooms (one zone per workshop track, often 3-5)
- Lab / Practice Area (hands-on stations)
- Materials & Resources Center (handouts, references)
- Break Area (refreshments, informal discussion)
- Instructor Lounge (for trainers between sessions)

**Unique elements**:

- Learning path visualization: "Track A" → "Track B" → "Advanced Track" progression
- Prerequisites and materials checklist per workshop
- Instructor bios with expertise tags
- Certification or completion badge info
- Post-workshop resources (recordings, slides, code repos)
- Skill level indicators per session (beginner, intermediate, advanced)

**Decorative emphasis**:

- Bookshelf shapes and knowledge-related icons
- Lightbulb ambient elements near learning areas
- Notebook and pencil shapes
- Graduation cap motifs

### Music Festival / Cultural Event

**Visual theme**: Dark nighttime with neon and stage lighting.

**Zone architecture**:

- Main Stage (headline acts)
- Secondary Stage(s) (2-4 additional performance areas)
- Art Installations (interactive exhibits)
- Food Village (multiple food vendors)
- Merchandise / Market
- Camping / Rest Area (for multi-day festivals)
- Safety / Medical / Lost and Found

**Unique elements**:

- Set times prominently displayed per stage (the primary content)
- Artist cards with genre tags and popularity indicators
- Sound bleed warnings between nearby stages
- Water station and first aid locations marked prominently
- Weather forecast strip at the top (critical for outdoor festivals)
- Crowd density indicators updated live in companion panel

**Decorative emphasis**:

- Maximum decorative intensity: neon lights, stage rigging, spotlights
- Musical note shapes along pathways
- Dancing human figures (not just walking)
- Balloon and confetti shapes
- Flame/fire shapes near food areas
- Stars and moon for nighttime atmosphere

## Variation by Event Size

### Small Event (50-200 Attendees)

**Board adjustments**:

- Smaller board: 3500x2500px
- 3-4 zones maximum
- Simplified pathway network (fewer connections needed)
- Single-page schedule (no separate schedule section)
- Speaker info integrated into zone content rather than separate directory

**Content adjustments**:

- More personal: speaker photos, individual attendee acknowledgment
- Detailed logistics: "Parking is in Lot B, enter from Oak Street"
- Single-room events may need only a timeline, not a spatial map

**Decorative adjustments**:

- Fewer decorative elements (10-15 total)
- Smaller buildings with less elaborate treatment
- Intimate, cozy atmosphere rather than grand scale

### Medium Event (200-1,000 Attendees)

**Board adjustments**:

- Standard board: 5000x3500px
- 5-7 zones with full treatment
- Complete pathway network with walking times
- Full schedule section and speaker directory
- Companion panel for live updates

**This is the default configuration** described in the core knowledge files.

### Large Event (1,000-5,000 Attendees)

**Board adjustments**:

- Larger board: 5500x4000px or 6000x4500px
- 7-10 zones with detailed content per zone
- Multi-building zones (Main Stage area might have 3 buildings: Main Stage, Overflow Screen, VIP Viewing)
- Wayfinding system with landmark references: "The Expo Hall is past the fountain, near the tall clock tower"
- Multi-day schedule with day-by-day navigation

**Content adjustments**:

- Crowd management info: "Arrive 15 min early for keynotes — they fill up"
- Shuttle/transport routes between distant zones
- Multiple food areas distributed across the venue
- Emergency evacuation routes marked on pathways

### Mega Event (5,000+ Attendees)

**Board adjustments**:

- Multi-board strategy:
  - Overview board: Bird's-eye view with zone labels and pathway network (no session detail)
  - Zone boards: One per major area with full session/content detail
  - Schedule board: Complete day-by-day, hour-by-hour schedule
  - Speaker board: Full speaker directory with search-friendly layout
- Master board links to all zone boards

**Content adjustments**:

- Transit information critical: parking, public transport, rideshare pickup points
- Multiple registration zones with dedicated check-in areas by last name
- Real-time capacity management in companion panel
- App integration: QR codes linking to event app for personal scheduling

## Variation by Event Duration

### Half-Day Event (3-4 Hours)

**Board adjustments**:

- Compact board: 3500x2500px
- Linear timeline is more useful than spatial map (few locations, tight schedule)
- Combine schedule and spatial information into a single view
- No companion panel needed (events too short for real-time updates)

### Single-Day Event (6-10 Hours)

**Board adjustments**:

- Standard board with full spatial treatment
- Day-long timeline along the bottom
- Meal schedule important (lunch is a significant event break)
- Evening event (if applicable) shown as a distinct phase

### Multi-Day Event (2-3 Days)

**Board adjustments**:

- Day-by-day schedule section with visual day separators
- "Day 1" / "Day 2" / "Day 3" navigation in companion panel
- Evening events highlighted separately (galas, parties, networking dinners)
- Content that spans multiple days shown with multi-day indicators
- Attendee energy management: "Day 2 morning starts 30 min later for recovery"

### Multi-Day Festival (3+ Days)

**Board adjustments**:

- Larger board or multi-board strategy
- Camping/accommodation information is primary content
- Day-off or rest-day schedule if applicable
- Supply information: where to buy essentials, ATM locations, charging stations
- Weather updates critical: forecasts for each day

## Variation by Event Format

### In-Person Only

**Board treatment**: Full isometric spatial map with all decorative elements. This is the default configuration.

**Content focus**: Venue navigation, room locations, walking times, food locations, logistics.

**Companion panel**: "Now Playing" with room location, schedule changes, live polls.

### Virtual Only

**Board treatment**: Replace isometric buildings with screen/platform mockups. Each "zone" represents a virtual space: main stage stream, breakout room links, virtual expo, chat channels.

**Layout adjustments**:

- Flat layout rather than isometric (no need for physical navigation)
- Platform map: "How to navigate the virtual event platform"
- Session links prominently displayed (the URL IS the location)
- Time zone awareness: show schedule in viewer's local time or UTC

**Unique elements**:

- Platform tutorial section: "How to join a session," "How to ask a question," "How to visit a virtual booth"
- Technical requirements: bandwidth, browser, plugins needed
- "Virtual Networking" tips: how to make the most of online networking tools
- Break reminders: "Step away from screen for 10 minutes — your eyes need it"

### Hybrid Event

**Board treatment**: Split-world layout with physical venue on one side and virtual platform on the other, connected by a "bridge zone" showing shared experiences.

**Layout adjustments**:

- Left half: Full isometric treatment for physical venue
- Right half: Flat/screen-based treatment for virtual experience
- Center bridge: Shared elements (Q&A tool, polls, chat, hashtag)
- Parallel schedules: "In-Room: Panel Discussion" paired with "Online: Live Stream + Chat"

**Unique elements**:

- Clear labeling of which sessions are in-person only, virtual only, or hybrid
- Instructions for virtual attendees: "You will see the stage via Stream A"
- Instructions for in-person attendees: "Virtual attendees can see your questions — use the Q&A tool"
- Networking guidance: "Hybrid networking sessions use these tools..."

## Variation by Visual Theme

### Daytime / Bright (Default for most conferences)

**Background**: Soft Cream (#FDFEFE) or Warm White (#FAF9F6)
**Sky**: Light blue gradient with white clouds
**Ground**: Subtle warm gray or sandy texture
**Pathways**: Golden yellow (#FFD93D)
**Vegetation**: Bright green trees and flower clusters
**Figures**: Casual daytime attire
**Best for**: Standard conferences, corporate events, outdoor festivals (daytime)

### Nighttime / Dark (Tech events, galas, evening events)

**Background**: Midnight Blue (#0C1445) or Deep Navy (#1A1A2E)
**Sky**: Dark with stars, moon, and subtle galaxy textures
**Ground**: Dark gray with subtle lighting from buildings
**Pathways**: Neon cyan (#00E5FF) or glowing gold
**Vegetation**: Silhouette trees (dark shapes with subtle highlights)
**Figures**: Evening attire, some with phone-glow circles
**Best for**: Hackathons, developer events, galas, awards ceremonies, night markets

### Nature / Outdoor (Sustainable events, retreats)

**Background**: Park Green (#E8F5E9) or Forest Mist (#EDF7ED)
**Sky**: Bright blue with fluffy clouds and occasional birds
**Ground**: Green grass texture with pathways as dirt trails
**Pathways**: Brown/earthy (#8D6E63) with stone markers
**Vegetation**: Dense — twice the tree count of standard boards, with flowers, ferns, and vines
**Figures**: Casual outdoor attire, some with hiking gear
**Best for**: Sustainability events, yoga retreats, outdoor team-building, camp-style events

### Futuristic / Sci-Fi (Innovation events, tech showcases)

**Background**: Deep Black (#0A0A0A) or Space Blue (#0D1B2A)
**Sky**: Star field with nebula effects (colored transparent shapes)
**Ground**: Metallic gray with circuit-pattern lines
**Pathways**: Holographic teal (#00FFAB) with glow effect
**Vegetation**: None — replaced with holographic sculptures and light pillars
**Figures**: Futuristic silhouettes, some with AR headset shapes
**Best for**: Innovation summits, AI/ML conferences, product launch events, CES-style expos

### Retro / Vintage (Creative events, design conferences)

**Background**: Parchment (#F5E6CC) or Vintage Cream (#FFF8DC)
**Sky**: Watercolor-style clouds with warm tones
**Ground**: Canvas texture with hand-drawn-style elements
**Pathways**: Dotted lines in brown ink style
**Vegetation**: Illustrated trees with hand-drawn aesthetic
**Figures**: Sketched-style human shapes
**Best for**: Design conferences, art exhibitions, creative retreats, literary festivals

## Variation by Audience

### For Attendees (Primary Use Case)

**Content focus**: Navigation, schedule, logistics, "what do I need to know to have a great event?"
**Companion panel**: Active during event with live updates
**Shared as**: Live board link via email, app, QR codes at venue

### For Speakers / Presenters

**Additional content**: Speaker green room location, AV check schedule, presentation upload instructions, Q&A tool setup, post-session feedback collection
**Access**: Given edit access to update their session details

### For Sponsors / Exhibitors

**Additional content**: Booth setup times, load-in instructions, electricity and WiFi at booths, lead scanning setup, demo theater sign-up, badge scan instructions, sponsor benefits checklist
**Access**: Separate sponsor guide board or dedicated sponsor section on main board

### For Event Staff / Volunteers

**Additional content**: Staff check-in locations, walkie-talkie channels, shift schedules, emergency procedures, escalation contacts, supply room locations, lost-and-found protocol
**Access**: Staff version of the board with additional operational zones not shown to attendees

### For Virtual Attendees (Hybrid Events)

**Additional content**: Platform login instructions, technical requirements, virtual networking how-to, time zone conversion, recording availability schedule
**Access**: Right half of split-world board, or separate virtual companion board

## Variation by Reusability

### Single-Use Event Board

**Characteristics**: Built for one specific event, archived after completion.
**Maintenance**: Updated during the event, then locked with "ARCHIVED" watermark.
**Storage**: Saved in team's Miro folder with date in title.

### Recurring Event Template

**Characteristics**: Built once, then duplicated and content-swapped for each occurrence.
**What to template**: Zone positions, pathway network, decorative elements, section structure, companion panel layout.
**What to swap**: Session schedule, speaker directory, sponsor list, specific logistics, dates.
**Time savings**: 40-50% reduction in build time for subsequent events.

### Venue-Specific Template

**Characteristics**: Template tied to a specific venue used repeatedly.
**What to template**: Zone positions matching actual venue layout, pathway network with real walking times, logistics info that does not change between events.
**What to swap**: Event-specific content, branding, schedule, speakers.
**Time savings**: 50-60% reduction for events at the same venue.

### Multi-Event Series Template

**Characteristics**: Template for a series of related events (monthly meetups, quarterly summits, annual conference).
**What to template**: Brand treatment, zone structure, companion panel, board structure.
**What to swap**: Content for each occurrence, updated metrics, new speakers.
**Version control**: Number each instance: "TechMeetup v12 — March 2026."

## Combining Variations

Real event boards often combine multiple variations. A 2-day hybrid technology conference for 1,200 attendees (800 in-person + 400 virtual) at a venue used previously would combine:

- **Event type**: Technology conference (dev lounge, workshop labs, WiFi emphasis)
- **Size**: Large event (multi-building zones, wayfinding landmarks, crowd management)
- **Duration**: Multi-day (day-by-day schedule, evening events)
- **Format**: Hybrid (split-world layout with bridge zone)
- **Theme**: Daytime/bright for in-person, dark for virtual platform representation
- **Reusability**: Venue-specific template (zone positions pre-set from last year)

The knowledge files provide the building blocks; the builder combines them based on the specific event context.

## Summary

The Immersive Isometric Event Guide is a flexible framework that adapts to any event type, size, duration, format, and audience. The variations described here — by event type, size, duration, format, visual theme, audience, and reusability — provide the adaptation layer that transforms a generic event map into a precise, contextual event companion. Always start with the core principles (isometric world-building, zone architecture, golden pathways, companion panel, decorative atmosphere) and then apply the relevant variations. The goal is always the same: a visually stunning, functionally complete event guide that attendees open on their phones and use all day.
