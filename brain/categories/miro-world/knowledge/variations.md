# Miro World Product Showcase — Variations

## Overview

A World Product Showcase board is not a one-size-fits-all artifact. The same underlying principles — spatial metaphor, zone-based architecture, meaningful pathways, and atmospheric decoration — must adapt to different company stages, product types, team sizes, and presentation contexts. This guide covers the key variations and how to customize the board for each.

---

## Variation by Company Stage

### Startup / Early-Stage (Pre-PMF)

**Board adjustments**:

- Smaller board: 3500x2500px (fewer zones, less complexity)
- 3-4 zones maximum (Core Product, Growth Experiments, Integrations, Roadmap)
- Simplified metaphor: Single building cluster rather than full city
- More whitespace — the product is still small and the board should reflect that
- Prominent "What We Don't Know Yet" zone with open questions

**Content focus**:

- Hypothesis-driven: Feature descriptions framed as "We believe [hypothesis]..."
- Competitor positioning: Where we are vs. where incumbents are
- Runway and burn rate context: "We have X months to prove Y"
- User count (actual) with growth trajectory
- MVP scope boundaries clearly marked

**Visual treatment**:

- Light, airy background — the world is being built
- Buildings are smaller and less elaborate (no need for isometric hero buildings with 3 features)
- Prominent "Under Construction" markers on planned areas
- More sticky notes relative to buildings (ideas outnumber implementations)

### Growth Stage (PMF Achieved, Scaling)

**Board adjustments**:

- Standard board: 5000x3500px
- 5-7 zones representing established product areas
- Full spatial metaphor (city, campus, or landscape)
- Balance between current capabilities and growth roadmap
- Team information becomes important (who owns what)

**Content focus**:

- Metrics-driven: Every building has usage data, conversion rates, revenue attribution
- Team scaling: Headcount per zone, hiring plans, on-call rotations
- Platform stability: Uptime, latency, error rates alongside feature descriptions
- Integration ecosystem: Partner and marketplace strategy
- Competitive moat: What is defensible about each product area

**Visual treatment**:

- Full decorative treatment (clouds, trees, figures, pathways)
- Hero buildings for the 2-3 most important product areas
- Mature color system with zone-specific palettes
- Legend and presentation frames for board meetings

### Enterprise / Large Company

**Board adjustments**:

- Large board: 6000x4500px or multi-board strategy
- 7-10 zones representing business units or product lines
- Complex pathway network showing cross-product integrations
- Multiple audience views: executive summary, product detail, engineering detail
- Version control: "Last updated" timestamps on each zone

**Content focus**:

- Revenue attribution per product area
- Cross-sell and upsell pathways between zones
- Compliance and regulatory requirements per zone
- Enterprise customer requirements and SLA commitments
- Multi-year roadmap with investment allocation
- Organizational chart overlay: VP/Director ownership per zone

**Visual treatment**:

- Premium isometric treatment on all buildings
- Corporate color palette aligned with brand guidelines
- Executive-ready presentation mode
- Separate linked boards for zone-level deep dives
- Print-ready at A0 poster size for office wall display

## Variation by Product Type

### B2B SaaS Platform

**Additional zones**:

- API/Developer zone: SDKs, documentation, sandbox
- Admin/Enterprise zone: SSO, permissions, audit, billing
- Marketplace/Integrations zone: Partner ecosystem, app store

**Content emphasis**:

- Multi-tenancy architecture
- Per-tier feature availability (Free, Pro, Enterprise)
- Self-serve vs. sales-assisted customer journeys
- API usage metrics alongside UI usage metrics
- Integration count and connectivity

**Example zone arrangement**:

```
Core Product ← Central Hub → Admin/Enterprise
     ↕                              ↕
Analytics ← ─ ─ ─ ─ → Integrations/API
     ↕                              ↕
Collaboration ← ─ ─ → Marketplace
```

### Consumer / Mobile App

**Additional zones**:

- Onboarding Experience zone: First-run experience, tutorials
- Social/Community zone: Profiles, feed, sharing, engagement
- Monetization zone: Subscriptions, in-app purchases, ads

**Content emphasis**:

- DAU/MAU metrics prominently displayed
- Session duration and frequency per feature
- Funnel conversion at each onboarding step
- Retention curves (Day 1, Day 7, Day 30)
- App store ratings and review sentiment

**Visual treatment**:

- More playful, consumer-friendly aesthetic
- Brighter colors, more decorative elements
- Mobile screenshots integrated into buildings
- Engagement metrics visualized as "popularity" indicators (bigger = more popular)

### Developer Tools / Infrastructure

**Additional zones**:

- CLI/SDK zone: Command-line tools, language-specific libraries
- Infrastructure zone: Architecture, deployment, monitoring
- Developer Experience zone: Documentation, tutorials, community

**Content emphasis**:

- API endpoint counts and usage patterns
- Build/deploy times and performance benchmarks
- Developer adoption metrics (sign-ups, active developers, API calls/day)
- Open-source contribution activity
- Documentation coverage and quality scores

**Visual treatment**:

- Dark theme (developers prefer dark mode)
- Terminal/code-inspired aesthetics in building details
- Monospaced fonts for technical details within buildings
- GitHub-style contribution graphs as building decorations

### Hardware + Software Product

**Additional zones**:

- Hardware Design zone: Bill of materials, manufacturing status
- Firmware zone: Device software, OTA updates
- Supply Chain zone: Suppliers, inventory, fulfillment

**Content emphasis**:

- Hardware-software integration points
- Device fleet metrics (units shipped, active devices, firmware versions)
- Manufacturing yield and quality metrics
- Support ticket categorization (hardware vs. software)
- Regulatory certifications per market

## Variation by Spatial Metaphor

### City Metaphor (Default)

**Best for**: Products with 5-7 distinct, roughly equal areas.

**Zone treatment**: Buildings of varying sizes in a city grid, connected by streets.

**Advantages**: Familiar, intuitive, supports a wide range of zone sizes.

**Limitations**: Can feel generic if not customized with unique building treatments.

### Campus / University Metaphor

**Best for**: Products with a learning/development focus or clear hierarchical progression.

**Zone treatment**: Buildings arranged along a central corridor or quad, with specialty buildings branching off.

**Advantages**: Natural tour path, clear progression, supports "exploration" narrative.

**Limitations**: Linear arrangement can feel constraining for products with many cross-connections.

### Archipelago / Island Metaphor

**Best for**: Products with semi-independent modules, multi-product companies, or platforms with distinct ecosystems.

**Zone treatment**: Islands in an ocean, connected by sea routes (data flows).

**Advantages**: Natural isolation between zones, dramatic visual treatment (ocean, ships), clear module boundaries.

**Limitations**: Less connected feel — overuse of island isolation can make the product feel fragmented.

### Theme Park Metaphor

**Best for**: Consumer products, gamified platforms, or products with a playful brand.

**Zone treatment**: Theme park "lands" with rides (features) and pathways between attractions.

**Advantages**: Extremely engaging, memorable, fun to present.

**Limitations**: May not be appropriate for enterprise or serious business products.

### Mountain / Terrain Metaphor

**Best for**: Products with clear elevation levels (basic → advanced → expert features).

**Zone treatment**: Features arranged on a mountainside, with basic features at the base and advanced features at the summit.

**Advantages**: Natural hierarchy, clear progression path, dramatic visual.

**Limitations**: Implies that higher = better, which may not be the right framing for all product areas.

### Solar System Metaphor

**Best for**: Platform products with one dominant core and many orbiting extensions/integrations.

**Zone treatment**: Central "sun" (core platform) with planets (major features) and moons (sub-features) orbiting at different distances.

**Advantages**: Clear center-periphery hierarchy, beautiful dark-themed visualization, natural for platform/ecosystem products.

**Limitations**: Circular arrangement uses canvas space inefficiently; integration pathways are harder to draw.

## Variation by Audience

### For Product Team (Detailed View)

**What to include**:

- Full feature detail with metrics, status, and roadmap
- Persona connections to features
- Sprint/cycle cadence per zone
- User research findings as sticky notes
- Competitive comparisons per feature area

**What to adjust**:

- Maximum detail density — product teams need all the information
- Update frequency: weekly sticky notes, monthly metrics, quarterly structure
- Include in-progress work (alpha, beta features)
- Show backlog items alongside shipped features

### For Engineering Team (Technical View)

**What to include**:

- Architecture annotations within buildings (tech stack, service names)
- Performance metrics per feature (latency, error rates, uptime)
- Cross-zone dependency mapping (API calls, shared databases)
- Technical debt markers
- On-call ownership

**What to adjust**:

- Replace business metrics with technical metrics
- Add infrastructure context to each zone (cloud services, hosting details)
- Show data flow directions on pathways
- Include monitoring/alerting coverage

### For Executive / Board Presentations

**What to include**:

- Revenue attribution per zone
- Growth metrics and competitive positioning
- Investment allocation (engineering headcount per zone)
- Strategic narrative: "Why this product architecture wins"
- 3-year vision roadmap

**What to adjust**:

- Reduce to 5-7 high-level zones (no sub-features)
- Large text, minimal detail per building
- Focus on outcomes, not implementation
- Include financial context (ARR, gross margin per zone)
- Presentation frames for a 10-minute guided tour

### For New Employee Onboarding

**What to include**:

- Complete product overview with all zones
- "Start here" indicator
- Glossary of product-specific terms
- Links to documentation, demos, and training
- Team contact information per zone
- "Your first week" pathway highlighted

**What to adjust**:

- Add explanatory annotations that experienced team members would skip
- Include a reading order guide: "Start at the Central Hub, then explore clockwise"
- Highlight the 3 most important zones for initial learning
- Add links to onboarding resources within each zone

### For Customers / External Audience

**What to include**:

- Feature capabilities and benefits (not internal metrics)
- Integration ecosystem
- Product roadmap (what is coming next)
- Customer success stories per zone
- Getting started guides

**What to adjust**:

- Remove all internal metrics, team info, and operational details
- Use customer-facing language ("collaboration tools" not "real-time sync service")
- Highlight benefits over features
- Include pricing tier indicators per feature area
- Make the board publicly shareable (no sensitive data)

## Variation by Update Cadence

### Living Document (Weekly Updates)

**Board characteristics**:

- Sticky notes change weekly: current priorities, recent launches, blockers
- Metrics refresh monthly
- Zone structure changes quarterly
- This is the default for most product teams

**Maintenance tips**:

- Assign zone owners who update their area every week
- Dashboard header metrics auto-updated or manually refreshed monthly
- Archive old sticky notes quarterly (do not just accumulate forever)
- Schedule a quarterly "board restructure" meeting

### Snapshot / Point-in-Time

**Board characteristics**:

- Built for a specific moment: quarterly review, annual planning, board presentation
- Locked after creation — no ongoing updates
- Archived with date stamp
- A new snapshot is created for the next period

**Maintenance tips**:

- Date-stamp the title: "FlowBoard Product World — Q1 2026 Snapshot"
- Lock all elements after final review
- Duplicate the structure (not content) for the next snapshot
- Store snapshots in a dedicated Miro folder for historical reference

### Event-Triggered Updates

**Board characteristics**:

- Updated only when significant events occur: major launch, acquisition, pivot, reorg
- Between events, the board is static
- Each update includes an "Event Log" noting what changed and why

**Maintenance tips**:

- Add a "Last Updated" timestamp and "Last Event" label to the title banner
- Include an "Event Log" section documenting recent significant changes
- Set a maximum staleness rule: "If no event in 3 months, review and confirm the board is still accurate"

## Variation by Board Theme

### Dark World (Night City)

**Background**: Deep Navy (#1A1A2E) or Midnight (#0C1445)
**Building treatment**: Lighter zone colors pop against dark background; windows glow with warm light
**Decorative**: Stars, moon, glowing pathways, neon accents, city lights
**Best for**: Tech products, developer tools, night-event aesthetics

### Light World (Daytime Campus)

**Background**: Warm White (#FAFAF8) or Soft Cream (#FDFEFE)
**Building treatment**: Full-color zone buildings with subtle shadows on light ground
**Decorative**: Clouds, sun, green trees, bright pathways, people in summer clothing
**Best for**: B2B SaaS, enterprise products, corporate presentations

### Nature World (Landscape)

**Background**: Park Green (#E8F5E9) or Sky Blue (#E3F2FD)
**Building treatment**: Buildings integrated into natural terrain; organic shapes, wooden textures
**Decorative**: Mountains, rivers, forests, wildlife, natural lighting
**Best for**: Sustainability products, outdoor brands, wellness platforms, creative tools

### Retro / Pixel Art World

**Background**: Dark Gray (#2D2D2D) or Retro Blue (#1B2838)
**Building treatment**: Pixel-art-style buildings with blocky shapes and limited color palettes
**Decorative**: Pixel trees, 8-bit characters, retro game elements
**Best for**: Gaming products, developer tools with playful brand, hackathon showcases

### Blueprint / Technical World

**Background**: Blueprint Blue (#1A237E)
**Building treatment**: White wireframe outlines on blue background; technical drawing aesthetic
**Decorative**: Grid lines, measurement annotations, technical symbols
**Best for**: Engineering platforms, CAD/design tools, technical documentation

## Combining Variations

Real boards often combine multiple variations. A growth-stage B2B SaaS platform presenting to executives would combine:

- **Company stage**: Growth (5-7 zones, full metrics, team labels)
- **Product type**: B2B SaaS (API zone, Admin zone, Integrations zone)
- **Audience**: Executive (high-level, revenue attribution, strategic narrative)
- **Theme**: Light World (corporate-appropriate, clean, professional)
- **Cadence**: Quarterly snapshot (locked after board meeting)

The knowledge files provide the building blocks; the builder combines them based on the specific context. No two product world boards should look identical because no two products exist in identical contexts.

---

## Summary

The World Product Showcase is a flexible framework that adapts to any company, product, audience, and context. The variations described here — by company stage, product type, spatial metaphor, audience, update cadence, and visual theme — provide the adaptation layer that transforms a generic product map into a precise, contextual showcase. Always start with the core principles (spatial metaphor, zone architecture, pathway connectivity, decorative atmosphere) and then apply the relevant variations for the specific situation. The goal is always the same: a board that people want to explore, that communicates product reality, and that earns its place as a living strategic artifact.
