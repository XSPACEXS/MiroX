# Research Synthesis Board

## Overview

- **Purpose**: A structured board for synthesizing academic literature, organizing key findings by theme, comparing research methodologies, identifying gaps in existing knowledge, and planning next research steps — all in one visual workspace.
- **Best For**: Graduate students conducting literature reviews, academic researchers preparing publications, research teams synthesizing findings across studies, and R&D teams surveying a new domain.
- **Complexity**: Advanced
- **Board Size**: 5000 x 3200px

## Board Layout

The board is organized into a research workflow flowing from left (source collection) to right (synthesis and next steps), with methodology comparison across the bottom.

```
+----------------------------------------------------------------------+
|          HEADER: Research Synthesis — [Topic / Research Question]      |
+--------------------+----------------------+--------------------------+
|                    |                      |                          |
|  Frame 1:          |  Frame 3:            |  Frame 5:                |
```

## Variations

1. **Systematic Review Board**: Add a PRISMA flow diagram frame, add a "Risk of Bias Assessment" frame replacing Evidence Strength, and include a forest plot description for meta-analytic results.
2. **Industry Research Synthesis**: Replace academic citation formatting with company reports and market research sources, add a "Business Implications" frame, and simplify the methodology assessment.
3. **Student Literature Review**: Simplify to 10-15 sources, add an "Annotated Bibliography" frame, and include a "Thesis Argument Development" frame that maps findings to the student's thesis.

## Tips & Best Practices

- **Color-code by cluster from day one**: Assign each thematic cluster a color and maintain it across all frames. This makes cross-referencing instant.
- **Include effect sizes**: Do not just say "significant." Record d, r, OR, or whatever effect size the study reports. This enables later meta-analysis.
- **Track contradictions explicitly**: Contradictions are not failures — they are the most interesting findings. Give them their own sticky notes.
- **Update continuously**: Add new sources as you find them. This board should be a living workspace, not a one-time artifact.
- **Use the gaps to drive your contribution**: The most valuable part of this board is Frame 5. Your research contribution should directly address the gaps you identified.
- **Cite properly on every sticky note**: Include author, year, sample size, and key finding. This saves enormous time when writing the manuscript.
