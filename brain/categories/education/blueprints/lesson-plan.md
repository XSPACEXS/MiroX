# Lesson Plan Board

## Overview

- **Purpose**: Structured lesson planning board for designing a single class session with clear learning objectives, a timed activity sequence, required materials, assessment criteria, and differentiation strategies for diverse learners.
- **Best For**: K-12 teachers, university lecturers, corporate trainers, and instructional designers planning individual lessons or training sessions.
- **Complexity**: Medium
- **Board Size**: 4000 x 2800px

The board flows left to right: objectives and context on the left, activity timeline in the center, and assessment and differentiation on the right.

## Example Content

All example content is embedded above. The lesson models a 50-minute Grade 9 Biology class on Introduction to Photosynthesis:

- Three tiered learning objectives aligned to NGSS and Common Core standards
- A live Elodea plant demonstration as the engagement hook
- Five timed activity segments from hook through exit ticket
- Rubric-based assessment with a mastery target of 75%
- Differentiation for struggling, advanced, ELL, and IEP/504 students
- Realistic teacher notes addressing common misconceptions and pacing concerns

## Variations

1. **University Lecture Plan**: Replace the differentiation frame with "Discussion Prompts & Socratic Questions," extend the timeline to 75 or 90 minutes, and add a "Pre-Reading Assignment" frame.
2. **Corporate Training Session**: Replace standards alignment with "Business Objectives & KPIs," replace differentiation with "Role-Based Breakout Groups," and add a "Post-Training Survey" frame.
3. **Flipped Classroom Model**: Move direct instruction to a pre-class video assignment, use the in-class time entirely for practice and application activities.

## Tips & Best Practices

- **Start with the end**: Write your exit ticket questions first, then design activities backward from what students need to demonstrate.
- **Time buffers**: Build in 2-3 minutes of flex time. If you finish early, have a "bonus question" ready. If you run late, know which segment to cut.
- **Name your objectives with verbs**: Use Bloom's taxonomy action verbs (describe, label, predict) rather than vague terms (understand, know, learn).
- **Differentiation is not optional**: Plan for at least three learner profiles. If you do not have IEP/504 students, still plan for struggling and advanced learners.
- **Reflection after class**: Add sticky notes to the board after teaching the lesson with what worked and what to change next time. This turns the board into a living resource.
