# Course Curriculum Design Board

## Overview

- **Purpose**: Full course design board for laying out an entire course or training program — organizing modules, building a weekly schedule, defining assignments and readings, setting assessment weights, and mapping learning outcomes to ensure alignment from course objectives through to final evaluation.
- **Best For**: University professors designing semester courses, corporate L&D teams building training programs, bootcamp instructors planning multi-week curricula, and instructional designers creating structured learning experiences.
- **Complexity**: Advanced
- **Board Size**: 5400 x 3800px

## Variations

1. **Corporate Training Program**: Replace academic modules with competency-based learning tracks, replace grading with certification criteria, add a "Manager Feedback" assessment component, and shorten to 4-6 weeks.
2. **Online Asynchronous Course**: Replace session-based timeline with self-paced modules with deadlines, add discussion forum requirements, replace in-class activities with video lectures + interactive exercises.
3. **Bootcamp Intensive**: Compress to 8-12 weeks, daily sessions, project-based learning with weekly deliverables, replace the final paper with a portfolio of completed projects.

## Tips & Best Practices

- **Start with outcomes**: Design the CLOs first, then the assessments, then the activities. This backward design approach (Wiggins & McTighe) ensures everything is aligned.
- **Scaffold assignments**: Each assignment should build on the previous one. Students who complete Assignment 1 should have a head start on Assignment 2.
- **Be specific about sessions**: Vague session descriptions ("Discuss chapter 5") are unhelpful. Specify the activity, the format, and the expected student preparation.
- **Build in flexibility**: Mark 1-2 sessions as "buffer" or "catch-up" sessions for when things inevitably fall behind schedule.
- **Test the alignment matrix**: If any CLO lacks a "Practiced" cell before its "Assessed" cell, students are being tested on something they have not practiced. Fix this.
- **Share the board with students**: Transparency about the course design builds trust. Students who see the "why" behind assignments engage more deeply.
