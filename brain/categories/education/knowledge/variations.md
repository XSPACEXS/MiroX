# Variations — Education Boards

## Overview

Education spans from kindergarten to doctoral programs, from traditional classrooms to fully online platforms, from individual tutoring to lecture halls of 500. This guide covers how to adapt education board templates for different educational levels, modalities, disciplines, and special use cases.

---

## Educational Level Variations

### Elementary School (K-5)

**Lesson plan adaptations**:

- Shorter sessions (30-45 minutes) — reduce activity count to 3-4
- More transitions between activities — young learners need movement every 10-15 minutes
- Include physical movement activities (brain breaks, gallery walks, hand signals)
- Larger sticky notes with simpler language (12-15 words max per note)
- Include visual icons alongside text instructions
- Bloom's levels typically: Remember, Understand, Apply (Create for art/projects)

**Study map adaptations**:

- Use pictures and icons rather than text-heavy nodes
- Fewer subtopics (3-4 maximum) with concrete examples
- Mastery checkpoints are verbal or drawing-based, not written
- Include parent-facing section ("How to help at home")

**Visual adaptations**:

- Use the K-8 Vibrant palette
- Larger text (14pt minimum for all content)
- More white space between elements
- Include character illustrations or themed decorations appropriate to grade level

### Middle School (6-8)

**Lesson plan adaptations**:

- 45-55 minute sessions with 4-5 activities
- Include collaborative activities (pair-share, small group, jigsaw)
- Add technology integration activities (research, digital creation)
- Bloom's levels: span full range, with emphasis on Apply and Analyze
- Include explicit vocabulary instruction

**Study map adaptations**:

- Balance text and visual elements
- Include study strategy tips ("Use flashcards for definitions, concept maps for connections")
- Add self-assessment rubrics that students can use independently
- Include links to digital resources (videos, interactive simulations)

### High School (9-12)

**Lesson plan adaptations**:

- 50-90 minute sessions (block schedule variations)
- Activities should build toward AP/IB exam preparation where relevant
- Include writing-intensive activities (analysis, argumentation)
- Bloom's levels: emphasis on Analyze, Evaluate, Create
- Differentiation includes AP/honors extension and skill-building scaffold

**Study map adaptations**:

- More detailed and text-heavy than younger levels
- Include exam strategy sections
- Cross-reference between subjects (STEM connections, interdisciplinary links)
- Include college prep context ("This topic appears on the SAT/AP exam")

### Undergraduate University

**Course curriculum adaptations**:

- 14-16 week semester structure
- Include both lecture and lab/discussion section planning
- Grading structure with clear rubric references
- Office hours and TA availability noted
- Include academic integrity expectations
- Progressive project design (assignments build on each other)

**Research board adaptations**:

- Undergraduate research may be literature-review focused rather than original research
- Include advisor feedback integration points
- Simpler methodology (often qualitative or small-n studies)
- Include conference presentation preparation timeline

### Graduate / Doctoral

**Course curriculum adaptations**:

- Seminar-style: fewer lectures, more discussion and peer-led sessions
- Heavy reading load (10-15 papers per week)
- Bloom's levels: almost exclusively Analyze, Evaluate, Create
- Include comprehensive exam preparation and milestone tracking
- Dissertation timeline integration

**Research board adaptations**:

- Full research design with validity, reliability, and ethical considerations
- Comprehensive literature review (50-200 sources)
- Multiple methodology options considered and justified
- Publication timeline (submit to journal by X date)
- Committee member roles and review milestones

## Modality Variations

### Fully Online / Asynchronous

**Lesson plan adaptations**:

- Replace "minutes" with "estimated time on task"
- Include both synchronous (live session) and asynchronous (self-paced) activities
- Add "technology requirements" section (bandwidth, software, accounts)
- Include discussion forum prompts for async engagement
- Assessment shifts to portfolio, project, and peer review rather than proctored exams
- Add "check-in mechanisms" — how does the instructor know students are engaged?

**Course curriculum adaptations**:

- Module-based rather than week-based (students may pace differently)
- Include explicit "community building" activities (ice breakers, peer introductions)
- Technology tutorial content in first module
- Multiple assessment modalities (written, video, audio, interactive)

### Hybrid / Blended

**Lesson plan adaptations**:

- Clearly label which activities are in-person vs. online
- Design parallel activities for students attending virtually
- Include "bridge" activities that connect online pre-work to in-person sessions
- Technology setup instructions for both modes
- Consider how to manage simultaneous in-person and remote learners

### Flipped Classroom

**Lesson plan adaptations**:

- Pre-class: Video/reading assignments (tracked via LMS quiz)
- In-class: Start with brief clarification (5 min), then extensive practice and application
- The lesson board should show both the pre-class and in-class components
- Include "muddiest point" check at the start of class to address confusion from pre-work

## Discipline Variations

### STEM Subjects

**Special elements**:

- Lab safety section with hazard identification
- Equipment list with setup diagrams
- Data collection templates (lab notebooks, tables, graphs)
- Mathematical notation (equations, formulas displayed prominently)
- Hypothesis-experiment-observation-conclusion structure

**Assessment types**: Lab reports, problem sets, data analysis, design challenges

### Humanities

**Special elements**:

- Primary source analysis frameworks (SOAPS, OPVL)
- Discussion protocols (Socratic seminar, Harkness, fishbowl)
- Writing process stages (brainstorm, outline, draft, peer review, revise)
- Timeline and context panels for historical topics
- Vocabulary with etymological context

**Assessment types**: Essays, discussions, presentations, creative responses, document-based questions

### Arts / Performance

**Special elements**:

- Rubric-based assessment criteria (technique, creativity, expression)
- Portfolio documentation approach
- Process vs. product distinction (documenting creative process)
- Rehearsal schedule and performance milestones
- Peer critique protocols

**Assessment types**: Portfolio, performance, critique, exhibition, reflection journal

### Professional / Vocational

**Special elements**:

- Competency-based progression (not time-based)
- Industry standard references and certifications
- Real-world scenario and case study integration
- Skills demonstration checklists
- Employer or industry advisory connections

**Assessment types**: Skills demonstrations, case analyses, certification exams, capstone projects

## Complexity Variations

### Simple (1-2 Hours to Build)

**Board type**: Single lesson plan, basic format
**Scope**: One 50-minute session, 3-4 activities
**Content**: Objectives, activity timeline, basic materials
**Best for**: Weekly lesson planning, substitute teacher plans

### Medium (3-6 Hours to Build)

**Board type**: Detailed lesson plan with differentiation OR basic course curriculum
**Scope**: One session with full differentiation and teacher notes, OR 15-week overview
**Content**: Full alignment chain, Bloom's badges, three-path differentiation
**Best for**: Observed lessons, curriculum review, new course design

### Advanced (8-16 Hours to Build)

**Board type**: Complete course curriculum with weekly detail OR research board
**Scope**: Full semester with week-by-week activities, readings, assessments, and Bloom's progression
**Content**: Comprehensive with cross-references, assignment dependencies, grading rubrics
**Best for**: Accreditation documentation, program review, dissertation planning

### Expert (20+ Hours to Build)

**Board type**: Multi-course program map OR comprehensive research design board
**Scope**: 4-year degree program with course dependencies, OR doctoral dissertation with full literature review
**Content**: Course-to-course alignment, program-level outcomes, assessment maps
**Best for**: Academic program design, accreditation self-study, grant proposals

## Special Use Cases

### IEP / Accommodation Planning Board

A specialized board for planning accommodations for students with Individualized Education Programs:

```
+----------------------------------------------+
| STUDENT PROFILE (name, grade, disability)    |
+----------------------------------------------+
| IEP GOALS (annual goals with benchmarks)     |
+----------------------------------------------+
| ACCOMMODATION MAP (by activity type):        |
| - Reading: text-to-speech, extended time     |
| - Writing: speech-to-text, graphic organizer |
| - Testing: separate setting, 1.5x time      |
+----------------------------------------------+
| PROGRESS TRACKING (monthly data points)      |
+----------------------------------------------+
```

### Professional Development Lesson

A lesson plan board designed for teacher training rather than student instruction:

- Replace "Learning Objectives" with "Professional Growth Targets"
- Include "Classroom Application" section for how teachers will transfer learning
- Add "Evidence of Implementation" — how the PD facilitator will follow up
- Use adult learning principles (self-directed, experience-based, immediately applicable)

### Student-Created Boards

Templates designed for students to fill in collaboratively:

- Mostly empty with structural guidelines and prompts
- Larger sticky note zones for student contributions
- Include a "What I Already Know" section (activating prior knowledge)
- Include a "Questions I Still Have" section (metacognition)
- Simplified visual design (fewer layers, more open space)

### Assessment Rubric Board

A dedicated board for making assessment criteria visible:

```
+-------+----------+-----------+-----------+-----------+
|       | Exceeds  | Meets     | Approaching| Below   |
+-------+----------+-----------+-----------+-----------+
| Crit1 | [detail] | [detail]  | [detail]  | [detail] |
| Crit2 | [detail] | [detail]  | [detail]  | [detail] |
| Crit3 | [detail] | [detail]  | [detail]  | [detail] |
+-------+----------+-----------+-----------+-----------+
| EXEMPLARS: [Student work samples per level]           |
+-------------------------------------------------------+
```
