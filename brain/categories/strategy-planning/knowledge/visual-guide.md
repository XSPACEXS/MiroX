# Strategy & Planning — Visual Guide

## Overview

Strategy boards must project authority, clarity, and intellectual rigor. The visual treatment signals whether the strategic thinking behind it is serious or superficial. A beautifully designed strategy board commands respect in boardrooms, investor presentations, and team alignment sessions. Every visual choice — from color to typography to spacing — should convey confidence and clarity.

## Typography for Strategy Boards

Strategy boards require a commanding typography hierarchy. The text itself carries weight — it represents strategic decisions.

| Element              | Font Size | Weight          | Usage                         |
| -------------------- | --------- | --------------- | ----------------------------- |
| Board Title          | 32-42px   | Bold (700)      | Board name, framework type    |
| Framework Subtitle   | 18-22px   | Regular (400)   | Company name, date, version   |
| Section Header       | 22-28px   | Bold (700)      | Quadrant labels, block names  |
| Section Subtitle     | 14-18px   | Medium (500)    | Section descriptions          |
| Strategic Item Title | 16-20px   | Semi-Bold (600) | Individual strategic factors  |
| Supporting Detail    | 13-16px   | Regular (400)   | Data, evidence, explanation   |
| Data Annotation      | 11-14px   | Regular (400)   | Metrics, percentages, sources |
| Connector Label      | 11-13px   | Medium (500)    | Relationship descriptions     |
| Implications Text    | 16-20px   | Semi-Bold (600) | Strategic conclusions         |
| Action Items         | 14-18px   | Bold (700)      | Recommended actions           |

### Typography Rules

- Section headers should be ALL CAPS or Title Case — never lowercase (it would lack authority)
- Strategic items should be sentence case — they are analytical observations, not headings
- Data annotations should be smaller and lighter than the items they support
- Implications and action items should be visually prominent — they are the board's output

## Element Selection Guide

### Sticky Notes for Strategy Items

Strategy items are typically represented as sticky notes within framework sections:

- **Standard**: 200x150px, colored to match the section
- **Priority item**: 220x170px, with a bold border (3px) in a contrasting accent color
- **Evidence-backed item**: Standard size, with a small "data" icon and source line at the bottom

### Shapes for Framework Structure

- **Quadrant dividers**: Thin lines (2-4px) in neutral gray
- **Section backgrounds**: Large rounded rectangles with very subtle tint (5-8% opacity)
- **Axis lines**: Bold lines (4-6px) with labels at endpoints
- **Position markers**: Circles (competitors) and stars (our company) on positioning maps

### Frames for Grouping

- **Each framework section**: Framed with title, subtle background
- **Implications zone**: Distinct frame with accent-colored border
- **Evidence zone**: Small frame within or beside strategic items

### Connectors for Strategic Relationships

| Relationship Type             | Style             | Color         | Label             |
| ----------------------------- | ----------------- | ------------- | ----------------- |
| Strength enables Opportunity  | Solid arrow, 2px  | Green         | "enables"         |
| Weakness amplifies Threat     | Solid arrow, 2px  | Red           | "amplifies"       |
| Initiative supports Objective | Solid arrow, 3px  | Primary       | "supports"        |
| Causal link                   | Dashed arrow, 2px | Gray          | Relationship type |
| Cross-section insight         | Dotted, 1px       | Accent (gold) | Key insight       |

## Decorative Elements

### The "Key Insight" Callout

A bordered text block with a left accent bar (6px, gold) containing the board's single most important strategic insight. Placed prominently near the center or at the top of the implications zone. Font: 18-20px, italic, bold. This is the "if you read nothing else, read this" element.

### Section Scoring Indicators

Small horizontal bars or filled circles showing the relative weight or priority of items within a section. Creates an instant visual hierarchy without reading text.

### Confidence Borders

- **Solid border**: Confirmed by data
- **Dashed border**: Estimated or projected
- **Dotted border**: Hypothesis or speculation
  This system prevents viewers from treating all items with equal certainty.

### Strategic Theme Banners

Horizontal colored banners spanning the full board width, marking strategic themes that cross multiple framework sections. Creates a meta-layer of meaning on top of the framework structure.

## Accessibility and Visual Quality

### Critical Requirements

- All strategic items must be legible at medium zoom (the entire framework should be readable without zooming into individual stickies)
- Positive and negative sections must be distinguishable by non-color means (position labels, icons, text markers)
- The framework type must be identifiable within 3 seconds at full zoom-out
- Implications/actions must be visually prominent — they are the board's most important output

### Quality Checklist

- [ ] Framework structure is instantly recognizable
- [ ] Section colors follow positive/negative valence consistently
- [ ] All items contain specific, evidence-backed content (no generic filler)
- [ ] Cross-section connections are drawn where relevant
- [ ] Implications/actions zone is populated and prominent
- [ ] Typography hierarchy creates clear visual distinction between levels
- [ ] The board can be understood without a presenter explaining it
- [ ] Sources and data references are included where relevant
- [ ] The board has been reviewed by someone who was not involved in creating it
