# Content Guide — Marketing Enablement Board

## Domain Knowledge: The Marketing Operations Universe

### What Belongs on a Marketing Enablement Board

A Marketing Enablement Board is the operational hub for an entire marketing organization. It must contain everything a marketing team member needs to do their job — and nothing else. The content falls into seven operational categories.

**The seven content categories:**

1. **Campaign Management**: Active campaigns, their status, goals, budgets, timelines, and results
2. **Content Pipeline**: Blog posts, emails, social content, videos — their production status from ideation through publishing
3. **Design & Creative**: Landing page mockups, ad creatives, brand assets — their review and approval status
4. **Metrics & Analytics**: Funnel performance, channel metrics, CAC, LTV, attribution, and trend analysis
5. **Competitive Intelligence**: Competitor activities, positioning, messaging, and market landscape
6. **Assets & Resources**: Brand guidelines, templates, toolkits, approved messaging, logos, and photography
7. **Calendar & Planning**: Content calendar, campaign timeline, event schedule, and team availability

## Metric Standards for Marketing

### The Marketing Metrics Hierarchy

| Level      | Metric                            | Why It Matters               | Update Frequency |
| ---------- | --------------------------------- | ---------------------------- | ---------------- |
| North Star | Marketing Qualified Leads (MQLs)  | Direct pipeline contribution | Weekly           |
| Revenue    | Pipeline Generated ($)            | Revenue impact               | Weekly           |
| Revenue    | Customer Acquisition Cost (CAC)   | Efficiency                   | Monthly          |
| Revenue    | Marketing ROI                     | Overall return               | Monthly          |
| Funnel     | Awareness (impressions, reach)    | Top of funnel health         | Weekly           |
| Funnel     | Engagement (clicks, time on site) | Content effectiveness        | Weekly           |
| Funnel     | Conversion Rate (visitor to lead) | Funnel efficiency            | Weekly           |
| Channel    | Organic Traffic                   | SEO health                   | Monthly          |
| Channel    | Paid ROAS                         | Ad efficiency                | Weekly           |
| Channel    | Email Open/Click Rate             | Email health                 | Per campaign     |
| Channel    | Social Engagement Rate            | Social health                | Weekly           |

### Metric Display Rules

1. Every metric MUST have a target or benchmark — a number without context is meaningless
2. Every metric MUST show trend direction (up arrow, down arrow, flat)
3. Color coding: Green if meeting/exceeding target, Yellow if within 10% of target, Red if more than 10% behind
4. Include the data source (Google Analytics, HubSpot, Salesforce) so people know where to verify
5. Include last-updated date — stale metrics are worse than no metrics

## Writing Style for Enablement Boards

### Tone

- **Action-oriented**: Everything should answer "what do I need to do?" or "what is happening?"
- **Concise**: Maximum 2 sentences per sticky note. If it needs more, it belongs in a linked document.
- **Status-forward**: Lead with the status, then the detail. "BLOCKED: Landing page copy pending legal review" not "The landing page copy has been sent to legal for review and we are currently waiting."
- **Honest**: If something is behind, say it is behind. The board is for operational truth, not optimistic reports.

### Naming Conventions

- Campaign names: "[Quarter] [Type] — [Brief Name]" (e.g., "Q2 Launch — FlowBoard AI")
- Content pieces: "[Type]: [Title]" (e.g., "Blog: 10 Ways to Reduce Churn")
- Metrics: Use standard abbreviations (CAC, LTV, MQL, SQL, MRR, ARR, ROAS)
- Status labels: Use EXACTLY the standard labels (DRAFT, IN REVIEW, APPROVED, LIVE, PAUSED, DEPRECATED)

### Content Length Limits

| Element               | Max Length    | Example                                                          |
| --------------------- | ------------- | ---------------------------------------------------------------- |
| Campaign name         | 40 characters | "Q2 Launch — FlowBoard AI"                                       |
| Sticky note text      | 30 words      | One idea, one status, one action                                 |
| Metric interpretation | 15 words      | "CAC trending down — paid campaigns more efficient this quarter" |
| Section header        | 4 words       | "Content Pipeline"                                               |
| Status label          | 2 words       | "IN REVIEW"                                                      |
