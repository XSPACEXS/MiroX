# Enablement War Room

## Overview

- **Purpose**: A central command center for marketing operations — a massive workspace where campaign trackers, asset libraries, approval workflows, performance dashboards, and team coordination all live in one visual board. Inspired by military war rooms and NASA mission control, this board gives marketing leaders a single-pane-of-glass view of everything in flight, everything planned, and everything measured.
- **Best For**: Marketing operations managers running multi-channel campaigns, CMOs needing portfolio visibility, growth teams coordinating launches, agencies managing multiple client campaigns simultaneously.
- **Complexity**: Advanced
- **Board Size**: 5000 x 3500px

## Example Content

Fictitious "NovaTech" marketing department running Q1 2026 operations. 34-person team, $2.4M quarterly budget, 12 active campaigns.

## Variations

1. **Agency War Room**: Replace team roster with client roster. Add client approval columns.
2. **Product Launch War Room**: Focus on a single launch with deeper phases.
3. **Content-Only War Room**: Remove paid and events, expand content pipeline and editorial calendar.
4. **Startup Marketing War Room**: Smaller scale, 5-person team, $200K budget.

## Build Instructions

1. Create board at 5000x3500px, Dark Charcoal background
2. Place title banner at top with mission control aesthetic
3. Create top row: Campaigns, Assets, Approvals
4. Create middle row: Performance Dashboard (wider), Calendar
5. Create bottom row: Team Roster, Budget, Competitive Intel
6. Draw connectors between related sections
7. Add status color-coding throughout

## Tips & Best Practices

- Update this board daily during campaign sprints — it becomes the team's heartbeat
- Use Miro's timer feature during war room stand-ups to keep updates to 15 minutes
- Link campaign cards to detailed campaign boards with full briefs and creative assets
- Set up Miro notifications for approval workflow changes
- Review performance dashboard weekly with leadership — export as PDF for exec summaries
- Archive completed campaigns quarterly to keep the board focused on active work
