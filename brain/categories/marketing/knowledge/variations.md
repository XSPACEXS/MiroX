# Marketing — Variations

## Overview

Marketing boards must adapt to dramatically different contexts — a VC-funded startup's growth marketing board looks nothing like a non-profit's donor engagement plan, and an enterprise B2B campaign differs fundamentally from a D2C product launch. This document covers how to adapt marketing boards across industries, team sizes, complexity levels, cultural contexts, and integrations with other board types.

---

## Industry Adaptations

### SaaS / B2B Software

**Board emphasis**: Funnel Board + Campaign Board + Content Calendar
**Unique marketing concerns**: Lead generation, product-led growth, content marketing, ABM (Account-Based Marketing), freemium conversion, churn reduction

**Content adaptations**:

- Funnel stages use SaaS terminology: Visitor, Lead, MQL, SQL, Opportunity, Customer, Expansion
- Campaign boards include product usage triggers (in-app messages, feature adoption prompts)
- Content calendars integrate with product launch timelines
- Metrics emphasize unit economics: CAC, LTV, LTV:CAC ratio, payback period, NRR
- ABM campaign boards add a "target account" layer — campaigns are planned per account, not just per persona

**Visual adaptations**:

- Use Modern Growth palette (Tech Blue + Growth Teal)
- Include a "Product-Led" section showing in-app marketing touchpoints
- Funnel boards show both marketing and product funnels side by side (acquisition + activation + retention)
- Content calendar tags every piece with funnel stage AND buyer persona
- Add a "Competitive Response" zone for reactive marketing when competitors launch

**Example campaign card**: "LinkedIn Ads — Target: VP Engineering at companies using Competitor X. Message: 'Migrate from X to Us in 48 hours — we handle the data transfer.' Budget: $8K. Expected: 200 MQLs. Funnel: BOFU. Trigger: Competitor X price increase announcement."

### E-Commerce / D2C

**Board emphasis**: Campaign Board + Content Calendar + Funnel Board
**Unique marketing concerns**: Seasonal peaks, ROAS (Return on Ad Spend), cart abandonment, email lifecycle, influencer partnerships, marketplace strategy

**Content adaptations**:

- Campaign boards include seasonal overlay: Black Friday, Cyber Monday, Valentine's Day, Back-to-School, Holiday
- Funnel stages are purchase-focused: Browse, Add to Cart, Checkout, Purchase, Repeat Purchase, Refer
- Content calendars are heavily social-media-weighted (Instagram, TikTok, Pinterest)
- Metrics emphasize: ROAS, AOV (Average Order Value), cart abandonment rate, email revenue per send, customer repeat rate
- Influencer collaboration section: partnerships, deliverables, timelines, tracking codes

**Visual adaptations**:

- Use Brand Forward palette (Crimson + Gold) for energy and urgency
- Include product imagery prominently — campaign boards should show the actual product
- Seasonal calendar overlay with color-coded peak periods
- "Flash Sale" visual treatment: urgent red borders, countdown timer graphics
- Influencer partnership cards with social proof metrics (follower count, engagement rate, past campaign performance)

**Example campaign card**: "Instagram Reels — Summer Collection Launch. 5 influencers (combined 2.3M followers), 3 reels each over 2 weeks. Budget: $25K ($5K/influencer). Expected: 850K impressions, 12K site visits, 480 purchases. ROAS target: 3.2x. Tracking: UTM + unique discount codes per influencer."

### Healthcare / Pharma

**Board emphasis**: Campaign Board + Brand Guidelines + Funnel Board
**Unique marketing concerns**: Regulatory compliance (FDA, HIPAA), medical claims review, HCP (Healthcare Professional) vs. patient marketing, long sales cycles, evidence-based messaging

**Content adaptations**:

- Every content piece requires a "Medical/Legal/Regulatory (MLR) Review" status indicator
- Campaign boards include a compliance checkpoint column — content cannot launch without approval
- Messaging must distinguish between "promotional" and "educational" content (regulatory distinction)
- Funnel stages for HCP marketing: Awareness, Education, Clinical Evidence Review, Formulary Decision, Prescribing
- Metrics include: HCP reach, sample requests, formulary additions, patient starts

**Visual adaptations**:

- Use Balanced Wisdom or Premium Brand palette — healthcare requires trust and authority
- Add a "Compliance Status" badge on every card: Approved, Under Review, Needs Revision
- Red "REGULATED CONTENT" markers on cards subject to FDA/FTC oversight
- Separate board zones for HCP marketing vs. Patient marketing (different audiences, different rules)
- Evidence level indicators on content: Clinical Trial Data, Real-World Evidence, Expert Opinion

**Example campaign card**: "Educational webinar series for cardiologists — 'New Evidence in Anticoagulation Management.' 3 sessions, monthly. KOL: Dr. Sarah Chen (Cleveland Clinic). Budget: $35K. Expected: 800 HCP registrations, 120 sample requests. MLR Status: APPROVED (ref: MLR-2026-0342). Non-promotional — educational only."

### Financial Services

**Board emphasis**: Campaign Board + Brand Guidelines + Content Calendar
**Unique marketing concerns**: Regulatory compliance (SEC, FINRA), trust building, data privacy, conservative messaging, multi-stakeholder audiences (retail, institutional, advisors)

**Content adaptations**:

- All content requires compliance review with FINRA/SEC disclaimer requirements
- Campaign boards separate retail investor marketing from institutional marketing
- Content calendars align with market events: earnings seasons, Fed meetings, regulatory announcements
- Metrics include: AUM growth attribution, advisor engagement, account opening rate, seminar attendance
- Tone is conservative and factual — no exaggerated claims or projected returns

**Visual adaptations**:

- Use Executive Authority or Premium Brand palette — financial audiences expect gravitas
- Compliance disclaimer zone at the bottom of every content card
- "Market Conditions" banner showing current macro context that affects messaging
- Conservative typography — serif or clean sans-serif, no playful fonts
- Testimonials require "past performance" disclaimers — build these into the card template

### Non-Profit / Cause Marketing

**Board emphasis**: Campaign Board + Content Calendar + Funnel Board (donor funnel)
**Unique marketing concerns**: Donor engagement, storytelling, impact communication, grant reporting, volunteer recruitment, community building

**Content adaptations**:

- Replace revenue metrics with impact metrics: donors acquired, funds raised, beneficiaries served
- Funnel stages for donor journey: Aware, Informed, Engaged, First Gift, Recurring Donor, Major Gift, Legacy
- Campaign boards include storytelling elements: beneficiary stories, impact data, emotion-driven content
- Content calendars align with giving seasons: year-end giving, GivingTuesday, spring appeals
- Budget conversations emphasize "efficiency ratio" (% of funds going to program vs. overhead)

**Visual adaptations**:

- Use Community & Impact palette (Mission Blue + Earth Green + Impact Orange)
- Include beneficiary photos or story cards prominently (with appropriate consent)
- Impact metrics displayed with emotional weight: "1,247 students received tutoring this quarter"
- Donor thank-you touchpoints visible on the timeline (gratitude is critical for retention)
- "Impact Report" zone showing how marketing spend translates to mission outcomes

**Example campaign card**: "Year-End Giving Campaign — 'Every Child Reads.' Target: Raise $500K from existing donor base (5,200 donors). Channels: Direct mail (2 pieces), Email (5-part sequence), Social (Instagram stories featuring student voices). Average gift target: $96. Theme: Bridge the reading gap — $50 = one semester of tutoring for one student."

### Agency / Marketing Services

**Board emphasis**: Campaign Board (per client) + Content Calendar + Brand Guidelines (client brands)
**Unique marketing concerns**: Multi-client management, scope creep, revision limits, billable hours, client approval workflows

**Content adaptations**:

- Campaign boards are per-client with client brand identity applied
- Revision tracking on every content card: "Rev 1, Rev 2, Rev 3" with escalating warning colors
- Scope indicator: "In scope" (green tag) vs. "Out of scope" (red tag) vs. "Proposed addition" (gold tag)
- Time tracking: Estimated hours vs. actual hours per deliverable
- Client approval status prominently displayed: Pending, Feedback Received, Approved

**Visual adaptations**:

- Board uses the client's brand palette (not the agency's)
- Revision count badges on cards with color escalation: Rev 1 (green), Rev 2 (yellow), Rev 3 (red)
- Client feedback zone: a dedicated section where client comments are captured
- Scope change alerts: gold-bordered cards for items requested outside the original brief
- Multi-client dashboard: shows all active clients with project status summary

## Team Size Adaptations

### Solo Marketer (1 person)

**Board simplification**:

- Use a combined Campaign + Calendar board (one board for everything)
- 3 columns: This Week, Next Week, Backlog
- Skip persona sections (you know your audience — just reference the persona name)
- Metrics: Track 3 KPIs maximum (any more is overwhelming for one person)
- Board size: 4000x3000px

**Unique to solo**:

- Personal energy management: Tag tasks as "Creative" (high energy) vs. "Administrative" (low energy)
- Tool stack visible: Which tools are used for which channels (reduces context-switching)
- Repurposing plan: How one content piece becomes 5 (blog -> social -> email -> video -> webinar)
- Weekly review zone: What performed, what to double down on, what to stop

### Small Marketing Team (3-5 people)

**Standard setup**:

- Separate Campaign Board and Content Calendar
- Assignee on every card (visible at medium zoom)
- Channel ownership clear: "Sarah owns email, Tom owns social, Maria owns content"
- WIP limits per person: No more than 3 active projects per marketer
- Board size: 6000x4500px

**Unique to small teams**:

- Capacity indicator: Show each person's current workload
- Cross-training markers: "Maria learning paid ads this sprint"
- Shared asset library reference: "Campaign assets in Google Drive > Q2 > Spring Blitz"
- Team standup format: Board structured for 15-minute weekly marketing standup

### Marketing Department (10-20 people)

**Enhanced structure**:

- Sub-teams have their own boards: Content Team Calendar, Demand Gen Campaign Board, Brand Team Guidelines
- Master marketing dashboard aggregates metrics from all sub-team boards
- Cross-team dependency tracking: "Content team must deliver blog post before Demand Gen can run paid campaign"
- Board size: 8000-10000px per side per board

**Unique to departments**:

- Channel leadership matrix: Who owns which channel and which metrics
- Budget allocation by sub-team with performance tracking
- Campaign coordination calendar showing all teams' activities on one timeline
- Escalation path for cross-team blockers
- Marketing all-hands format: The dashboard board serves as the meeting agenda

### Enterprise Marketing Organization (50+ people)

**Portfolio structure**:

- Regional marketing boards (Americas, EMEA, APAC) linked to global strategy
- Product-line marketing boards linked to product teams
- Brand management board as the single source of truth for identity
- Executive marketing dashboard aggregating all regional and product boards
- Board size: 12000px+ or multiple linked boards

**Unique to enterprise**:

- Governance: Who approves campaigns, what requires legal review, escalation paths
- Brand consistency monitoring across regions and product lines
- Budget reconciliation: Planned vs. actual spend across the organization
- Campaign conflict detection: Two teams targeting the same audience with different messages
- Global/local balance: Core messaging that is consistent globally with local adaptation guidelines

## Complexity Levels

### Quick / Simple (15 minutes to build)

**Specifications**:

- Board size: 3500x2500px
- Single campaign or single month calendar
- 10-20 cards total
- No metrics dashboard (verbal discussion instead)
- Default styling with channel color coding only

**When to use**: Weekly marketing standup, quick campaign brainstorm, social media planning for the week

### Standard (30-45 minutes to build)

**Specifications**:

- Board size: 6000-8000px per side
- Full campaign board or monthly content calendar
- 20-50 cards with rich metadata
- Metric widgets (3-5 KPIs)
- Budget allocation visible
- Custom palette applied, styled headers

**When to use**: Sprint-based campaign planning, monthly content calendar, funnel analysis, quarterly review

### Advanced (2+ hours to build)

**Specifications**:

- Board size: 8000-14000px per side
- Multi-channel campaign board + timeline + metrics dashboard
- 50-100+ cards across multiple sections
- Full funnel analysis with conversion rates and optimization plans
- Budget-to-ROI chain visible
- Cross-channel orchestration mapped
- Export-ready quality for stakeholder presentations

**When to use**: Major product launch, annual marketing plan, investor-facing marketing strategy, brand guidelines creation

## Cultural Considerations

### Distributed / Remote Marketing Teams

- Board must be self-documenting — no "I'll explain in the meeting" dependencies
- Include a "How to Use This Board" frame for new team members or external partners
- Use explicit naming: "Blog post about SOC2 automation for VP Compliance audience" not "that blog post Sarah mentioned"
- Async-friendly update zones: "Weekly Update" section where team members post status without needing a meeting
- Timezone indicators on deadlines: "March 15, 5pm ET / 10pm GMT"

### Data-Privacy-Conscious Markets (EU / GDPR)

- Avoid displaying personal data on boards visible to unauthorized viewers
- Customer examples use anonymized data: "Customer A (Healthcare, 500 beds)" not "Memorial Hospital"
- GDPR compliance notes on campaign cards processing personal data
- Data retention awareness: Old campaign boards should be archived per data retention policies
- Consent tracking: Campaigns touching personal data should note the consent basis

### Multi-Language / Multi-Market Organizations

- Board language should match the primary team's language, with key terms translated for other markets
- Campaign boards for multi-market launches should show local adaptations side by side
- Messaging localization tracking: "English copy approved. German localization: In Review. French: Pending."
- Cultural sensitivity flags on content that may need local adaptation (humor, imagery, references)

### High-Velocity / Startup Marketing

- Speed over perfection: Boards update daily, not weekly
- Experiment-oriented: Every campaign card should include a hypothesis and expected outcome
- "Kill criteria" on campaigns: "If CPL exceeds $150 by day 7, pause and reassess"
- Rapid iteration tracking: Version numbers on campaign strategies (v1, v2, v3)
- Lean metrics: Focus on 2-3 metrics that matter most, not comprehensive dashboards

## Integration with Other Board Types

### Marketing Board to Strategy Board

**Transition point**: When marketing needs strategic context — positioning, competitive landscape, organizational priorities.

**How to connect**: SWOT strengths inform marketing messaging (what to emphasize). SWOT opportunities inform campaign targeting (where to invest). Competitive positioning informs differentiation messaging.

**Mapping**:

- SWOT Strengths become marketing messaging pillars
- SWOT Opportunities become campaign target segments
- Competitive positioning gaps become differentiation angles
- OKR targets become marketing KPI targets

### Marketing Board to Project Management Board

**Transition point**: When campaign execution needs task-level tracking with assignees and deadlines.

**How to connect**: Each campaign activity on the marketing board spawns tasks on the PM board. Campaign milestones become project milestones. Marketing deadlines feed into sprint planning.

**Mapping**:

- Campaign activity card becomes a project task or epic
- Campaign timeline milestones become PM board deadlines
- Channel owner becomes task assignee
- Campaign KPIs become project success criteria

### Marketing Board to Mind Map

**Transition point**: When the marketing team needs to brainstorm — campaign themes, content ideas, audience exploration, messaging options.

**How to connect**: A content theme or campaign challenge becomes the mind map's central topic. Brainstormed ideas become campaign cards or content pieces on the marketing board.

**Mapping**:

- Campaign challenge becomes mind map central topic
- Brainstorm output becomes campaign activity cards
- Content ideas become content calendar entries
- Audience insights become persona refinements

### Marketing Board to Sales Enablement

**Transition point**: When marketing campaigns need sales support, or when sales needs marketing materials.

**How to connect**: Campaign launch triggers sales enablement material production. Marketing-generated leads feed into sales pipeline. Sales feedback on lead quality feeds back into marketing optimization.

**Mapping**:

- Campaign launch date triggers sales enablement deadline
- Marketing MQLs become sales SQL pipeline input
- Sales win/loss reasons feed into marketing messaging refinement
- Sales collateral needs become marketing production tasks

## Board Type Selection by Scenario

When users request a marketing board, use this decision tree:

```
What marketing challenge are you solving?

├── "We need to plan a multi-channel campaign"
│   ├── Single campaign, defined timeline → Campaign Board (30-45 min)
│   ├── Product launch with cross-functional teams → GTM Launch Board (60+ min)
│   └── Annual marketing plan → Campaign Board (expanded, 2+ hours)
│
├── "We need to organize our content production"
│   ├── Single month → Content Calendar, simple (20 min)
│   ├── Quarterly editorial plan → Content Calendar, full (45 min)
│   └── Multi-channel content strategy → Content Calendar + Campaign Board (90+ min)
│
├── "We need to understand our conversion funnel"
│   ├── Quick pipeline check → Funnel Board, simple (20 min)
│   ├── Funnel optimization project → Funnel Board, full (45-60 min)
│   └── Full funnel + channel attribution → Funnel Board + Channel Analysis (2+ hours)
│
├── "We are launching a new product"
│   ├── Minor feature launch (Tier 3) → Campaign Board, simple (20 min)
│   ├── Major feature launch (Tier 2) → GTM Launch Board (60 min)
│   └── New product/market launch (Tier 1) → GTM Launch Board, full (2+ hours)
│
└── "We need to document our brand"
    ├── Quick brand reference → Brand Guidelines, simple (30 min)
    ├── Full brand system → Brand Guidelines, comprehensive (2+ hours)
    └── Brand refresh → Brand Guidelines current + Brand Guidelines new (3+ hours)
```
