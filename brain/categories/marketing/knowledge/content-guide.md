# Marketing — Content Guide

## Overview

Marketing board content must be specific, data-informed, audience-aware, and action-oriented. Generic marketing language ("build brand awareness," "engage our audience," "drive growth") is as useless on a marketing board as it is in a marketing plan. God-level marketing boards contain content that specifies who, what, where, when, why, and how much — every card should feel like a brief, not a wish.

---

## Essential Sections by Board Type

### Campaign Planning Board

**Campaign Overview**:

- Campaign name (specific and memorable, not "Q2 Campaign")
- Campaign objective tied to a business outcome (not a vanity metric)
- Primary KPI with target value
- Campaign dates (start, launch, end, review)
- Total budget with allocation breakdown
- Example: "Spring Compliance Blitz — Drive 500 MQLs from mid-market SaaS companies ($50-200 employees) through content-led demand gen. Budget: $45K over 8 weeks. Primary KPI: MQLs at <$90 CPL."
- NOT: "Q2 Awareness Campaign — Increase brand awareness" (no target, no audience, no metric)

**Target Audience**:

- Define 1-3 personas with specificity
- Each persona: Role, company size, industry, primary pain point, preferred channels, decision-making involvement
- Example: "VP of Compliance at Series B+ SaaS companies (100-500 employees). Pain: Spending 6+ weeks preparing for SOC2 audits manually. Channels: LinkedIn, industry newsletters, compliance conferences. Decision: Final budget authority for compliance tools under $50K/year."
- NOT: "Decision makers at companies" (could be anyone)

**Channel Plan**:

- Each channel should include: What content/activity, when, budget allocation, expected output
- Example: "Email — 4-part nurture sequence to existing leads (n=3,200). Schedule: Weekly for 4 weeks starting March 15. Budget: $2K (copywriting + tool cost). Expected: 35% open rate, 8% CTR, 80 demo requests."
- NOT: "Email — Send newsletters" (no specifics)

**Messaging Framework**:

- Key message per audience segment
- Value proposition specific to this campaign
- Proof points and evidence
- Example: "Message: 'Cut SOC2 audit prep from 6 weeks to 3 days.' Proof: Case study — TechCorp reduced audit prep by 87% in their first quarter using ComplianceAI. Supporting stat: Average mid-market company spends $50K+ per audit cycle on manual compliance work."

**Timeline & Milestones**:

- Pre-launch activities with dates and owners
- Launch day activities with hour-by-hour schedule (for major launches)
- Post-launch activities with measurement dates
- Key decision points: "Week 2 checkpoint — if CPL exceeds $120, shift 30% of paid budget to content syndication"

### Content Calendar

**Content Pieces**:

- Each content card should include: Title, content type, target persona, funnel stage, channel, publish date, author, status
- Example: "Title: '5 Signs Your SOC2 Audit Process Is Costing You Too Much.' Type: Blog post (1,500 words). Persona: VP Compliance. Funnel: TOFU (Awareness). Channel: Blog + LinkedIn + Email newsletter. Publish: March 12. Author: Sarah K. Status: In Review."
- NOT: "Blog post about compliance" (no specifics)

**Monthly Theme**:

- Each month should have a cohesive content theme that ties all content together
- Example: "March Theme: 'Compliance Automation Month' — All content focuses on the transition from manual to automated compliance processes. Aligns with EU DSA awareness and Q1 audit season."

**Content Mix Targets**:

- Specify the ideal distribution across content types and funnel stages
- Example: "Target mix: Blog (4/month), Case Study (1/month), Webinar (1/month), Social posts (12/month), Newsletter (4/month). Funnel distribution: TOFU 40%, MOFU 35%, BOFU 25%."

**SEO Integration**:

- For content-marketing-heavy boards, include keyword targets per content piece
- Example: "Blog target keyword: 'SOC2 audit automation' — Monthly search volume: 2,400, Keyword difficulty: 42, Current rank: #14, Target: Top 5 by Q3."

### Funnel Board

**Funnel Stage Definitions**:

- Each stage should be precisely defined — what qualifies a prospect to be in this stage?
- Example:
  - AWARENESS: "Visited website or engaged with social content. Known by cookie/pixel, not by name."
  - INTEREST: "Provided email address through content download, newsletter signup, or webinar registration."
  - CONSIDERATION (MQL): "Scored 50+ points in lead scoring model. Visited pricing page, requested demo, or engaged with 3+ content pieces in 14 days."
  - DECISION (SQL): "Accepted by sales team after MQL handoff. Active conversation with sales rep."
  - PURCHASE: "Signed contract or completed self-serve purchase."

**Stage Metrics**:

- Each stage: Volume, conversion rate to next stage, velocity (days in stage), cost per unit
- Example: "AWARENESS: 125,000 monthly visitors. Conv to INTEREST: 4.2% (5,250 leads/month). Avg time in stage: 14 days. Cost per visitor: $0.38."
- Include benchmarks: "Industry benchmark conversion AWARENESS to INTEREST: 2-5%. Our 4.2% is at median."
- Include trend: "Trend: Up from 3.1% in Q4 2025. Improvement driven by new SEO content strategy."

**Drop-off Analysis**:

- Where do prospects leave the funnel, and why?
- Example: "Biggest drop-off: INTEREST to CONSIDERATION (82% drop). Root cause analysis: 60% of leads are content downloaders who never return to the site. Hypothesis: Post-download nurture sequence is too generic — needs persona-specific follow-up."

**Optimization Tactics**:

- Specific improvements planned for each funnel stage
- Example: "INTEREST to CONSIDERATION optimization: A/B test persona-specific nurture sequences (test group n=500 per variant). Expected improvement: +3% conversion rate (from 18% to 21%). Test period: March 15 - April 15."

### Go-to-Market Board

**Positioning Statement**:

- Clear articulation of the product's market position
- Format: "For [target customer] who [need/pain], [product] is a [category] that [key benefit]. Unlike [competitor/alternative], our product [primary differentiation]."
- Example: "For VPs of Compliance at mid-market SaaS companies who spend 6+ weeks on each SOC2 audit, ComplianceAI Mobile is a compliance automation platform that reduces audit prep to 3 days. Unlike Vanta and Drata, our product uses proprietary NLP to automatically map controls to evidence from existing documents."

**Launch Tier Classification**:

- Tier 1 (Major): Full GTM — press release, launch event, all channels active, sales enablement, customer communications
- Tier 2 (Medium): Blog post, email announcement, social campaign, sales brief
- Tier 3 (Minor): In-product announcement, changelog update, email to affected users
- Specify which tier this launch falls into and why

**Sales Enablement Content**:

- What does the sales team need to sell this product?
- Example: "Deliverables: (1) 2-page product sheet with pricing, (2) 5-slide demo deck, (3) Competitive battle card vs. Vanta and Drata, (4) FAQ document with 20 common objections + responses, (5) Customer case study — TechCorp's compliance transformation. All materials due 2 weeks before launch."

**Success Metrics**:

- Primary: The one metric that defines launch success
- Secondary: Supporting metrics that indicate health
- Example: "Primary: 5,000 app downloads in 30 days. Secondary: 60+ NPS from beta users, $50K pipeline generated from mobile-specific campaigns, 70% day-7 retention rate."

### Brand Guidelines Board

**Logo Usage Rules**:

- Minimum size: "Logo must be at least 32px height on screen, 0.5 inches in print"
- Clear space: "Maintain clear space equal to the height of the logo's icon on all sides"
- Approved versions: Primary (dark background), Primary (light background), Monochrome, Icon only
- Prohibited usage: "Never stretch, rotate, add effects, change colors, or place on busy backgrounds"

**Color Specifications**:

- Each brand color with: Name, Hex, RGB, CMYK, Pantone, usage percentage, usage context
- Example: "Primary Purple (#4A148C): Use for headlines, primary CTAs, and brand marks. Usage: 60% of brand color presence. RGB: 74, 20, 140. CMYK: 85, 100, 0, 10. Pantone: 2685 C."

**Voice & Tone Guidelines**:

- Brand personality attributes (3-5 adjectives with definitions)
- Dos and Don'ts with examples
- Tone spectrum showing how voice adapts across contexts
- Example: "We are: Confident (not arrogant), Clear (not simplistic), Helpful (not condescending). We say: 'Here is how to fix this' (confident + helpful). We never say: 'Obviously, you should...' (arrogant + condescending)."

## Writing Guidelines for Marketing Board Content

### The Campaign Brief Test

Every campaign card should pass this test: If you handed this card to a freelancer with no other context, could they execute the work?

- Weak: "Write a blog post about compliance" (freelancer has no idea what angle, audience, length, or tone)
- Strong: "Write a 1,500-word blog post titled '5 Signs Your SOC2 Audit Process Is Costing You Too Much.' Target audience: VP Compliance at mid-market SaaS. Tone: Authoritative but accessible. Include: 3 data points on audit costs, 2 customer quotes, CTA to request a demo. SEO keyword: 'SOC2 audit automation.' Due: March 10."

### The Metric Triad

Every metric on the board should include three elements:

1. **Value**: The actual number (e.g., "4.2%")
2. **Context**: A benchmark or comparison (e.g., "vs. industry avg 2.1%")
3. **Direction**: A trend indicator (e.g., "up from 3.1% last quarter")

A number without context is noise. "4.2% conversion rate" means nothing until you know the industry average, the historical trend, and the target.

### The Audience Specificity Rule

Marketing content is always for someone. Every board element should make the audience visible:

- NOT: "Target: Businesses" (everyone targets businesses)
- NOT: "Target: Enterprise companies" (still too broad)
- YES: "Target: VPs of Compliance at Series B+ SaaS companies, 100-500 employees, currently doing manual SOC2 compliance"

### The Budget Accountability Rule

Every campaign, channel, or activity should show its financial footprint:

- Budget allocated
- Expected return (leads, MQLs, pipeline, revenue)
- Implied ROI or cost-per-outcome
- Example: "LinkedIn Ads: $15K budget → Expected: 300 MQLs → CPL: $50 → If 10% convert to SQL: 30 SQLs at $500 CPA → If 25% close: 7.5 customers at $2K CPA → LTV of $8,400/customer = 4.2x ROI"

## Content Volume Guidelines

| Board Type       | Items per Section | Total Items     | Max Depth                      |
| ---------------- | ----------------- | --------------- | ------------------------------ |
| Campaign Board   | 3-8 per channel   | 20-40 total     | 2 levels (campaign + activity) |
| Content Calendar | 1-3 per day       | 30-60 per month | 1 level (content piece)        |
| Funnel Board     | 3-5 per stage     | 15-25 total     | 2 levels (stage + tactic)      |
| GTM Launch Board | 5-10 per phase    | 20-40 total     | 2 levels (phase + activity)    |
| Brand Guidelines | 3-8 per section   | 25-50 total     | 1 level (guideline items)      |

### Warning Signs

**Too much content**: The board looks like a to-do list rather than a marketing plan. Individual activities are lost in a sea of cards. The viewer cannot identify the strategic priorities.

**Too little content**: Channels have 1-2 activities. The campaign feels thin and under-resourced. Metrics sections are empty or contain only targets without actual data.

**Wrong content**: Activities without audience context. Metrics without benchmarks. Campaigns without budget information. Content pieces without funnel stage assignments. Channels operating in isolation without cross-channel connections.

## Questions the Board Should Answer

### For the CMO / VP Marketing

1. What are our top 3 campaign priorities this quarter? (30 seconds)
2. How is each channel performing against targets? (1 minute)
3. Where in the funnel are we losing the most prospects? (1 minute)
4. What is our marketing ROI and how is it trending? (2 minutes)
5. Are we spending our budget in the right places? (2 minutes)

### For the Marketing Team

1. What content do I need to produce this week? (30 seconds)
2. What campaign is launching next and what is my role? (1 minute)
3. How did my last campaign/content piece perform? (1 minute)
4. What audience are we targeting and what do they care about? (2 minutes)

### For the CEO / Executive Team

1. Is marketing generating enough pipeline to hit revenue targets? (30 seconds)
2. What is the cost to acquire a customer through marketing? (30 seconds)
3. How does our marketing compare to competitors? (2 minutes)
4. What marketing investments are planned and what is the expected return? (3 minutes)

### For the Sales Team

1. What campaigns are running that will generate leads for me? (30 seconds)
2. What content can I use in my sales conversations? (1 minute)
3. What is the quality of marketing-generated leads? (1 minute)
4. When is the next product launch and what materials will be available? (1 minute)
