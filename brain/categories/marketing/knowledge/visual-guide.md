# Marketing — Visual Guide

## Overview

Marketing boards must project energy, clarity, and brand sophistication. Unlike strategy boards that convey authority, or project management boards that convey order, marketing boards convey momentum — the feeling that a well-oiled marketing machine is moving forward with purpose. Every visual choice should balance professionalism with the creative energy that defines great marketing.

## Typography for Marketing Boards

Marketing boards require typography that balances authority with approachability — more energetic than strategy boards, more structured than mind maps.

| Element             | Font Size | Weight          | Usage                                  |
| ------------------- | --------- | --------------- | -------------------------------------- |
| Board Title         | 36-48px   | Bold (700)      | Board name, campaign name              |
| Campaign Subtitle   | 18-24px   | Regular (400)   | Campaign dates, target audience        |
| Section Header      | 22-28px   | Bold (700)      | Channel names, funnel stages           |
| Section Description | 14-18px   | Medium (500)    | Section context                        |
| Campaign Card Title | 16-20px   | Semi-Bold (600) | Individual campaign or content title   |
| Card Description    | 13-16px   | Regular (400)   | Activity details, channel info         |
| Metric Value        | 24-36px   | Bold (700)      | Key numbers (conversion rate, traffic) |
| Metric Label        | 12-14px   | Regular (400)   | What the metric measures               |
| Metric Context      | 11-13px   | Regular (400)   | Benchmark, trend, comparison           |
| Date/Deadline       | 12-14px   | Medium (500)    | Publication dates, deadlines           |
| Status Label        | 11-13px   | Bold (700)      | LIVE, PLANNED, IN REVIEW               |
| CTA / Action        | 14-18px   | Bold (700)      | Next steps, action items               |

### Typography Rules

- Campaign names should be visually prominent — they are the primary navigation element
- Metric values should be the largest text in their section — they are the board's key output
- Metric labels should be smaller and lighter — they provide context, not emphasis
- Status labels should be ALL CAPS for instant recognition
- Dates should use a consistent format throughout: "Mar 6, 2026" or "2026-03-06"
- Avoid italic text except in testimonials or quotes

## Element Selection Guide

### Campaign Cards

Campaign activity cards are the primary building block of marketing boards:

- **Standard card**: 220x160px, white background, colored left border (4px) indicating channel
- **Priority card**: 240x180px, white background with subtle accent-colored shadow, indicating a high-priority campaign
- **Live campaign card**: Standard size, green left border (4px) + small "LIVE" badge in top-right corner
- **Draft/Planned card**: Standard size, gray left border, reduced opacity (85%)

Card content layout:

```
┌─────────────────────────┐
│ ▌ Campaign Title        │  ← Left border color = channel
│   Subtitle / audience   │
│                         │
│   📧 Email + 📱 Social │  ← Channel icons
│   Mar 15 - Apr 12       │  ← Date range
│                         │
│   ● LIVE    Budget: $5K │  ← Status + Budget
└─────────────────────────┘
```

### Metric Widgets

Marketing boards need prominent metric displays:

- **Big Number**: Large metric value (36px, Bold) with small label (12px) below. Example: "4.2%" with "Conversion Rate" beneath.
- **Trend Metric**: Big number + small arrow (up = green, down = red) + percentage change. Example: "4.2% ↑ +0.8% vs last month"
- **Gauge**: Circular or semi-circular progress indicator showing actual vs. target. Fill color changes from red to yellow to green as the metric approaches target.
- **Sparkline**: Miniature trend line (last 30 days) embedded within a metric card. Shows direction without needing full chart detail.
- **Comparison**: Two numbers side by side — "Actual: 4.2%" vs. "Target: 5.0%" with a visual gap indicator.

### Funnel Shapes

For funnel visualizations:

- **Trapezoid stages**: Each funnel stage is a trapezoid with the wider end at top. Stage name and metric displayed within.
- **Stage colors**: Progress from light to dark (top to bottom) or follow a warm-cool gradient
- **Conversion arrows**: Downward-pointing arrows between stages with conversion rate displayed on the arrow
- **Drop-off indicators**: Optional side arrows showing where prospects exit the funnel, with the drop-off volume displayed

### Timeline Elements

For campaign timelines and content calendars:

- **Phase markers**: Vertical dashed lines separating phases (Pre-Launch, Launch, Post-Launch)
- **Milestone diamonds**: Diamond shapes at key dates (launch day, review date, budget checkpoint)
- **Activity bars**: Horizontal bars showing duration of multi-day activities, color-coded by channel
- **Today marker**: A bold vertical line in accent color showing the current date, creating an instant before/after division

## Decorative Elements

### The Campaign Hero Zone

A large visual area at the top of campaign boards showing the campaign's hero creative — the key visual that anchors the campaign. This could be a mockup of the landing page, a social media post design, or the key visual asset. The hero zone connects the marketing plan to the marketing output.

- Size: 600-800px wide, 300-400px tall
- Position: Centered in the header zone or left-aligned with the strategy column
- Treatment: Subtle shadow (4px, 10% opacity) to lift it from the board background

### Channel Activity Heat Map

A small grid visualization showing channel activity intensity across the campaign timeline. Days or weeks on the X-axis, channels on the Y-axis, with cell color intensity indicating activity volume. This provides a birds-eye view of marketing effort distribution.

### The Metric Scorecard Bar

A horizontal bar spanning the full board width, positioned between the plan sections and the detail sections. Contains 4-6 key metrics in a dashboard format — large numbers with small labels. This bar provides an at-a-glance performance summary without requiring the viewer to read through individual cards.

### Persona Avatar Cards

Visual representations of target personas placed in the audience section. Each card includes a placeholder avatar, persona name, key demographic info, primary pain point, and preferred channels. These humanize the marketing plan by making the target audience visible.

### Budget Allocation Pie/Bar

A visual representation of budget distribution across channels or campaigns. Can be a pie chart, horizontal stacked bar, or treemap. Color-coded to match channel colors. Always includes both percentage and absolute dollar amounts.

## Accessibility and Visual Quality

### Critical Requirements

- Campaign status must be distinguishable by non-color means (text labels "LIVE" / "PLANNED" alongside color dots)
- Funnel stage labels must include text identifiers, not just position-based meaning
- Metrics must include labels, not just numbers — "4.2%" alone is meaningless without "Conversion Rate"
- Channel indicators should use both color AND icons — do not rely on color alone
- The board's overall structure should be understandable at 50% zoom

### Quality Checklist

- [ ] Campaign cards are consistently styled with channel color coding
- [ ] All metrics include value, label, and context (benchmark or trend)
- [ ] Funnel stages are labeled and show conversion rates between stages
- [ ] Timeline includes date markers and today-line
- [ ] Channel colors are consistent across all board sections
- [ ] Status indicators use both color and text labels
- [ ] Budget information is visible and connected to activities
- [ ] The board tells a coherent marketing story from strategy to metrics
- [ ] Persona/audience information is present and specific
- [ ] The board has been reviewed by someone outside the marketing team
