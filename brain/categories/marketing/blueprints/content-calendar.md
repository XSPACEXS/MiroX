# Content Calendar Board

## Overview

- **Purpose**: Plan, schedule, and track content production across channels on a weekly and monthly basis. Provides a visual grid of what content is being published, where, when, and by whom, along with status tracking from ideation through publication.
- **Best For**: Content marketing teams, social media managers, editorial leads, and agencies managing multi-channel content pipelines.
- **Complexity**: Medium
- **Board Size**: 5600x3000px

## Board Layout

The board is organized as a monthly calendar grid (5 columns for weeks, rows for channels) with supporting frames for content pipeline and performance tracking on the right side.

## Example Content

Every cell in the calendar contains a specific, realistic content piece with title, author, channel, and status. The example follows a SaaS product launch content strategy for "AutoPilot 3.0" across blog, social media (LinkedIn, Twitter/X, Instagram), email, and video channels. Week 2 is highlighted as Launch Week with elevated output. The content pipeline tracks 12 pieces across their production lifecycle. Performance targets are based on realistic SaaS marketing benchmarks.

## Variations

1. **Quarterly View**: Compress weeks into month columns (3 months visible). Show only content titles without full details. Good for executive-level content planning.
2. **Social-Only Calendar**: Remove blog, email, and video rows. Expand social into platform-specific rows (LinkedIn, Twitter/X, Instagram, TikTok). Add posting time slots.
3. **Agency Multi-Client**: Duplicate the calendar grid per client, each with its own color-coded theme bar. Add a shared resource allocation row showing writer/designer capacity.

## Build Instructions

1. Create board at 5600x3000px with background #FAFAFA.
2. Place Frame 1 (Theme Overview) at (50, 50), size 5400x400, background #3F51B5.
3. Place Week 1-5 frames in a row starting at (50, 500), each 1000x1600, spaced 50px apart.
4. Place Content Pipeline frame at (5150, 500), size 1200x1200.
5. Place Performance Tracker frame at (5150, 1750), size 1200x1000.
6. Add left-border color accents to each week frame.
7. Within each week frame, create section headers for each channel (Blog, Social, Email, Video).
8. Add sticky notes under each section header with the content details.
9. Color-code sticky notes by channel (indigo=blog, teal=social, amber=email, red=video).
10. Build the pipeline table and performance table in their respective frames.
11. Add connectors between frames.
12. Add the status legend color dots in the Pipeline frame header.

## Tips & Best Practices

- Fill out the calendar 2-3 weeks ahead minimum — never plan content the same week it needs to publish.
- Color-code by content pillar (not just channel) to ensure you are balancing educational, promotional, and community content.
- Keep a running "Content Ideas" sticky note cluster off to the side of the board for ideas that do not have a date yet.
- After each month, screenshot the Performance Tracker and attach it to the board as an archive — this builds a longitudinal view of content effectiveness.
- Use the Pipeline frame as your daily stand-up view — writers and designers check here first thing each morning.
