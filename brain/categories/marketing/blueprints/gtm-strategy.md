# Go-to-Market Strategy Board

## Overview

- **Purpose**: Plan and execute a comprehensive go-to-market strategy for a new product, feature, or market entry. Covers market analysis, competitive positioning, messaging framework, launch phases, channel strategy, and success metrics in a single visual workspace.
- **Best For**: Product marketing managers, startup founders, growth teams, and cross-functional launch squads preparing to bring something new to market.
- **Complexity**: Advanced
- **Board Size**: 5200x3600px

## Example Content

The entire board is pre-filled with a realistic GTM strategy for "AutoPilot 3.0," a fictional AI-powered project management SaaS product. Market sizing uses plausible industry figures. Competitor analysis references real product categories with realistic feature comparisons. Pricing tiers follow standard SaaS conventions. The messaging framework provides ready-to-use copy for different buyer personas. Launch phases use specific dates anchored to an April 8, 2026 launch.

## Variations

1. **Market Entry (New Geography)**: Replace Market Analysis with market-specific regulatory and cultural considerations. Add a Localization frame for language, pricing currency, and local partnerships.
2. **Feature Launch (Existing Product)**: Simplify by removing Pricing Strategy and Sales Enablement. Focus on in-app announcement, email to existing users, and feature adoption metrics.
3. **Partner/Channel GTM**: Add a Partner Tier frame with partner types, incentives, co-marketing agreements, and partner enablement materials. Modify Channel Strategy to emphasize indirect distribution.

9. Draw connectors between frames with arrowheads and labels.
10. Group elements within each frame.

## Tips & Best Practices

- Start with Frames 1-3 (analysis and positioning) in a working session before filling in execution frames — strategy should drive tactics, not the other way around.
- The Messaging Framework (Frame 4) is the most-referenced artifact after launch — keep it updated as you learn what resonates.
- Use the GTM Scorecard as your weekly leadership update — color-code cells green/yellow/red based on progress toward targets.
- Review the competitive landscape quarterly — new entrants and feature releases from competitors can shift your positioning.
- Share the Sales Enablement frame directly with your sales team as their single source of truth for launch materials.
