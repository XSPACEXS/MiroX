# Campaign Planning Board

## Overview

- **Purpose**: Orchestrate a full marketing campaign from initial brief through execution and measurement, keeping all stakeholders aligned on objectives, audience, channels, timeline, budget, and creative assets.
- **Best For**: Marketing managers, campaign leads, and cross-functional teams launching multi-channel campaigns (product launches, seasonal promotions, awareness drives, event marketing).
- **Complexity**: Advanced
- **Board Size**: 4800x3200px

## Variations

1. **Event Marketing Campaign**: Replace Channel Strategy frame with an event-specific logistics frame (venue, speakers, agenda, registration). Replace Creative Briefs with event collateral (booth design, swag, presentation decks).
2. **Seasonal Promotion (B2C)**: Simplify personas to demographic segments. Replace enterprise-focused channels with consumer channels (Instagram, TikTok, influencer partnerships). Add a Promotions & Discounts frame with offer mechanics.
3. **Awareness-Only Campaign**: Remove KPI rows related to conversion. Add brand lift measurement (aided/unaided recall, sentiment tracking). Focus Creative Briefs on video and social content rather than direct-response ads.

## Tips & Best Practices

- Update the KPI tracking table weekly during the campaign — this board should be a living document, not a static plan.
- Use the "Contingency" budget line to absorb unexpected opportunities (e.g., a viral moment you want to amplify with paid spend).
- Keep creative briefs on the same board so designers and writers never have to search for the brief in email threads.
- After the campaign, duplicate this board and use it as a retrospective template — add a "Learnings" frame with what worked and what to change next time.
- If the board feels crowded, collapse completed phases in the timeline to keep focus on the current phase.
