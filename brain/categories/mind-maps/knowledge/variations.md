# Mind Maps — Variations

## Overview

A single mind map pattern can serve vastly different contexts depending on how it is adapted. This document covers how to modify mind map boards for different industries, team sizes, complexity levels, cultural contexts, and integrations with other board types.

---

## Industry Adaptations

### SaaS / Software

**Branch structure**: Product (Features, Technical Debt, Platform), Growth (Acquisition, Activation, Retention, Revenue), Engineering (Architecture, Infrastructure, DevOps), Customer (Support, Success, Feedback), Market (Competition, Positioning, Pricing)

**Content characteristics**:

- Heavily metric-driven: MRR, ARR, CAC, LTV, churn rate, NPS on every branch
- Technical vocabulary: API, SDK, microservices, CI/CD, latency, uptime
- Sprint-aligned: Ideas are tagged with sprint numbers or quarters
- Integration-centric: Every feature idea references which integrations it enables

**Visual treatment**:

- Use the Neon Clarity palette (dark mode) for tech audiences
- Include small architecture diagrams as embedded images within technical branches
- Use code-style monospace font for technical specifications
- Badge system: "API Ready," "Needs Backend," "Frontend Only," "Full Stack"

**Unique elements**:

- Tech debt heat map: A color gradient overlay on the Engineering branch showing debt severity
- User persona icons attached to relevant branches
- API endpoint references linked to feature ideas

### E-Commerce / Retail

**Branch structure**: Product Catalog (Categories, SKUs, Pricing), Customer Journey (Discovery, Consideration, Purchase, Post-Purchase), Operations (Inventory, Fulfillment, Returns), Marketing (Channels, Campaigns, Seasonal), Analytics (Conversion, AOV, Cart Abandonment, Lifetime Value)

**Content characteristics**:

- Seasonal awareness: All ideas tagged with relevant season (Holiday, Summer, Back-to-School)
- Revenue attribution: Each idea has an estimated revenue impact
- Visual-heavy: Product images embedded alongside text where possible
- Competitive pricing data included alongside product decisions

**Visual treatment**:

- Use the Sunset Warmth palette — warm colors reflect consumer energy
- Include product photography thumbnails within relevant sticky notes
- Seasonal color overlays: Red/green for holidays, pastels for spring
- Shopping cart and dollar sign icons liberally used

**Unique elements**:

- Funnel overlay showing conversion rates at each customer journey stage
- Inventory status badges (In Stock, Low Stock, Out of Stock) next to product ideas
- Price comparison tables embedded within the competitive analysis branch

### Healthcare / Biotech

**Branch structure**: Patient (Experience, Outcomes, Safety), Clinical (Workflows, Protocols, Evidence), Regulatory (Compliance, Approvals, Documentation), Technology (EHR, Telemedicine, Devices), Research (Studies, Publications, Trials)

**Content characteristics**:

- Evidence-graded: Every claim has a citation or evidence level (RCT, Observational, Expert Opinion)
- Regulatory awareness: HIPAA, FDA, GDPR considerations flagged on relevant items
- Patient-centric language: Outcomes described in patient-relevant terms
- Cautious phrasing: "May improve," "Evidence suggests," "Pending validation"

**Visual treatment**:

- Use the Earth Tones palette — conveys trust, calm, and seriousness
- Cross-shaped or shield-shaped icons for safety-related items
- Red flags (literally red triangles) on items with regulatory risk
- Green checkmarks on evidence-backed items

**Unique elements**:

- Evidence strength indicators on every item (Level I through Level V)
- Patient safety callout boxes with prominent borders
- Regulatory pathway annotations showing approval requirements

### Fintech / Financial Services

**Branch structure**: Products (Payments, Lending, Insurance, Wealth), Risk (Credit, Market, Operational, Compliance), Technology (Core Banking, APIs, Security), Growth (Customer Acquisition, Cross-sell, Partnerships), Regulatory (Licensing, Reporting, Capital Requirements)

**Content characteristics**:

- Quantitative everywhere: Basis points, percentages, dollar amounts, ratios
- Risk-aware: Every opportunity has an associated risk assessment
- Regulatory citations: "Per SEC Rule 15c3-1" or "Basel III compliant"
- Time-sensitive: Many items have regulatory deadlines

**Visual treatment**:

- Use the Ocean Depths palette — conveys stability and trustworthiness
- Financial data tables embedded within branches
- Green/red for positive/negative metrics (industry convention)
- Lock icons on security-related items

**Unique elements**:

- Risk heat map overlay on the entire board (green/yellow/red zones)
- Regulatory deadline timeline embedded as a horizontal bar across the bottom
- Capital requirement calculations next to product launch ideas

### Education

**Branch structure**: Curriculum (Subjects, Learning Objectives, Assessment), Students (Demographics, Needs, Engagement), Teaching (Methods, Technology, Professional Development), Operations (Scheduling, Facilities, Budget), Community (Parents, Partners, Outreach)

**Content characteristics**:

- Learning-outcome focused: Every idea ties to a measurable learning objective
- Inclusive language: Accessibility and equity considerations embedded throughout
- Research-backed: Pedagogical research citations where relevant
- Student-voice included: Direct quotes from student feedback

**Visual treatment**:

- Use the Earth Tones palette — approachable and non-threatening
- Playful icons (lightbulbs, books, stars) but not childish
- Color-coding by grade level or subject area
- Progress indicators showing curriculum completion

**Unique elements**:

- Bloom's Taxonomy level badges on learning activities
- Student engagement score indicators
- Cross-curricular connection lines between subject branches

### Agency / Creative Services

**Branch structure**: Creative (Concepts, Visual Direction, Copywriting), Strategy (Client Goals, Audience, Positioning), Production (Timeline, Resources, Deliverables), Client Management (Feedback, Approvals, Communication), Growth (New Business, Portfolio, Industry Recognition)

**Content characteristics**:

- Visual inspiration: Mood board thumbnails embedded in creative branches
- Client-specific vocabulary: Uses the client's brand language
- Deliverable-focused: Every idea maps to a specific output
- Timeline-aware: Production deadlines throughout

**Visual treatment**:

- Adapt palette to match client's brand colors (this is a unique chameleon quality of agency boards)
- Bold, creative typography — can push boundaries more than corporate boards
- Image-rich branches with visual references
- Inspiration tags: "Reference: Nike Just Do It campaign" or "Style: Bauhaus geometric"

**Unique elements**:

- Client feedback zones: Frames where client comments are captured
- Revision tracking: Version badges (V1, V2, V3) on evolved concepts
- Inspiration image collages within creative branches

## Team Size Adaptations

### Solo (1 person)

**Characteristics**:

- Personal thinking tool — no need for extensive legend or instructions
- Can be messy and organic — only the creator needs to understand it
- Typically faster to create, less polish needed
- Often used for personal planning, study, or creative exploration

**Adaptations**:

- Skip the legend zone (you know your own color coding)
- Skip the meta zone (you know when you made it)
- Reduce board size by 30% (less content expected)
- Use more free-form placement (less strict alignment)
- Add personal annotations that wouldn't make sense to others
- Focus on content depth over visual polish

### Small Team (3-5 people)

**Characteristics**:

- Collaborative creation — everyone adds ideas
- Needs basic visual coding so everyone can read it
- Voting and prioritization become important
- Discussion happens around specific items

**Adaptations**:

- Include a legend zone (necessary for shared understanding)
- Use consistent color coding for branches
- Add contributor indicators (initials or avatars on sticky notes)
- Include voting mechanism (dot voting or emoji reactions)
- Add a "Parking Lot" zone for off-topic ideas
- Maintain moderate visual polish (clean enough to screenshot and share)

### Large Team (10+ people)

**Characteristics**:

- Requires strong facilitation structure
- Can become chaotic quickly without clear zones
- Needs role-based color coding (not just branch-based)
- Information density is high — progressive disclosure becomes critical

**Adaptations**:

- Add facilitator instructions frame (how to navigate, how to add ideas)
- Use branch owners (each branch has a named responsible person)
- Add real-time status updates (active discussion zone highlighted)
- Include a team roster with role assignments
- Create a structured input process: each person gets a specific color for their stickies
- Add "round" markers showing which brainstorm round produced which ideas
- Increase board size by 50% to accommodate more content
- Use frames aggressively to contain sections and prevent drift

### Organization-Wide (50+ stakeholders)

**Characteristics**:

- More viewers than contributors
- Needs to tell a story to executives who will spend <2 minutes on it
- Must work as a standalone artifact (no presenter needed to explain it)
- Version control and update tracking become essential

**Adaptations**:

- Include an executive summary frame (top-center, highly visible)
- Add a "How to Read This Board" guide frame
- Use department-coded color overlays
- Include a changelog: "Last updated March 6, 2026 — Added Q2 priorities"
- Create a navigation map (minimap) for orientation
- Maximize visual polish — this is a communication artifact, not a working tool
- Add a "Contact" frame with the board owner's information

## Complexity Levels

### Quick / Simple (15 minutes)

**Specifications**:

- Board size: 3000x3000px
- Branches: 3-4
- Items per branch: 3-5
- Total items: 12-20
- Depth: 2 levels maximum
- Cross-connections: 0-2
- Zones: Content only (no legend, no actions)

**When to use**: Quick capture during a meeting, personal brainstorm, simple topic overview

**Design shortcuts**:

- Use Miro's default sticky note colors (one per branch)
- Skip custom shapes — use stickies for everything including branch headings
- Use straight connectors (skip curved routing)
- No decorative elements
- No typography hierarchy beyond "bigger = more important"

### Standard (30 minutes)

**Specifications**:

- Board size: 5000x5000px
- Branches: 5-6
- Items per branch: 5-10
- Total items: 25-60
- Depth: 3 levels
- Cross-connections: 3-5
- Zones: Content + Legend + Action Items

**When to use**: Team brainstorm, project planning, research synthesis, standard workshops

**Design approach**:

- Custom color palette (chosen, not default)
- Shaped branch headings (rounded rectangles)
- Curved connectors with weight hierarchy
- Basic typography scale (3 sizes)
- Legend zone with color key
- Action items zone with owners and dates

### Advanced (1+ hours)

**Specifications**:

- Board size: 7000-10000px per side
- Branches: 6-8
- Items per branch: 10-20
- Total items: 60-120+
- Depth: 3-4 levels
- Cross-connections: 5-10
- Zones: All zones populated (Content, Legend, Actions, Meta, Navigation)

**When to use**: Strategic planning, comprehensive research synthesis, client deliverables, organizational alignment

**Design approach**:

- Fully custom palette with documented rationale
- All elements follow strict visual hierarchy
- Decorative polish: badges, dividers, pull quotes, subtle backgrounds
- Inline data visualizations (mini charts, comparison tables)
- Evidence strength indicators
- Comprehensive legend with shape, color, and icon keys
- Navigation minimap
- Executive summary zone
- Export-ready (looks stunning as a high-res PDF or image)

## Cultural Considerations

### Right-to-Left Languages (Arabic, Hebrew)

- Mirror the Hemispheric pattern: central node on the right, branches extend leftward
- Right-align text within sticky notes and text blocks
- Ensure all reading flow guides work right-to-left
- Branch numbering flows right-to-left

### East Asian Contexts (Chinese, Japanese, Korean)

- Vertical text is common and can be used within branch headings for aesthetic effect
- Color symbolism differs: red signifies luck/prosperity (not danger), white signifies mourning (not purity)
- Higher information density is culturally comfortable — boards can include more items without feeling overwhelming
- Hierarchical respect: use formal titles and role indicators

### European Contexts

- Left-to-right reading pattern standard
- Formal/informal spectrum varies by country: German boards tend to be more structured, Italian boards more expressive
- Multi-language support: include English translations alongside local language where international teams are involved
- GDPR awareness: redact personal data in shared boards

### Global / Multicultural Teams

- Use icons and emojis with universal meanings (avoid culturally specific gestures)
- Include a glossary zone for domain-specific or culturally loaded terms
- Avoid idioms in sticky note text ("hit it out of the park" doesn't translate)
- Test color choices against multiple cultural color associations
- Provide multiple entry points (not everyone starts reading from the same place)

## Integration with Other Board Types

### Mind Map to Kanban Board

**Transition point**: When brainstorm ideas become actionable tasks
**How to connect**: Export top-voted mind map items as Kanban cards. Each mind map branch becomes a swim lane or category tag. Cross-connect the boards with hyperlinks: the Kanban card links back to the original mind map sticky for context.

**Mapping**:

- Mind map branch heading becomes Kanban category/tag
- Mind map sub-item becomes Kanban card title
- Mind map priority badge becomes Kanban card priority
- Mind map owner annotation becomes Kanban card assignee

### Mind Map to Roadmap

**Transition point**: When brainstorm themes become quarterly initiatives
**How to connect**: Group mind map branches into time horizons (Now/Next/Later). Each group maps to a roadmap quarter. The mind map serves as the "why" behind the roadmap's "what."

**Mapping**:

- Mind map branch becomes roadmap swim lane
- Top-voted items become roadmap milestones
- Cross-connections inform dependency mapping on the roadmap

### Mind Map to SWOT Analysis

**Transition point**: When a brainstorm needs to be evaluated strategically
**How to connect**: Each mind map item is classified into one of four SWOT quadrants. The mind map provides the raw material; the SWOT provides the strategic framing.

**Mapping**:

- Positive internal items become Strengths
- Negative internal items become Weaknesses
- Positive external items become Opportunities
- Negative external items become Threats

### Mind Map to Business Model Canvas

**Transition point**: When idea exploration needs to be structured into a business model
**How to connect**: Mind map branches about customer needs map to Customer Segments and Value Propositions. Revenue ideas map to Revenue Streams. Partnership ideas map to Key Partners.

### Mind Map to Sprint Planning

**Transition point**: When prioritized ideas become sprint backlog items
**How to connect**: The mind map's action items zone feeds directly into the sprint planning board's backlog. Each item carries over its priority, owner, and branch context. Story point estimation happens on the sprint board, not the mind map.

### Mind Map to Content Calendar

**Transition point**: When content brainstorming needs to be scheduled
**How to connect**: Each content idea on the mind map becomes a calendar entry. The mind map branch (e.g., "Blog," "Social," "Video") maps to the calendar's channel row. The mind map is the ideation phase; the calendar is the execution phase.
