# Revenue Model Canvas

## Overview

- **Purpose**: Comprehensive revenue model canvas for mapping all revenue streams, designing pricing tiers, calculating unit economics, projecting growth, and running scenario analysis (optimistic, base, pessimistic) to stress-test the business model.
- **Best For**: Founders, CFOs, product managers, investors, and strategy teams designing or refining how a business generates and grows revenue.
- **Complexity**: Advanced
- **Board Size**: 5200 x 3600px

## Example Content

All example content is embedded in the Frames & Sections above. The model represents NovaTech Inc., a mid-stage B2B SaaS company with:

- Four revenue streams: SaaS subscriptions, professional services, marketplace, and API access
- Three pricing tiers: Starter ($49/user/mo), Professional ($129/user/mo), Enterprise (custom)

## Tips & Best Practices

- **Start with actuals**: Always ground projections in real historical data. The model loses credibility if the base year is itself a forecast.
- **Segment everything**: Blended metrics hide problems. Always break down by tier, stream, cohort, and customer segment.
- **Stress-test with scenarios**: Optimistic and pessimistic are not "best case" and "worst case" — they should represent realistic alternative outcomes with assigned probabilities.
- **Update quarterly**: Revenue models should be living documents, recalibrated with each quarter's actuals.
- **Make assumptions explicit**: Every projection should trace back to a stated assumption. This makes the model auditable and debatable.
- **Separate leading and lagging indicators**: Pipeline, trial conversions, and MRR movement are leading; revenue, retention, and LTV are lagging. Track both.
