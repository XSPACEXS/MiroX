# Variations — Finance Boards

## Overview

Finance boards serve audiences from seed-stage startup founders to Fortune 500 CFOs. The core principles remain constant (accuracy, context, narrative, action), but the implementation varies dramatically based on company stage, industry, audience, and complexity level. This guide covers how to adapt finance board templates for different contexts.

---

## Company Stage Variations

### Pre-Seed / Seed Stage

**Budget board adaptations**:

- Replace "Department Breakdown" with "Spending Categories" (Engineering, Marketing, Overhead)
- Budget is quarterly, not annual (too much uncertainty for 12-month planning)
- Headcount section is simple: "Team: 8 people, burn: $120K/month"
- Add "Runway Calculator" prominently: Cash / Monthly Burn = X months
- Include "Milestone Budget" — how much runway is needed to reach each key milestone

**Dashboard adaptations**:

- Focus on 3 metrics: MRR, Burn Rate, Runway
- Replace P&L with a simple "Money In vs. Money Out" view
- Cash position is the headline KPI, not revenue
- Add "Path to Default Alive" chart (when revenue > expenses)

**Revenue model adaptations**:

- Single revenue stream is typical — no need for stream decomposition
- Unit economics may be based on estimates, not actuals (label as "Projected")
- Cohort data may only cover 3-6 months — show what you have
- Scenarios are crucial: "What if growth is 50% slower?"

### Series A-B (Growth Stage)

**Budget board adaptations**:

- Full department breakdown is now relevant (4-6 departments)
- Include "Hiring Plan" as a distinct section — headcount is the largest budget item
- Add "Investor Board Metrics" section showing burn multiple, magic number, Rule of 40
- Budget approval involves board of directors — include board-level summary view

**Dashboard adaptations**:

- Add SaaS metrics: ARR, NDR, Logo Churn, Gross Margin
- Include "Investor Update" format as an alternate view of the same data
- Cash runway shifts from "survival" to "strategic runway" (runway to profitability vs. runway to next round)

**Revenue model adaptations**:

- Multiple revenue streams emerge — product tiers, services, add-ons
- Cohort data becomes meaningful (6-12+ months of cohorts)
- Show "Path to $10M ARR" or relevant milestone
- Include competitive pricing comparison

### Enterprise / Public Company

**Budget board adaptations**:

- Full departmental hierarchy with cost center breakdown
- Variance tracking at monthly and quarterly cadence
- Include FX impact analysis for multinational operations
- Audit trail: every number cites its GL source code
- Board-level governance: approval workflow includes Audit Committee

**Dashboard adaptations**:

- GAAP-compliant reporting structure
- Revenue recognition nuances (ASC 606)
- Segment reporting matching 10-K structure
- Peer benchmark comparison (industry multiples)
- Quarterly guidance tracking (actual vs. guidance to Street)

**Revenue model adaptations**:

- Revenue by segment, geography, product line, customer tier
- Deferred revenue and backlog analysis
- Seasonality patterns (trailing 3-year overlay)
- M&A revenue contribution (organic vs. inorganic growth)

## Industry Variations

### SaaS / Software

**Key metrics to include**:

- ARR / MRR with growth rates
- Net Dollar Retention (NDR)
- Logo churn vs. Revenue churn
- CAC Payback Period
- Gross Margin (target: >70%)
- Rule of 40 (growth rate + profit margin)
- Burn Multiple (net burn / net new ARR)

**Special sections**:

- Cohort retention grid (monthly cohorts with 12-month retention)
- Pricing tier analysis (revenue per tier, conversion between tiers)
- Expansion revenue breakdown (upsell vs. cross-sell vs. seat expansion)

### E-Commerce / Retail

**Key metrics to include**:

- GMV (Gross Merchandise Value) vs. Net Revenue
- AOV (Average Order Value) with trend
- Customer Acquisition Cost by channel
- Repeat purchase rate and frequency
- Inventory turnover
- Fulfillment cost per order
- Return rate and cost of returns

**Special sections**:

- Channel P&L (e-commerce vs. retail vs. wholesale)
- Seasonal revenue overlay (this year vs. last year by month)
- Promotion impact analysis (revenue lift vs. margin compression)

### Professional Services / Consulting

**Key metrics to include**:

- Revenue per employee (utilization)
- Billable utilization rate (target: 70-80%)
- Average bill rate vs. cost rate (margin)
- Pipeline coverage ratio
- Days Sales Outstanding (DSO)
- Project profitability by engagement

**Special sections**:

- Utilization heatmap by team/individual
- Backlog and pipeline by practice area
- Project margin analysis (planned vs. actual)

### Healthcare

**Key metrics to include**:

- Revenue per patient visit
- Payer mix (commercial vs. Medicare vs. Medicaid)
- Days in AR by payer
- Cost per procedure
- Operating margin by department
- Bad debt ratio

**Special sections**:

- Payer mix trend (shift from commercial to government)
- Procedure volume and revenue by specialty
- Capital equipment ROI analysis

## Audience Variations

### For the Board of Directors

**Simplify and contextualize**:

- Maximum 1 page / 1 screen of information
- 4-6 KPIs maximum, all with traffic light status
- Industry benchmarks for every metric
- Focus on strategic questions: "Can we afford the expansion?" not "What is our IT spend?"
- Include "Board Actions Required" section with specific asks
- Provide quarters of data, not months

### For Department Heads

**Customize and empower**:

- Show THEIR department data prominently, with company context
- Include line-item detail they can act on
- Add "Reforecast Request" mechanism — a designated area for them to propose adjustments
- Include headcount detail (filled vs. open positions)
- Show peer department comparison for context (not competition)

### For Investors

**Tell the growth story**:

- Lead with ARR growth, not expenses
- Show unit economics improving over time (CAC declining, LTV growing)
- Include competitive positioning metrics
- Use the SaaS metrics investors know: NDR, Rule of 40, Burn Multiple
- Frame challenges as "investment thesis risks" not "problems"
- Include clear "Path to \_\_\_" milestones

### For All-Hands / Company-Wide

**Simplify and inspire**:

- Use accessible language (no accounting jargon)
- Lead with growth metrics that everyone understands (revenue, customers, team size)
- Connect financial performance to company mission
- Show how team effort translates to results
- Omit sensitive details (individual salaries, M&A targets, runway-to-zero)

## Complexity Variations

### Simple Dashboard (2-3 Hours)

- 4 KPI cards + 1 trend chart + 1 breakdown chart
- Single period (current month or quarter)
- No variance analysis
- Best for: Monthly team update, seed-stage founders

### Standard Budget Board (4-8 Hours)

- Department breakdown + allocation visual + cash flow
- Annual budget with quarterly splits
- Variance tracking (budget vs. actual)
- Approval workflow
- Best for: Series A+ companies, annual planning

### Advanced Financial Dashboard (8-16 Hours)

- 6+ KPI cards with sparklines
- 12-month trend lines with comparisons
- Departmental breakdown with drill-down
- Cash flow waterfall
- Alert system with severity tiers
- Best for: CFO monthly review, board meetings

### Expert Investment Analysis (16-24 Hours)

- Multi-option comparison matrix
- Financial projections per option
- Risk-return analysis
- Due diligence status
- Sensitivity analysis
- Recommendation with full rationale
- Best for: M&A decisions, major capex, strategic bets

## Special Use Cases

### Budget vs. Actual Variance Board

A focused board specifically for tracking how spending compares to plan:

```
+-------------------------------------------------+
| BUDGET VS. ACTUAL — February 2026               |
+-------------------------------------------------+
| Overall: $2,400K spent vs. $2,500K budget       |
|          $100K under budget (4.0%)  [GREEN]     |
+-------------------------------------------------+
| Department-level variance waterfall             |
| [Each dept bar showing over/under]              |
+-------------------------------------------------+
| Top 5 Line Items Over Budget (detail)           |
| Top 5 Line Items Under Budget (detail)          |
+-------------------------------------------------+
| Reforecast Recommendations                      |
+-------------------------------------------------+
```

### Scenario Planning Board

A board showing three financial scenarios side by side:

```
+--BASELINE--+  +--GROWTH---+  +--DOWNSIDE-+
| Revenue:   |  | Revenue:  |  | Revenue:  |
| $38M       |  | $45M      |  | $30M      |
| EBITDA:    |  | EBITDA:   |  | EBITDA:   |
| $5.7M      |  | $7.8M     |  | $2.1M     |
| Cash EOY:  |  | Cash EOY: |  | Cash EOY: |
| $20.6M     |  | $24.2M    |  | $15.8M    |
| Headcount: |  | Headcount:|  | Headcount:|
| 187        |  | 210       |  | 165       |
+------------+  +-----------+  +-----------+
|   ASSUMPTIONS & TRIGGER POINTS             |
+--------------------------------------------+
```

### Fundraising Finance Board

A board designed for investor due diligence:

```
+-------------------------------------------------+
| [COMPANY] — Financial Overview for Series B     |
+-------------------------------------------------+
| ARR: $4.2M  |  Growth: 15% MoM  |  NDR: 125%  |
+-------------------------------------------------+
| Unit Economics:  CAC: $450  LTV: $5,733  12.7x |
+-------------------------------------------------+
| Revenue Model: 3 streams, 3 tiers, 3 scenarios |
+-------------------------------------------------+
| Use of Funds: Engineering (50%), S&M (30%),     |
|               G&A (10%), Buffer (10%)           |
+-------------------------------------------------+
| 18-Month Milestones & Capital Needs             |
+-------------------------------------------------+
```
