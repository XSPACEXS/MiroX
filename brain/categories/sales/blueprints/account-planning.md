# Account Planning Board

## Overview

- **Purpose**: Provide a comprehensive strategic plan for a high-value target or existing account, mapping stakeholders, relationship strength, revenue opportunities, competitive landscape, and coordinated action items on a single visual canvas.
- **Best For**: Enterprise sales teams, key account managers, customer success leaders planning expansion, and sales leadership conducting quarterly account reviews.
- **Complexity**: Advanced
- **Board Size**: 6000x4000px

## Board Layout

The board is organized into a 3x3 grid of frames with a prominent account overview banner at the top. The left column focuses on people (stakeholders), the center on strategy (opportunities and actions), and the right on metrics (revenue and timeline).

```
+=================================================================+
|                    ACCOUNT OVERVIEW BANNER                        |
|  [Logo] [Company] [Industry] [ARR] [Health Score] [Owner]        |
+=================================================================+
| STAKEHOLDER MAP    | RELATIONSHIP     | REVENUE                  |
|                    | STRENGTH MATRIX  | LANDSCAPE                |
|  [org chart with   | [grid: champion, | [current ARR, expansion  |
|   influence lines] |  supporter,      |  potential, whitespace]  |

```

## Connectors & Flow

- Solid arrows from Stakeholder Map to Relationship Strength Matrix (indicating people flow to analysis).
- Dashed arrows from Opportunities & White Space to relevant stakeholder cards in the Stakeholder Map (showing sponsor relationships).
- Solid arrows from Opportunities & White Space down to Action Plan & Timeline (strategy flows to execution).
- Dotted arrows from Revenue Landscape to Opportunities (current products informing expansion).
- Connector from Competitive Landscape to Opportunities (competitive context informing positioning).

## Example Content

All frames above contain realistic pre-filled content for the Pinnacle Financial Group account. Key strategic narrative:

**Account Story**: Pinnacle Financial is a Fortune 500 financial services company currently spending $340K ARR across three products. The compliance dashboard is a home run (9/10 health), but the reporting add-on is at risk (6/10). The biggest expansion opportunity is Fraud Detection ($280K) driven by new CFPB regulations. The main blocker is SVP Operations Greg Holloway who favors the incumbent. The strategic play is to build a direct relationship with CFO Patricia Owens through executive engagement while simultaneously proving ROI to neutralize Holloway's objections.

**12-Month Revenue Target**: Grow from $340K to $540K ARR (+59%) by closing Fraud Detection and renewing all existing products.

## Build Instructions

1. Set board background to #F0F2F5 and dimensions to 6000x4000px.
2. Create the Account Overview Banner (5800x500px) at the top with #1B2A4A background. Add company name, metadata text, and six white info card rectangles.
3. Create a 3x2 grid of frames below the banner, each approximately 1800x1500px, with white backgrounds.
4. Build the Stakeholder Map (Frame 2) with stakeholder cards using color-coded role badges. Draw org chart lines between cards.
5. Build the Relationship Strength Matrix (Frame 3) as a 2x2 quadrant grid with colored backgrounds. Place stakeholder circles in the appropriate quadrants.
6. Build the Revenue Landscape (Frame 4) with current product cards (green) and expansion opportunity cards (blue).
7. Build the Competitive Landscape (Frame 5) with competitor analysis cards including strengths/weaknesses stickies.
8. Build the Opportunities & White Space (Frame 6) with detailed opportunity stickies including value, sponsor, stage, and compelling event.
9. Build the Action Plan & Timeline (Frame 7) organized by quarter with color-coded action stickies.
10. Create the Meeting Notes frame (5800x600px) at the bottom with chronological meeting cards.
11. Add all connectors: solid arrows for direct relationships, dashed for influence, dotted for informational links.

## Tips & Best Practices

- **Update before every account review**: This board should be the single source of truth. Stale account plans erode team confidence.
- **Map all stakeholders, not just friendlies**: The blockers and gatekeepers matter as much as champions. Include a plan to neutralize or convert each blocker.
- **Track relationship strength honestly**: If you have not spoken to someone in 60+ days, they are not a strong relationship — downgrade them on the matrix.
- **Tie every action to a revenue outcome**: Every item in the Action Plan should trace back to either protecting existing ARR or creating new pipeline.
- **Include competitive intelligence**: Update the Competitive Landscape whenever you learn something new from the customer. This intel feeds your entire sales team.
- **Review with your manager monthly**: Account plans work best as collaborative documents. Your manager may have executive relationships that unlock stuck deals.
