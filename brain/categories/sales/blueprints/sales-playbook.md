# Sales Playbook Board

## Overview

- **Purpose**: Codify the entire sales motion into a visual, referenceable playbook covering ICP definition, outreach sequences, discovery frameworks, objection handling scripts, competitor battlecards, and closing techniques so every rep can execute consistently.
- **Best For**: Sales enablement teams building onboarding materials, sales managers standardizing their team's approach, founders documenting their first repeatable sales process, RevOps teams creating scalable playbooks.
- **Complexity**: Advanced
- **Board Size**: 7000x4500px

## Board Layout

The board is organized as a horizontal flow from left (understanding the buyer) to right (closing the deal), with six major sections arranged in a 3x2 grid plus a header and reference footer.

```
+====================================================================+
|                       PLAYBOOK HEADER                                |
|  [Company] [Version] [Last Updated] [Owner]                         |
+====================================================================+
| IDEAL CUSTOMER     | OUTREACH           | DISCOVERY                 |
| PROFILE (ICP)      | SEQUENCES          | FRAMEWORK                 |
|                    |                    |                           |
| [persona cards,    | [email/call/social | [questions, MEDDPICC,     |
|  firmographics,    |  sequence flows    |  qualification criteria]  |
```

## Example Content

All frames above contain realistic pre-filled content for a fictional SaaS analytics company called "CloudMetrics." The playbook covers:

**Company Context**: CloudMetrics is a mid-market SaaS analytics platform ($50/user/month) that competes with Tableau, Looker, and in-house builds. The target buyer is a VP of Data or CFO at companies with 200-5,000 employees.

**Sales Motion**: Outbound-heavy (60% outbound, 40% inbound). Average deal size is $85,000 ARR. Average sales cycle is 45 days. Win rate against Tableau is 42%, against Looker is 38%, against in-house is 55%.

## Tips & Best Practices

- **Keep it alive**: A playbook that is not updated quarterly becomes shelfware. Assign a DRI (Directly Responsible Individual) to own updates.
- **Crowdsource objection responses**: When a rep hears a new objection or finds a winning response, add it to the board immediately. The best playbooks are team-built.
- **Role-play weekly**: Use the objection handling section for live role-play exercises during team meetings. Read the objection, have a rep respond, then review the suggested framework.
- **Track what works**: Add win/loss annotations to battlecards. If your win rate against Tableau drops below 35%, revisit your positioning.
- **Customize, do not copy-paste**: These templates are starting points. Every prospect conversation should feel personalized, not scripted.
- **Onboard with the playbook**: New reps should read this board end-to-end in their first week, then shadow experienced reps executing each section live.
