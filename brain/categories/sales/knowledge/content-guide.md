# Sales Board Content Guide

## Introduction

A beautifully designed sales board with empty or generic content is worthless. Content is the substance that transforms a layout into a strategic tool. This guide covers what content belongs on each type of sales board, how to write it, how to structure data for visual consumption, and what real-world examples look like. Every section is informed by how actual revenue teams communicate, decide, and operate.

---

## Essential Content Sections by Board Type

### Pipeline Board Content

A pipeline board requires seven categories of content:

1. **Pipeline Summary Metrics**
   - Total pipeline value (unweighted)
   - Weighted pipeline value
   - Deal count by stage
   - Pipeline coverage ratio (pipeline / quota)
   - Average deal size
   - Average sales cycle length (days)
   - Win rate (trailing period)

2. **Deal Cards**
   Every deal card must contain:
   - Company name and deal title
   - Dollar value
   - Win probability (percentage)
   - Contact name and title
   - Lead source
   - Next step with date
   - Days in current stage
   - Owner (if team board)

3. **Stage Definitions**
   Each pipeline stage should have a visible definition so the team agrees on what qualifies a deal for that stage. Example:
   - **Lead**: Initial interest identified. No qualifying conversation yet.
   - **Qualified**: BANT confirmed — Budget, Authority, Need, Timeline all validated.
   - **Proposal**: Formal proposal or SOW sent to prospect.
   - **Negotiation**: Pricing, terms, or contract under active discussion.
   - **Closed Won**: Signed contract and PO received.
   - **Closed Lost**: Deal officially dead. Reason documented.

4. **Conversion Rate Labels**
   Between each stage column, display the historical conversion rate:
   - Lead to Qualified: 62%
   - Qualified to Proposal: 58%
   - Proposal to Negotiation: 47%
   - Negotiation to Won: 72%
   - Overall Lead to Won: 12%

5. **Velocity Metrics**
   Average days in each stage:
   - Lead: 8 days
   - Qualified: 12 days
   - Proposal: 9 days
   - Negotiation: 14 days
   - Total cycle: 34 days (target: 38)

6. **Aging Deal Alerts**
   Any deal exceeding 1.5x the average stage duration should be flagged:
   - "BrightEdge Media — 18 days in Qualified (avg: 12 days)"
   - "Summit Education — 16 days in Proposal (avg: 9 days)"

7. **Period Context**
   - Review cadence: "Weekly, Mondays at 9:00 AM"
   - CRM sync status: "Last sync: March 4, 2026"
   - Quota: "$1,200,000 (Q1 combined)"
   - Current attainment: "$412,000 (34.3%)"

### Account Planning Content

1. **Account Identity**
   - Company name, logo, industry vertical
   - Account tier (Strategic / Growth / Maintain)
   - Annual Recurring Revenue (current)
   - Account health score (RAG)
   - Account owner and team

2. **Stakeholder Map Content**
   For each stakeholder:
   - Name and title
   - Role in buying process (Champion, Economic Buyer, Technical Evaluator, Blocker, Coach, End User)
   - Relationship strength (Strong / Growing / Neutral / Cold)
   - Last interaction date and type
   - Communication preference
   - Personal priorities and motivations

3. **Relationship Strength Matrix**
   A grid categorizing all known contacts:
   - Champions: People who actively sell internally on your behalf
   - Supporters: People who favor your solution but are not actively advocating
   - Neutral: No strong opinion formed yet
   - Blockers: People who prefer a competitor or the status quo

4. **Revenue Landscape**
   - Current ARR from this account
   - Products/modules currently deployed
   - Whitespace: Products they do not yet use
   - Expansion potential (dollar estimate)
   - Renewal date and risk assessment
   - Cross-sell and up-sell opportunities

5. **Competitive Intelligence**
   For each competitor in the account:
   - Competitor name and products used
   - Competitor strengths in this account
   - Competitor weaknesses we can exploit
   - Our positioning statement against this competitor
   - Trap questions to ask that highlight competitor weaknesses

6. **Action Plan**
   Quarterly action items with:
   - Action description
   - Owner
   - Due date
   - Status (Not Started / In Progress / Complete / Blocked)
   - Expected outcome

### Playbook Content

1. **Ideal Customer Profile (ICP)**
   - Firmographic criteria: industry, company size (employees), revenue range, geography
   - Technographic criteria: tech stack, tools used, platforms
   - Organizational criteria: team structure, budget authority level
   - Behavioral criteria: buying signals, engagement patterns
   - Disqualification criteria: explicit reasons NOT to pursue

2. **Buyer Personas**
   For each persona (typically 2-4):
   - Title and reporting line
   - Day-to-day responsibilities
   - Top 3 pain points
   - Top 3 goals
   - How they evaluate solutions
   - Common objections
   - Messaging that resonates
   - Messaging that backfires

3. **Outreach Sequences**
   For each sequence (cold outbound, inbound follow-up, referral):
   - Day-by-day cadence (Day 1: Email, Day 3: Call, Day 5: Email, etc.)
   - Template text for each touchpoint
   - Subject line options
   - Call opening script
   - LinkedIn message template
   - Voicemail script
   - Expected response rates by step

4. **Discovery Framework**
   The qualification methodology (MEDDPICC, BANT, SPIN, etc.) with:
   - Each category defined
   - 3-5 specific questions per category
   - What "good" answers sound like
   - Red flag answers that signal disqualification
   - How to score the discovery

5. **Objection Handling**
   For each common objection (typically 8-12):
   - The objection as the prospect says it
   - The underlying concern behind the objection
   - The recommended response framework
   - Example response script
   - Follow-up question to advance the conversation
   - Supporting proof points (case studies, data)

6. **Competitor Battlecards**
   For each major competitor:
   - Competitor overview (positioning, pricing, market segment)
   - Feature comparison table
   - Our strengths vs. them
   - Their strengths vs. us
   - Trap questions to ask prospects
   - Landmine topics to raise
   - Positioning statement when they come up

```
Angela Rossi — Chief Information Officer
Role: Economic Buyer (final budget authority)
Relationship: STRONG (met 4x in past quarter)
Last interaction: Feb 28, 2026 — Executive lunch
Priorities: Reduce IT operational costs, consolidate vendors
Motivation: Board is pressuring for digital transformation ROI
Communication: Prefers 30-min structured agendas, no surprises

```

### Example: Objection Handling Card

```
OBJECTION: "Your solution is too expensive compared to [Competitor]."

Underlying concern: Budget constraint or lack of perceived value differential.

Response framework:
1. Acknowledge: "I understand cost is a critical factor."
2. Reframe: "Let me share what our customers have found when comparing total cost of ownership..."
3. Evidence: "Acme Corp initially chose [Competitor] and migrated to us after 8 months because [specific reason]. Their total cost over 2 years was actually 30% higher with [Competitor] due to [hidden costs]."
4. Question: "If I could show you a 3-year TCO comparison that accounts for implementation, support, and scaling costs, would that be helpful?"

Supporting proof: Acme Corp case study, TCO calculator spreadsheet, Gartner peer review data.
```

### Example: Competitor Battlecard

```
COMPETITOR: Acme Solutions

Positioning: "Enterprise-grade platform for large organizations"
Target Market: Enterprise (5,000+ employees)
Pricing: $80-150K/year (higher than us)
Market Share: ~18% in our segment

OUR STRENGTHS vs. ACME:
- 3x faster implementation (6 weeks vs. 18 weeks)
- Self-service configuration (no professional services required)
- Modern API-first architecture
- 99.99% uptime SLA (theirs is 99.9%)

THEIR STRENGTHS vs. US:
- Deeper enterprise feature set (audit trails, SOC 2 Type II)
- Established brand recognition with Fortune 500
- Larger professional services team for customization
- Broader ERP integration library

TRAP QUESTIONS:
- "How long did their last implementation take? Were there any overruns?"
- "Do they charge separately for professional services?"
- "What is their API rate limit? Can it handle your transaction volume?"

LANDMINE TOPICS:
- Ask about their recent security incident (Jan 2026 data breach disclosure)
- Ask about their product roadmap commitment — they missed 3 of 4 roadmap items in 2025

POSITIONING WHEN ACME IS IN THE DEAL:
"Unlike legacy enterprise platforms that require 6-month implementations and ongoing professional services, [Our Company] delivers the same enterprise capabilities with a modern, self-service architecture that your team can own from day one."
```

## Copywriting Principles for Sales Boards

### Write for Scanning, Not Reading

Sales professionals scan boards during high-pressure moments — pipeline reviews, deal strategy sessions, mid-call reference. Write accordingly:

- Use bullet points, not paragraphs
- Lead with the most important information
- Use bold for key data points
- Keep deal card text to 5 lines maximum
- Use abbreviations when meaning is clear (AE, ACV, ARR, MRR, SDR, BDR, ICP, MEDDPICC)
- Front-load numbers — "$210K ERP Migration" not "ERP Migration worth $210K"

### Tone and Voice for Sales Content

- **Direct**: "Follow up by March 8" not "It would be beneficial to follow up"
- **Specific**: "$210,000" not "significant deal"
- **Action-oriented**: "Schedule demo" not "Demo pending"
- **Honest**: "Win rate below target" not "Win rate improving" (when it is not)
- **Concise**: "Budget confirmed $100K" not "The client has confirmed that their allocated budget for this initiative is approximately $100,000"

### Numbers Formatting Standards

| Type                | Format                        | Example                |
| ------------------- | ----------------------------- | ---------------------- |
| Currency < $10,000  | Full number with $            | $8,500                 |
| Currency $10K-$999K | Abbreviated with K            | $210K                  |
| Currency $1M+       | Abbreviated with M, 1 decimal | $2.4M                  |
| Percentage          | One decimal max               | 28.3%                  |
| Change              | Sign prefix + number          | +8.3% or -2.1%         |
| Date                | Mon DD, YYYY                  | Mar 6, 2026            |
| Duration            | Number + unit                 | 34 days                |
| Count               | Full number or abbreviated    | 47 deals or 2.4K leads |

## Content Questions Checklist

Before declaring a sales board complete, answer these questions:

### Pipeline Boards

- Can I identify every deal's next step and deadline?
- Can I see which deals are stale (aging beyond average)?
- Can I calculate pipeline coverage for the quota?
- Can I see conversion rates between stages?
- Is every deal attributed to an owner?
- Is the last-update timestamp visible and current?

### Account Plans

- Are all known stakeholders mapped with roles and relationship strength?
- Is the competitive landscape documented with strengths and weaknesses?
- Are expansion opportunities sized with dollar estimates?
- Does the action plan have specific owners and dates?
- Is the account health scored with a clear methodology?

### Playbooks

- Is the ICP specific enough to disqualify non-fits?
- Are personas based on actual customer interviews, not assumptions?
- Are outreach templates written in a voice reps would actually use?
- Are objection responses battle-tested from real conversations?
- Are competitor battlecards updated within the last 90 days?

### Forecasts

- Are forecast categories (Commit, Best Case, Upside) clearly defined?
- Is the forecast methodology consistent across all reps?
- Are risk factors called out for each major deal?
- Is the forecast compared to target/quota with a gap analysis?
- Is there a historical comparison (this quarter vs. last quarter)?

## Content Maintenance Cadence

| Board Type              | Update Frequency       | Who Updates             | Review Cadence               |
| ----------------------- | ---------------------- | ----------------------- | ---------------------------- |
| Pipeline                | Daily (deal movements) | AEs and SDRs            | Weekly pipeline review       |
| Account Plan            | Monthly (strategy)     | Account Manager/CSM     | Quarterly business review    |
| Playbook                | Quarterly (content)    | Sales Enablement        | Bi-annual overhaul           |
| Deal Tracker / Forecast | Weekly (numbers)       | RevOps or Sales Manager | Weekly forecast call         |
| Competitor Battlecards  | Monthly (intelligence) | Product Marketing       | Quarterly competitive review |

## Domain-Specific Vocabulary

A glossary of terms that appear on sales boards, for reference:

| Term              | Definition                                                                                                                                   |
| ----------------- | -------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------- |
| ACV               | Annual Contract Value — the annualized revenue from a single customer contract                                                               |
| ARR               | Annual Recurring Revenue — total annualized value of all active subscriptions                                                                |
| MRR               | Monthly Recurring Revenue — ARR divided by 12                                                                                                |
| AE                | Account Executive — the sales rep who owns the deal                                                                                          |
| SDR/BDR           | Sales/Business Development Rep — generates qualified leads                                                                                   |
| ICP               | Ideal Customer Profile — the firmographic definition of your best-fit customer                                                               |
| MEDDPICC          | Qualification framework: Metrics, Economic Buyer, Decision Process, Decision Criteria, Paper Process, Implicated Pain, Champion, Competition |
| BANT              | Qualification framework: Budget, Authority, Need, Timeline                                                                                   |
| Pipeline Coverage | Ratio of total pipeline to quota — typically 3x+ is healthy                                                                                  |
| Win Rate          | Percentage of opportunities that close as won                                                                                                |
| Velocity          | Speed at which deals move through the pipeline (days per stage)                                                                              |
| Weighted Pipeline | Sum of (deal value x probability) across all open deals                                                                                      |
| RAG               | Red-Amber-Green status indicator                                                                                                             |
| QBR               | Quarterly Business Review                                                                                                                    |
| RevOps            | Revenue Operations — the team that manages sales process, tools, and analytics                                                               |
| Battlecard        | A competitive reference document comparing your solution to a specific competitor                                                            |
| Champion          | An internal advocate at the prospect who actively sells your solution to their colleagues                                                    |
