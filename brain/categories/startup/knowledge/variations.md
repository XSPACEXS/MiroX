# Startup Board Variations

## Introduction

Not all startups are the same, and their boards should not be either. A marketplace startup faces different challenges than a SaaS company. A hardware startup has considerations that a software-only company does not. A pre-seed founder with 5 customer interviews needs a different board than a Series B company with $10M ARR. This guide covers specific adaptations for different business models, stages, team sizes, and contexts.

---

## Business Model Variations

### SaaS (Software-as-a-Service)

**Focus Metrics**: MRR, ARR, churn rate, LTV, CAC, activation rate, NPS, expansion revenue
**Special Lean Canvas Adaptations**:

- Revenue Streams block should include pricing tiers with conversion rates between them
- Key Metrics should emphasize retention and expansion over acquisition
- Cost Structure should separate cloud infrastructure from people costs
- UVP should differentiate from both enterprise competitors (cheaper, simpler) and consumer tools (more powerful, team-oriented)

**Pitch Deck Adaptations**:

- Traction slide: MRR growth chart is the hero metric
- Business model slide: Show unit economics (LTV:CAC ratio, payback period)
- Include a slide on retention and expansion — investors care about net revenue retention more than new logo acquisition

### Marketplace / Two-Sided Platform

**Focus Metrics**: GMV, take rate, liquidity (matches per listing), supply-side fill rate, demand-side conversion
**Special Lean Canvas Adaptations**:

- Customer Segments must be split: Supply side (e.g., freelancers) and Demand side (e.g., companies)
- Problem block needs two problem sets — one per side
- Solution must address both sides and the matching mechanism
- Key Metrics should track both sides independently plus the matching efficiency
- Revenue Streams should show take rate and any premium/featured listing revenue

**MVP Planning Adaptations**:

- Feature backlog should be color-coded by side (supply vs. demand)
- Prioritization must account for chicken-and-egg sequencing
- Include a "Manual Operations" section for processes that will be manual before automation
- Timeline should interleave product building with supply-side recruitment

### Hardware / Physical Product

**Focus Metrics**: Unit cost (BOM), units shipped, pre-orders, manufacturing yield, certification status
**Special Lean Canvas Adaptations**:

- Add custom blocks: "Bill of Materials" (component costs), "Manufacturing Plan" (prototype → batch → scale), "Certification Timeline" (UL, FCC, CE, FDA as applicable)
- Cost Structure must separate R&D from manufacturing from distribution
- Revenue Streams should distinguish direct sales from channel partner margins
- Unfair Advantage should address IP (patents, trade secrets) and manufacturing partnerships

**Pitch Deck Adaptations**:

- Include a slide on manufacturing and supply chain
- Address the "valley of death" between prototype and mass production
- Show pre-order or LOI evidence (letters of intent from potential customers)
- Include certification timeline and regulatory strategy

### B2B Enterprise

**Focus Metrics**: ACV (annual contract value), pipeline, win rate, sales cycle length, logo count, expansion rate
**Special Lean Canvas Adaptations**:

- Customer Segments should include ICP (ideal customer profile) with firmographics
- Channels must address the enterprise sales motion (outbound, events, partnerships)
- Revenue Streams should show ACV distribution and contract length
- Key Metrics should track pipeline stages and conversion rates
- Cost Structure should account for the long sales cycle (CAC includes time cost)

**Investor Pipeline Adaptations**:

- Investors should be segmented by those who understand enterprise sales cycles
- Traction metrics should include pipeline value, not just closed revenue
- Include a customer case study or pilot results as proof of enterprise viability

### Consumer / B2C

**Focus Metrics**: DAU/MAU, retention curves, viral coefficient (K-factor), engagement time, conversion to paid
**Special Lean Canvas Adaptations**:

- Customer Segments should include psychographic detail (behaviors, habits, motivations)
- Channels should emphasize organic/viral growth mechanics alongside paid acquisition
- Key Metrics should weight engagement and retention over revenue (early stage)
- UVP should be emotionally resonant, not just functionally descriptive

**Pitch Deck Adaptations**:

- Lead with engagement data, not revenue
- Show retention curves (Day 1, Day 7, Day 30)
- Demonstrate virality mechanics if applicable
- Address the path to monetization (even if revenue is not the immediate priority)

### API / Developer Tools

**Focus Metrics**: API calls, developer sign-ups, time-to-first-API-call, documentation engagement, paid plan conversion
**Special Lean Canvas Adaptations**:

- Customer Segments should distinguish between the developer (user) and the company (buyer)
- Channels should include developer community, documentation, GitHub, Stack Overflow
- Key Metrics should track developer experience metrics (time to first value, documentation bounce rate)
- Revenue Streams should show usage-based pricing tiers

## Stage Variations

### Idea Stage (Pre-Customer Discovery)

**Board Focus**: Hypothesis documentation, research planning
**Adaptations**:

- All canvas blocks carry [HYPOTHESIS] badges — nothing is validated
- Include a "Research Plan" section: who to interview, what to learn, how many interviews
- Simpler board design — do not over-polish a board that will change completely after 10 interviews
- Revenue and Cost blocks may be blank or contain rough estimates
- Board size: 4000 x 3000px (smaller, simpler)

### Pre-Seed (Post-Discovery, Pre-Product)

**Board Focus**: Validated problem, hypothesized solution, early metrics plan
**Adaptations**:

- Problem and Customer Segments should be VALIDATED (or at least PARTIAL)
- Solution is HYPOTHESIS or TESTING (MVP in development)
- Include interview evidence in Problem block (n=XX, key quotes)
- Key Metrics should define what will be measured, even if measurement has not begun
- Pitch Deck emphasis: Problem validation, team credibility, market size

### Seed (Post-MVP, Early Revenue)

**Board Focus**: Product-market fit signals, early traction, fundraising readiness
**Adaptations**:

- Key Metrics should have actual data (MRR, users, activation rate, NPS)
- Validation Tracker should show a mix of VALIDATED and TESTING
- Investor Pipeline becomes relevant — active fundraise management
- MVP Planning shifts to "next phase" planning (what to build after MVP)
- Pitch Deck emphasis: Traction, unit economics, path to scale

### Series A (Validated Model, Scaling)

**Board Focus**: Repeatable sales motion, unit economics, team scaling
**Adaptations**:

- Most canvas blocks should be VALIDATED with strong evidence
- Key Metrics should include trailing 12-month trends
- Revenue Streams should show cohort analysis (retention by month of sign-up)
- Cost Structure should include hiring plan and burn rate projections
- Pitch Deck emphasis: Repeatability, market expansion, team scaling plan
- Add competitive landscape analysis as a dedicated section

### Series B+ (Growth Stage)

**Board Focus**: Go-to-market execution, international expansion, market dominance
**Adaptations**:

- Lean Canvas becomes less relevant — the business model is validated
- Replace with a strategic planning board (OKRs, market expansion map, competitive positioning)
- Investor Pipeline focuses on growth equity and strategic investors
- Pitch Deck is data-heavy with detailed financial models
- Board design should be more polished (higher production value for larger rounds)

## Team Size Variations

### Solo Founder

**Adaptations**:

- Boards serve as personal thinking tools — less polish needed
- Lean Canvas is the primary board — serves as both strategy and communication tool
- Include a "Founder Tasks" section on the MVP board (personal to-do, not team kanban)
- Investor Pipeline is critical when fundraising (managing all conversations alone)
- Simpler visual design — efficiency over aesthetics

### 2-3 Person Founding Team

**Adaptations**:

- Boards serve as alignment tools — ensure co-founders share the same mental model
- Lean Canvas should be built collaboratively in a workshop session
- MVP Planning should assign owners to each feature and experiment
- Include a "Team Responsibilities" section: who owns what
- Version control becomes important — track who changed what and why

### 5-10 Person Team

**Adaptations**:

- Boards serve as onboarding tools — new hires walk the board to understand the business
- MVP Planning should use a proper sprint-based timeline with team capacity planning
- Add a "Team" section to the Lean Canvas or create a separate team board
- Investor Pipeline should be accessible to the fundraising team member (not just founders)

### 10+ Person Team

**Adaptations**:

- Create department-specific views linked to the master boards
- MVP Planning should link to the engineering team's sprint planning tools (Jira, Linear)
- Lean Canvas becomes a reference document, not a daily working tool
- Add board access permissions — not everyone needs edit access to the investor pipeline

## Context Variations

### Accelerator / Incubator

**Adaptations**:

- Include accelerator-specific milestones (demo day, mentor meetings, office hours)
- Version the canvas with weekly cadence (accelerators move fast)
- Pitch Deck should evolve toward the demo day format
- Include mentor feedback tracking: which advisors said what and how it influenced decisions
- Multi-board hub is recommended to show the full picture to program directors

### Bootstrapped / Profitable

**Adaptations**:

- De-emphasize fundraising boards (Investor Pipeline may not be needed)
- Lean Canvas should emphasize profitability metrics (profit margin, cash flow, payback period)
- MVP Planning should be constrained by revenue, not runway
- Cost Structure should show profitability trajectory, not just burn rate
- Pitch Deck may focus on strategic partnerships or acquisition interest rather than VC fundraising

### Pivoting

**Adaptations**:

- Display pre-pivot and post-pivot canvases side by side (60% opacity for pre-pivot)
- Include a "Pivot Rationale" section with evidence for why the old model failed
- Mark pre-pivot hypotheses as INVALIDATED with learning documentation
- Post-pivot hypotheses should reference pre-pivot learnings
- Update version number to a major version (e.g., v2.3 → v3.0)

### International Expansion

**Adaptations**:

- Customer Segments should be split by geography with local market characteristics
- Channels should address market-specific distribution (WeChat vs. Instagram, Mercado Libre vs. Amazon)
- Cost Structure should include localization costs (translation, compliance, local team)
- Revenue Streams should show currency considerations and pricing localization
- Regulatory and compliance requirements vary by market — add a "Regulatory" section

### Deep Tech / Research-Heavy

**Adaptations**:

- Solution block should distinguish between "what works now" and "what requires research breakthroughs"
- Include a "Technology Readiness Level" (TRL) indicator for core technologies
- Key Metrics should include research milestones alongside business metrics
- Timeline on MVP Planning should account for research uncertainty (longer, more variable)
- Pitch Deck should address the "technology risk" explicitly and show the de-risking plan
- Unfair Advantage should emphasize IP, patents, and research team credentials

## Complexity Variations

### Minimal Canvas (Idea Stage)

- Single Lean Canvas, no linked boards
- 5 blocks filled (Problem, Segments, UVP, Solution, Revenue)
- All [HYPOTHESIS] badges
- No validation tracker (nothing to track yet)
- Board size: 4000 x 2500px
- Build time: 30 minutes

### Standard Canvas Set (Seed Stage)

- Lean Canvas + Pitch Deck Storyboard
- All 9 blocks filled with validation badges
- Validation tracker with 5-8 hypotheses
- Board sizes: Standard per board type
- Build time: 3-4 hours total

### Full Startup Hub (Active Fundraise)

- Lean Canvas + Pitch Deck + Investor Pipeline + MVP Plan + Hub Board
- All boards cross-referenced and version-synchronized
- Validation tracker with 10+ hypotheses
- Daily update cadence on pipeline, weekly on others
- Board sizes: Standard per board type + 3000x2000 hub
- Build time: 8-12 hours initial, 2-3 hours/week maintenance

## Variation Selection Matrix

| Factor                     | Recommendation                                                       |
| -------------------------- | -------------------------------------------------------------------- |
| SaaS business model        | Emphasize MRR, retention, unit economics in all boards               |
| Marketplace business model | Split all boards by supply/demand side, address chicken-and-egg      |
| Hardware product           | Add BOM, manufacturing, certification blocks to canvas               |
| Idea stage                 | Minimal canvas, all hypotheses, focus on research plan               |
| Pre-seed with interviews   | Standard canvas with validated Problem, hypothesis Solution          |
| Seed with revenue          | Full canvas set, add Investor Pipeline, validated Key Metrics        |
| Series A+                  | Polished, data-heavy, competitive analysis, detailed financials      |
| Solo founder               | Personal thinking tool, simpler design, efficiency over aesthetics   |
| 5+ person team             | Alignment and onboarding tool, version control, team assignments     |
| Accelerator context        | Weekly versioning, demo day prep, mentor feedback tracking           |
| Bootstrapped               | De-emphasize fundraising, emphasize profitability metrics            |
| Post-pivot                 | Side-by-side canvases, pivot rationale, learning documentation       |
| Deep tech                  | TRL indicators, research milestones, technology risk de-risking plan |
