# Pitch Deck Planning Board

## Overview

- **Purpose**: Plan and storyboard a fundraising pitch deck slide-by-slide on a Miro board — outlining the narrative arc, key talking points, data to include, visual direction, and rehearsal notes for each slide so the deck tells a compelling, investor-ready story.
- **Best For**: Founders preparing for seed or Series A fundraising, startup teams aligning on pitch narrative, advisors coaching founders on storytelling, accelerator demo day preparation.
- **Complexity**: Medium
- **Board Size**: 6000x3000px

## Board Layout

The board uses a horizontal storyboard layout with slide cards arranged in presentation order, a narrative arc guide at the top, and a preparation notes section at the bottom.

```
+=================================================================+
|                    NARRATIVE ARC GUIDE                            |
|  [Hook] → [Problem] → [Solution] → [Why Now] → [Traction] →    |
|  [Market] → [Business Model] → [Team] → [Financials] → [Ask]   |
+=================================================================+
| SLIDE 1  | SLIDE 2  | SLIDE 3   | SLIDE 4  | SLIDE 5            |
| HOOK     | PROBLEM  | SOLUTION  | DEMO     | WHY NOW            |
|          |          |           |          |                    |
| [thumb]  | [thumb]  | [thumb]   | [thumb]  | [thumb]            |
| [points] | [points] | [points]  | [points] | [points]           |
+----------+----------+-----------+----------+--------------------+
| SLIDE 6  | SLIDE 7  | SLIDE 8   | SLIDE 9  | SLIDE 10           |
| TRACTION | MARKET   | BIZ MODEL | TEAM     | FINANCIALS         |
|          |          |           |          |                    |
| [thumb]  | [thumb]  | [thumb]   | [thumb]  | [thumb]            |
| [points] | [points] | [points]  | [points] | [points]           |
+----------+----------+-----------+----------+--------------------+
| SLIDE 11 | SLIDE 12 |                                           |
| THE ASK  | APPENDIX |    REHEARSAL NOTES & TIMING               |
+=================================================================+
```

## Example Content

All frames contain a complete pitch deck plan for "DataBridge," a fictional AI-powered data integration startup raising a $3M seed round. The storyboard covers 11 presentation slides plus appendix, with detailed talking points, visual direction, timing guidance, and anticipated Q&A preparation.

**Pitch Context**: DataBridge is raising $3M at a $15M post-money valuation from early-stage VCs. The founders have backgrounds at Snowflake, Stripe, and Figma. ## Variations

1. **Demo Day Pitch (5 minutes)**: Compress to 6 slides: Hook, Problem+Solution (combined), Demo, Traction, Market+Model (combined), Ask. Remove Financials and Team detail.
2. **Series A Deck**: Expand Traction to 2 slides (metrics deep-dive + customer stories). Add a Competitive Moat slide. Expand Financials to include unit economics detail and path to profitability.
3. **Corporate Innovation Pitch**: Replace "The Ask" with "Resource Request" and "Milestones." Replace Market with "Internal Opportunity Size." Replace Team with "Team + Sponsorship Structure."

## Build Instructions

1. Set board background to #F5F7FA and dimensions to 6000x3000px.
2. Create the Narrative Arc Guide (5800x350px) at the top with #1565C0 background and connected pill shapes showing the story flow.
3. Create a grid of slide cards: 5 per row, 2 main rows, plus a third partial row. Each card is 1100x900px with white background.
4. For each slide card: add slide label (colored by section), thumbnail sketch rectangle, talking point stickies, visual notes sticky (blue), and duration text.
5. Create the Appendix card and Rehearsal Notes section at the bottom.
6. Build the Rehearsal section with timing guide, Q&A preparation stickies, and prep checklist.
7. Add horizontal flow arrows between slide cards and color-coded arc connector at the top.
8. Connect Q&A stickies back to their relevant slides with dashed arrows.

## Tips & Best Practices

- **Story first, slides second**: Plan the narrative on this board before opening PowerPoint or Keynote. The story is what investors remember, not the formatting.
- **One idea per slide**: If a slide has two main points, it should be two slides. Cognitive load is the enemy of investor engagement.
- **Show, do not tell**: The demo or product screenshot is the most important moment. Practice it until it is flawless.
- **Lead with traction**: If you have impressive traction, consider moving it before Why Now. Investors fund momentum.
- **Prepare for Q&A harder than the pitch**: Most deals are won or lost in the Q&A. Anticipate the 10 toughest questions and rehearse crisp answers.
- **Time yourself ruthlessly**: If you cannot deliver the pitch in 12 minutes, you are not ready. Cut slides, do not speed up.
- **Get feedback early**: Present to 3-5 friendly advisors before real pitches. Use this board to capture and incorporate their feedback.
