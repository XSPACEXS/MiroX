# Venue Layout Board

## Overview

- **Purpose**: A floor plan-style Miro board that visualizes the physical event venue from above, showing rooms, stages, booths, attendee flow paths, capacity labels, equipment placements, and logistics notes. This board replaces traditional CAD drawings with a collaborative, annotatable visual that the entire event team can reference and update.
- **Best For**: Event production managers, venue coordinators, AV teams, safety officers, and operations staff who need to understand the physical layout and logistics of the event space.
- **Complexity**: Advanced
- **Board Size**: 5000 x 4000px

## Board Layout

The board simulates a bird's-eye view floor plan of the San Francisco Convention Center. The main floor occupies the center of the board. A legend and key sit in the top-right corner. Equipment and logistics notes are placed alongside the relevant rooms. Flow path arrows show how attendees move through the space.

## Variations

1. **Small Venue (Single Room)**: Reduce to one large room with stage area, seating, registration desk, and refreshment table. Board size: 2000 x 1500px.
2. **Multi-Building Campus**: Add multiple floor plan frames side by side, connected by outdoor pathway arrows. Add a campus overview mini-map in the corner.
3. **Virtual Venue Map**: Replace physical rooms with virtual platform "rooms" (Zoom links, Discord channels). Keep the spatial metaphor but label as digital spaces.
4. **Festival / Outdoor Event**: Replace building floor plan with an open-air map showing tents, stages, food trucks, parking, and port-a-potty locations.

## Build Instructions

1. Create the board at 5000 x 4000px. Set background to Blueprint Blue (#E3F2FD) with grid enabled (50px).
2. Place the title banner at the top (4900 x 300px) with Deep Indigo background.
3. Create the Legend frame (1000 x 800px) in the top-right.
4. Build Level 1 as a large frame (4500 x 2200px). Draw room shapes as rectangles with appropriate fill colors. Label each room with name, area, and capacity.
5. Place equipment sticky notes (purple) adjacent to each room. Place logistics sticky notes (yellow/orange) near relevant areas.
6. Draw attendee flow arrows (green) through the floor plan.
7. Mark emergency exits with red circle icons.
8. Build Level 2 below Level 1 (4500 x 1200px) with breakout rooms and support spaces.
9. Add the Outdoor Area frame (4500 x 600px) below Level 2.
10. Create the Equipment Checklist frame (2200 x 800px) at bottom-left.
11. Create the Safety & Emergency Plan frame (2200 x 800px) at bottom-right with red border.
12. Draw all connectors: attendee flow (green), emergency routes (red dashed), equipment links (grey thin).
13. Lock room shapes and floor plan elements. Leave sticky notes and equipment lists editable.

## Tips & Best Practices

- Walk the actual venue with this board on a tablet, annotating discrepancies between the plan and reality.
- Use Miro's measurement tool to keep room proportions roughly accurate.
- Share this board with the AV vendor, caterer, and security team — they each can add their own sticky notes in different colors.
- Update the board after the venue walkthrough to reflect actual outlet positions, pillar locations, and sight-line obstructions.
- Print a simplified version (hide equipment notes) as a wayfinding map for attendees.
- After the event, annotate what worked and what did not — this becomes the baseline for next year's venue planning.
- Use Miro layers (if available) to toggle between "Attendee View" (clean map) and "Operations View" (all equipment and logistics notes).
