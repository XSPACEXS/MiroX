# Event Planning Boards — Content Guide

## Overview

Event board content straddles two worlds: the aspirational (what makes the event exciting
and desirable) and the operational (what makes the event actually work). The best event
boards blend both — a zone description should make you want to visit it AND tell the
production team what they need to set up. This guide covers the essential content for
each event board type, with real examples and copywriting guidelines.

---

## Essential Sections by Board Type

### Event Guide (Conference Overview) — Essential Content

| Section             | Required?   | Key Questions Answered                                   |
| ------------------- | ----------- | -------------------------------------------------------- |
| Event Identity      | Required    | Name, date, venue, tagline, expected attendance          |
| Zone Map            | Required    | What spaces exist and how are they connected?            |
| Zone Details        | Required    | What happens in each zone? Capacity, equipment, schedule |
| Schedule Overview   | Required    | Multi-day timeline with tracks                           |
| Speaker Highlights  | Required    | Who is speaking? Key bios and session details            |
| Sponsor/Expo        | Recommended | Who is sponsoring? Booth layouts, tiers                  |
| Logistics           | Recommended | Registration flow, wayfinding, accessibility info        |
| Engagement Features | Optional    | Networking activities, gamification, social media        |

### Agenda Board — Essential Content

| Section             | Required?   | Key Questions Answered                                       |
| ------------------- | ----------- | ------------------------------------------------------------ |
| Day/Track Structure | Required    | How many days? How many parallel tracks?                     |
| Session Blocks      | Required    | Title, speaker, time, duration, room for every session       |

| Section           | Required?   | Key Questions Answered                                |
| ----------------- | ----------- | ----------------------------------------------------- |

| Featured Speakers | Required    | Medium cards with essential info                      |

## Writing Guidelines

### Event Copywriting Voice

Event board text should be:

- **Energetic but not hyperbolic**: "42 speakers across 3 days" is exciting. "THE MOST
  AMAZING CONFERENCE EVER" is not credible.
- **Specific over vague**: "2,000 attendees from 34 countries" not "thousands of global
  attendees."
- **Action-oriented**: "Register at check-in, grab your badge, and head to the Main Stage
  for the 9:00 AM keynote" not "Attendees should proceed to registration."
- **Inclusive**: "All sessions include live captioning" not "Special accommodations available
  upon request."

### Zone Description Formula

Each event zone should have a description following this pattern:

```
[Zone Name]
[One-sentence purpose]: What happens here and why it matters.
[Key details]: Capacity, hours, notable features.
[What to expect]: 2-3 activities or highlights within the zone.
[Practical info]: Access requirements, location reference, tips.
```

**Example: Community Hub**

```
Community Hub
The beating heart of the event — where connections happen between sessions.

Capacity: 200 | Open all day, both days | Bean bags + standing tables

What to expect:
- Community Meetup Board: Pin your city, find locals to connect with
- Open Source Showcase: 8 project booths with live demos
- Birds of a Feather: Self-organized discussion circles — sign-up sheet posted daily
- Photo Wall: Polaroid station + event hashtag #InnovateTech2026

Community Manager on-site: Priya Sharma — Slack: @priya
Located between Main Stage and Networking Lounge — follow the yellow pathway.
```

### Session Description Formula

```
[Session Title]
Speaker: [Name], [Title] at [Company]
Time: [Day X, HH:MM - HH:MM] | Room: [Room Name] | Capacity: [N]
Level: [Beginner / Intermediate / Advanced]

[2-3 sentence abstract answering: What will attendees learn? Why does it matter?
What should they bring or prepare?]
```

**Example**:

```
"AI-Native Product Design"
Speaker: Dr. Amara Chen, VP Product at NovaTech
Time: Day 1, 9:00 - 10:00 AM | Main Stage | Capacity: 800
Level: All levels

The era of AI-augmented products is here, but most teams are bolting AI onto existing
workflows rather than designing AI-native experiences. Dr. Chen shares NovaTech's
framework for rethinking product design from the ground up with AI as a core capability,
not an add-on. She will walk through three real product transformations — from intent
detection to predictive interfaces — with before/after demos. Bring your laptop for
a live audience exercise.
```

### Speaker Bio Formula

```
[Name]
[Current Title] at [Company]
[1-2 sentence professional summary focusing on expertise and credibility]
[Notable achievement or stat that establishes authority]
Session: "[Talk Title]" — Day [X], [Time], [Room]
```

**Example**:

```
Dr. Amara Chen
VP Product at NovaTech

Leading product strategy for NovaTech's AI platform, serving 12M+ users across 140
countries. Previously at Google Research where she co-authored the "Attention Is All
You Need" architecture paper that became the foundation of modern LLMs.

Named one of Fast Company's Most Creative People in Business 2025.

Session: "AI-Native Product Design" — Day 1, 9:00 AM, Main Stage
```

## Real Content Examples

### Example: Event Statistics Banner

"InnovateTech Summit 2026 — March 18-20, San Francisco Convention Center"
"2,000 in-person attendees | 5,000 streaming | 42 speakers | 6 tracks | 3 days |
3 Platinum sponsors | 12 expo booths | ASL interpretation on Main Stage"

### Example: Registration Zone Content

**Registration & Check-In**
Your event starts here. Three lanes to get you in fast.

- **Pre-registered** (QR scan lane): Average 30 seconds per person. Have your QR code
  ready on your phone or printed confirmation.
- **Walk-in registration**: ID verification + on-site badge printing. Average 3 minutes.
- **VIP / Speaker lane**: Dedicated desk with welcome packet, premium lanyard, and
  green room access card.

Hours: Opens 7:30 AM (30 minutes before first session)
Staff: 12 volunteers + 2 event coordinators
Swag pickup: Tote bags, T-shirts (S-XL), sticker packs — available until supplies last.
Help desk: Located at registration — for schedule questions, accessibility needs, or
lost badges.

### Example: Sponsor Tier Content

**Platinum Sponsors** (3 booths, premier placement)

- TechCorp: "Building the future of enterprise AI" — Booth P1 (double-wide, demo station)
- CloudBase: "Infrastructure that scales with you" — Booth P2 (demo + meeting room)
- DevTools Inc.: "Developer productivity, supercharged" — Booth P3 (interactive demo wall)

**Gold Sponsors** (6 booths, standard placement)

- DataMesh: Booth G1 | StreamLine: Booth G2 | CodePilot: Booth G3
- NextWave: Booth G4 | QuantumLeap: Booth G5 | StackShift: Booth G6

**Expo Hall passport challenge**: Visit all 12 booths, collect stamps, enter the prize
drawing. Top prize: Conference ticket for next year + $500 travel credit.

### Example: Accessibility Information

**Accessibility at InnovateTech Summit 2026**

- **Wheelchair access**: All main areas are wheelchair accessible. Elevator located between
  Lobby and Level 2 (near the stairwell). Accessible seating reserved in rows 1-3 of
  Main Stage and row 1 of all breakout rooms.
- **ASL interpretation**: Available on Main Stage for all keynotes and panels. Request ASL
  for breakout sessions by March 10 — contact events@innovatetech.com.
- **Live captioning**: Real-time captions displayed on side screens during all Main Stage
  sessions. Transcript available within 2 hours of session end.
- **Quiet Zone**: Dedicated quiet room on Level 1, rear corridor. Noise-cancelling
  headphones available for lending at registration.
- **Lactation room**: Private, lockable room on Level 2 — key available at registration desk.
- **Dietary accommodations**: All meals labeled for allergens (gluten, dairy, nuts, soy).
  Vegan and halal options available at every meal station.
- **Service animals**: Welcome in all areas. Water bowls available at registration and the
  Networking Lounge.

## Data and Metrics on Event Boards

### Pre-Event Planning Metrics

- Expected attendance (in-person + virtual)
- Number of speakers, sessions, tracks
- Sponsor count and tier breakdown
- Budget allocation by category
- Staff and volunteer count

### Live Event Metrics (if board is used during the event)

- Real-time attendance per zone
- Session fill rates ("Session X: 92% full — overflow to Room B")
- WiFi usage and connectivity status
- Social media mentions (#InnovateTech2026: 4,200 tweets)

### Post-Event Metrics

- Actual vs. expected attendance
- Session ratings (4.3/5 average)
- NPS score (72)
- Top-attended sessions
- Sponsor satisfaction scores
- Budget actual vs. planned

## Questions Every Event Board Should Answer

### For Any Viewer (5-second scan)

1. What is this event called?
2. When and where is it?
3. How big is it? (Attendance, duration, number of speakers)

### For an Attendee (30-second exploration)

4. What zones/areas exist?
5. Who are the keynote speakers?
6. What is the schedule at a glance?

### For a Speaker (1-minute review)

7. When and where is my session?
8. What AV equipment is available?
9. Where is the green room?

### For a Sponsor (1-minute review)

10. Where is my booth located?
11. What foot traffic can I expect?
12. What promotional opportunities exist?

### For the Event Team (2-minute deep dive)

13. Are all zones accounted for with capacity, equipment, and staffing?
14. Is the schedule complete with no overlaps or gaps?
15. Are all logistics and contingencies documented?
16. Is accessibility fully addressed?

### For a Stakeholder (10-second glance)

17. Does this look professional and well-organized?
18. Is the scale appropriate for the budget?
19. Will attendees have a great experience?
