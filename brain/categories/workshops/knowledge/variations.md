# Workshop Boards — Variations

## Overview

Workshops vary enormously across dimensions: the facilitation goal (ideation vs.
reflection vs. learning vs. bonding), the group size (5 people vs. 40 people), the
session format (in-person vs. remote vs. hybrid), and the organizational context
(startup vs. enterprise vs. non-profit). This guide provides adaptation guidelines
for different workshop types, scales, and contexts.

---

## Workshop Type Variations

### Ideation / Brainstorm Workshops (5-20 participants, 30-90 min)

**Characteristics**: Generating and prioritizing ideas around a specific problem.
High energy, divergent-convergent rhythm, silent writing + group discussion + voting.

**Adaptations**:

- Use the Phase Flow pattern with 5 phases (Problem -> Ideation -> Cluster -> Vote -> Action)
- Ideation zone should be the widest phase (1200px+) to hold 50-100+ sticky notes
- Include constraint sticky notes (pink) to bound ideation productively
- Use Impact/Effort 2x2 matrix for prioritization, not just dot voting
- Action plan must include owner, deliverable, due date, sprint assignment
- Color palette: Workshop Purple (energetic, creative)
- Board size: 4000 x 2500px

### Retrospective / Reflection Workshops (4-12 participants, 30-60 min)

**Characteristics**: Reflecting on a past period to identify improvements. Structured
categories, emotional honesty, accountability tracking.

**Adaptations**:

- Use the Metaphor Canvas pattern (Sailboat, 4Ls, Start-Stop-Continue)
- Always include "Previous Retro Follow-Up" with status-coded action items
- Quadrant zones with emotionally coded colors (green=positive, red=negative, blue=goals, orange=risks)
- Silent writing before discussion is non-negotiable for equitable participation
- Dot voting to surface collective priorities, not facilitator selection
- Action items tagged to their source category for traceability
- Limit to 3-5 actions per session (more than that dilutes follow-through)
- Color palette: Workshop Purple with metaphor-appropriate zone colors
- Board size: 3500 x 2500px

### Design Thinking / Innovation Workshops (6-20 participants, 2-8 hours)

**Characteristics**: Human-centered problem-solving through empathy, definition,
ideation, prototyping, and testing. Complex, multi-phase, high-investment.

**Adaptations**:

- Use the Double Diamond pattern with 5 clearly delineated phases
- Include layered empathy activities (interview + observation + empathy map)
- Persona card must be specific and memorable — a real person, not a demographic
- HMW questions should narrow from 5+ options to 1 focused question
- Ideation includes structured techniques (Crazy Eights, Gallery Walk)
- Prototype phase includes storyboard panels AND paper prototype mockups
- Testing phase includes test script, feedback capture, and iteration notes
- For half-day sessions: compress Empathize to 30 min and merge Prototype+Test
- For multi-day sprints: expand each phase to a full day with detailed sub-boards
- Color palette: Workshop Purple with phase-specific tints (purple, blue, green, orange, red)
- Board size: 5000 x 3000px (single day) or hub + sub-boards (multi-day)

### Discussion / World Cafe Workshops (12-40 participants, 60-120 min)

**Characteristics**: Exploring complex topics through rotating small-group
discussions. Multiple tables, table hosts, layered rounds, collective harvesting.

**Adaptations**:

- Use the Circular Table pattern with 3-5 themed tables
- The Big Question must be genuinely open and include permission to challenge
- Table questions should contain inherent tension (success AND obstacle)
- Color-code rounds within each table to show conversation deepening
- Rotation guide must be explicit about which groups go where
- Harvest Wall is the most important zone — allocate at least 1000px height
- Include dual guides: lead facilitator guide + table host guide
- For smaller groups (12-20): reduce to 3 tables with 2 rounds
- For virtual sessions: use Miro breakout boards for each table, shared board for harvest
- Color palette: Workshop Purple or Strategic Grey (depending on audience)
- Board size: 5000 x 3500px

### Icebreaker / Team-Building Workshops (4-30 participants, 10-45 min)

**Characteristics**: Building rapport, psychological safety, and energy through
playful activities. Low-stakes, inclusive, optional participation.

**Adaptations**:

- Use the Activity Grid pattern with 3-6 independent activity zones
- Activities should vary in energy level and vulnerability required
- Include example responses that model expected quality and personality
- Facilitator guide should be a decision tree: which activity for which context
- Ground rules must emphasize "pass" option and psychological safety
- For remote teams: prioritize async-friendly activities (Photo Gallery, Fact Cards)
- For large groups (30+): skip high-interaction activities (Two Truths) and use breakout-compatible ones
- For recurring use: rotate activities weekly to prevent staleness
- Color palette: Warm Collaboration (inviting, playful)
- Board size: 3000 x 2500px

### Strategy / Planning Workshops (8-30 participants, 2-4 hours)

**Characteristics**: Aligning leadership on direction, goals, and resource allocation.
High-stakes, political complexity, need for structured decision-making.

**Adaptations**:

- Combine Phase Flow with 2x2 prioritization matrices
- Include data dashboards or pre-read summaries to ground discussion in facts
- Use anonymous voting (Miro's built-in feature) to reduce political influence
- Ensure equal representation: structured turn-taking or round-robin sharing
- Decision criteria should be explicit and agreed upon before evaluating options
- Output must be formal: OKR-style commitments, resource allocation decisions
- Include a "Decision Log" zone capturing what was decided, by whom, and why
- For executive audiences: use Strategic Grey palette and minimal decoration
- Color palette: Strategic Grey
- Board size: 4500 x 3000px

## Scale Variations

### Small Group (4-8 participants)

- Simpler board structure: 3-4 zones maximum
- Silent writing phase can be shorter (3-5 min instead of 10)
- Clustering can be done as a group conversation rather than silent affinity mapping
- All participants' contributions are visible without scrolling
- Dot voting: 2-3 votes per person
- No need for breakout groups — work as one unit
- Board size: 3000 x 2000px

### Medium Group (8-15 participants)

- Standard board structure: 5-7 zones
- Silent writing is essential (8-10 min)
- Clustering benefits from silent affinity mapping followed by group discussion
- May need to plan for 50-100 sticky notes in ideation zones
- Dot voting: 3 votes per person
- Consider breakout pairs for empathy interviews or prototyping
- Board size: 4000 x 2500px

### Large Group (15-30 participants)

- Use more structured facilitation: breakout groups for activities, full-group for voting
- Ideation zones need to be very large (1400px+ wide) to hold 100-200 sticky notes
- Use Miro's voting feature instead of manual dots (too many to count manually)
- Clustering should be done by a facilitator or small team while others observe
- Consider parallel tracks: 2-3 groups working on different aspects simultaneously
- Action items may need a separate "Action Planning Board" for detail
- Board size: 5000 x 3500px

### Very Large Group (30-50+ participants)

- Board-of-boards architecture: Hub board for overview + sub-boards for each group
- World Cafe format works well at this scale (5-6 tables, 4-6 groups)
- Use digital voting exclusively (manual dot voting is impractical)
- Consider using multiple facilitators, each managing a sub-group
- Harvest Wall becomes the primary collaborative artifact
- Remote-first large groups may need asynchronous phases (24-48 hours for ideation)
- Board size: Hub at 4000 x 3000px, sub-boards at 3000 x 2000px each

## Format Variations

### In-Person Workshops

- Board serves as the digital record of a physical session
- Participants may use physical sticky notes first, then photograph/transfer to Miro
- The board's primary value is as the POST-session artifact (searchable, shareable)
- Consider real-time typing during discussion: one person captures on the Miro board
  while others discuss
- Print a simplified version of the board as a table handout for reference

### Remote / Virtual Workshops

- Board is the ONLY shared workspace — it must do all the work
- Ensure all participants can edit simultaneously without lag
- Use Miro's follow-me mode for facilitator-guided navigation
- Increase font sizes by 2-4pt (screen sharing reduces readability)
- Add a 5-10 minute orientation: "This is how the board is organized, here is where
  you will contribute"
- Consider camera-on policy during icebreakers and discussions
- Use breakout rooms (Zoom/Teams) paired with board sub-sections for parallel work
- Timer tool is essential — without physical presence, time-awareness drops
- Async pre-work (silent ideation before the session) can save synchronous time

### Hybrid Workshops

- Board must serve both in-room participants and remote participants equally
- In-room: display board on a large screen; one person types contributions from the room
- Remote: participants contribute directly on the board via their laptops
- Assign a "bridge facilitator" to ensure remote voices are heard equally
- Voting must use the digital system (remote participants cannot walk up to a physical board)
- Test the hybrid setup before the session: screen visibility, microphone pickup, board responsiveness

### Asynchronous Workshops

- Board is set up with all phases and instructions, but participants contribute on
  their own time (usually 24-72 hours)
- Silent ideation becomes the ONLY mode (which eliminates production blocking entirely)
- Facilitator adds instructions for each phase with deadlines: "Add ideas by Tuesday 5 PM.
  Voting opens Wednesday 9 AM."
- Clustering may need to be done by the facilitator after ideation closes
- Discussion happens as comments on sticky notes rather than live conversation
- Action planning may require a synchronous meeting to finalize commitments
- Board size and structure are the same as synchronous versions

## Industry and Organizational Variations

### Software / Tech Teams

- Retrospective boards should reference agile terminology (sprints, stories, CI/CD)
- Brainstorm boards should include technical feasibility as an evaluation criterion
- Design thinking boards may include technical architecture sketches in the prototype phase
- Use Jira/Linear integration terminology in action items (story points, sprints, epics)

### Healthcare / Regulated Industries

- Design thinking should emphasize patient safety and regulatory compliance
- Brainstorm constraints must include regulatory boundaries (HIPAA, FDA, etc.)
- Action items need compliance review steps before implementation
- Personas should reflect diverse patient populations and accessibility needs

### Education / Non-Profit

- Workshop language should be jargon-free and accessible
- Brainstorm topics often center on program impact and community engagement
- Voting may need to be explicitly democratic (no weighted votes for leaders)
- Budget constraints should be a prominent factor in prioritization

### Enterprise / Corporate

- Workshops often involve multiple departments with competing priorities
- Use the Strategic Grey palette for executive audiences
- Include a stakeholder map to clarify decision-making authority
- Action items may require approval chains or budget allocation references
- World Cafe format is particularly effective for cross-functional alignment

### Startup / Early-Stage

- Workshops are often faster-paced with fewer participants
- Use the Innovation Green palette for high-energy sessions
- Brainstorms may need less structure (the team is small enough for organic collaboration)
- But still insist on silent writing and action items — small teams are not immune to
  groupthink or inaction
