# World Cafe Workshop Board

## Overview

- **Purpose**: A structured facilitation board for the World Cafe method — a conversational format where small groups rotate between themed discussion tables across multiple rounds, building on each other's ideas. At the end, a collective "Harvest Wall" captures the most powerful insights from all tables. This template is ideal for exploring complex, multi-faceted topics where diverse perspectives create richer understanding than any single conversation could.
- **Best For**: Leadership offsites, cross-functional strategy sessions, community gatherings, and organizational change initiatives with 12-40 participants, facilitated by one lead facilitator with table hosts.
- **Complexity**: Advanced
- **Board Size**: 5000 x 3500px

## Board Layout

The board features a welcome/instructions section at the top, 4 circular "table" frames in the middle (one per discussion topic), a rotation guide, and a large Harvest Wall at the bottom for collecting cross-table insights.

## Example Content

The World Cafe is fully pre-filled for an annual leadership offsite at Acme Corp (500 employees, B2B SaaS):

- **Big Question**: "What does Acme Corp need to become in the next 3 years?"
- **4 tables**: Culture & Values, Growth & Market, Product & Technology, People & Organization
- **3 rounds per table**: 12 sticky notes per table (4 per round), totaling 48 discussion points
- **Harvest Wall**: 3 key insights, 3 cross-table patterns, 3 surprises, 5 action seeds
- **28 participants** divided into 4 groups of 7, rotating 3 times
- **4 Table Hosts**: VP People, CRO, CTO, COO — each an executive sponsor for their theme
- **Total session time**: 75 minutes

## Variations

1. **3-Table Version**: For smaller groups (12-20), use 3 tables with groups of 4-6. Reduce to 2 rounds if time is tight.
2. **Virtual World Cafe**: Use Miro breakout boards for each table. Table Hosts share screen and facilitate via video. Rotation means moving between Zoom breakout rooms. Harvest happens on a shared Miro board.
3. **Topic-Specific World Cafe**: Instead of broad strategy, focus all 4 tables on one initiative from different angles (e.g., "New Product Launch" explored through Customer, Engineering, Marketing, and Support lenses).
4. **Open Space Hybrid**: Let participants choose which table to visit (no forced rotation). Tables self-organize around the most popular questions. Less structured but more energy-driven.
5. **Action-Oriented Cafe**: Replace Round 3 with an "Action Round" where each table defines 3 concrete next steps rather than more discussion.

## Build Instructions

1. Create board at 5000 x 3500px with Warm White (#FFF8E1) background.
2. Place the title/instructions banner (4900 x 450px) at the top with Deep Purple background.
3. Create 4 table frames (each 1100 x 1400px) arranged in a row. Each gets a unique pastel background and circular "table surface" shape.
4. Within each table, create 3 round zones (top, middle, bottom thirds). Label each round and add 4 pre-filled sticky notes per round.
5. Place the Rotation Guide (4900 x 300px) between the tables and the harvest wall.
6. Create the Harvest Wall (4900 x 1000px) at the bottom with amber-tinted background. Build 4 columns: Key Insights, Patterns, Surprises, Action Seeds.
7. Add the Facilitator Guide (1200 x 500px) at bottom-left and Table Host Guide (1200 x 500px) at bottom-right.
8. Draw clockwise rotation arrows between tables.
9. Draw dashed arrows from each table's Round 3 zone to the Harvest Wall.
10. Lock table questions, host names, and instructions. Leave all sticky note areas editable.

## Tips & Best Practices

- The Big Question is everything. Spend time crafting it before the session. It should be open enough to allow diverse perspectives but focused enough to drive actionable insights.
- Table Hosts are the linchpin. Brief them 30 minutes before the session. Their job is to listen, connect, and summarize — not to lead or lecture.
- Physical tables: Use butcher paper as tablecloths and encourage doodling. The messiest tables often have the best conversations.
- Virtual adaptation: Use a shared playlist of ambient music during rounds to create a "cafe atmosphere."
- The Harvest is where magic happens. Do not rush it. When someone says "Every table mentioned X," that is a signal of organizational alignment.
- Follow up within 48 hours with a photo of the Harvest Wall and the 5 action seeds. Assign owners and deadlines or the energy dissipates.
- World Cafe works best when there is genuine uncertainty at the top. If leadership already knows the answer, do not pretend to ask the question — people sense it and disengage.
- Serve actual coffee. The metaphor works better when the environment feels warm and informal.
