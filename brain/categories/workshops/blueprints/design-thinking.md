# Design Thinking Workshop — 5-Phase Board

## Overview

- **Purpose**: A comprehensive design thinking workshop board that guides teams through all five phases — Empathize, Define, Ideate, Prototype, and Test — with structured activities, time boxes, and deliverables for each phase. This template turns the abstract design thinking methodology into a concrete, step-by-step facilitation canvas.
- **Best For**: Design teams, product squads, and innovation labs running half-day or full-day design thinking workshops with 6-20 participants, facilitated by a design lead or Agile coach.
- **Complexity**: Advanced
- **Board Size**: 5000 x 3000px

The board flows left to right through 5 diamond-shaped phases, connected by arrows. Each phase is a large frame with specific activities inside. A facilitator strip runs along the top with timing and instructions. A shared resources bar sits at the bottom.

## Example Content

The workshop is fully pre-filled for a healthcare product team redesigning patient check-in:

- **Challenge**: 23-minute average check-in wait, 34% patient frustration
- **Persona**: Maria Gonzalez, 67, retired teacher managing chronic conditions
- **POV Statement**: Specific, empathy-driven problem framing
- **5 HMW questions** narrowed to one focused question
- **8 Crazy Eights ideas** from QR codes to facial recognition
- **3 winning ideas** selected by dot vote: QR + pre-populated screen, human greeter hybrid, companion app
- **6-panel storyboard** showing the end-to-end solution experience
- **3 paper prototype screens**: patient text, QR display, greeter tablet dashboard
- **5 test feedback items** from 3 testers, including concerns and edge cases
- **4 iterations** based on feedback, plus 4 concrete next steps with owners and dates

## Variations

1. **Half-Day Sprint (3 hours)**: Compress Empathize to 30 min (skip interviews, use pre-made personas). Merge Prototype + Test into a 40-min combined session.
2. **Multi-Day Design Sprint (5 days)**: Expand each phase to a full day. Add Lightning Demos on Day 1, Decision Matrix on Day 3, and real user testing on Day 5.
3. **Remote Workshop**: Add a 10-minute icebreaker at the start. Use Miro voting instead of physical dots. Add breakout room assignments for pair activities.
4. **B2B/Enterprise Focus**: Replace patient persona with a business user. Adjust activities for enterprise software workflows.

## Build Instructions

1. Create board at 5000 x 3000px with Warm White (#FFF8E1) background.
2. Place title banner (4900 x 350px) at top with Deep Purple background and 5-segment timeline.
3. Create 5 phase frames arranged left to right (widths: 1200, 900, 1000, 1000, 900px; all 2000px tall).
4. Color each phase frame with its designated tint (purple, blue, green, orange, red at 8% opacity).
5. Within each phase, add activity sub-sections with headers, instructions, and pre-filled sticky notes.
6. Build the empathy map as a 4-quadrant shape in Phase 1.
7. Build the persona card as a rounded rectangle with photo circle in Phase 2.
8. Build the Crazy Eights grid (4x2) as shapes in Phase 3.
9. Build the storyboard (6 panels) and phone/tablet mockups as shapes in Phase 4.
10. Add test feedback sticky notes in Phase 5 with green (positive) and orange (concerns).
11. Place the Resources bar (4900 x 400px) at the bottom with template cards.
12. Draw all flow arrows between phases and the feedback loop from Test back to Empathize.
13. Lock phase headers, instructions, and facilitator notes. Leave all sticky note areas editable.

## Tips & Best Practices

- Print the persona and post it where everyone can see it throughout the workshop — it keeps the team grounded in the user's reality.
- The Define phase is the hardest to facilitate. A strong POV statement takes practice — prepare examples beforehand.
- During Ideate, enforce "build on" rather than "tear down." Use "Yes, and..." language.
- Prototypes should be ugly. If they look polished, teams get attached and resist iteration.
- In Test, the facilitator must resist the urge to explain or defend the solution. Just listen.
- Time-box ruthlessly. Design thinking works because of constraints, not despite them.
- Photograph all physical artifacts (sticky notes, sketches) and upload to the Miro board for the permanent record.
- The iteration loop is the soul of design thinking — if you skip it, you are doing design theater, not design thinking.
