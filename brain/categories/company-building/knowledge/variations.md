# Company Building — Variations

## Overview

Company building boards must adapt to the company they describe — a 5-person pre-seed startup needs a fundamentally different board than a 500-person Series D company. The industry, stage, culture, and audience all shape the content, visual design, and level of detail. This guide covers the key variations and how to customize each board type for specific contexts.

---

## Variation by Company Stage

### Pre-Seed / Idea Stage (1-3 people)

**Board focus**: Why this problem matters and why this team can solve it.

**Vision Board adjustments**:

- Vision is the centerpiece — it is the main selling point
- Strategy is minimal (1-2 bets, not 4 pillars)
- Metrics are forward-looking targets, not historical data
- Milestone timeline is mostly future-facing
- Team section emphasizes founder backgrounds and why they are uniquely qualified

**Culture Canvas adjustments**:

- Typically not needed at this stage (culture IS the founders)
- If created, focus on founding principles (3 maximum)
- Keep it aspirational — "This is who we intend to be"

**Key content**:

```
Vision: [Bold, specific future state]
Problem: [Why this matters — data-backed]
Solution: [What we're building]
Team: [Founders + why them]
Ask: [What we need — investment, talent, customers]
```

### Seed Stage (3-15 people)

**Board focus**: We have product-market fit signals and a plan to scale.

**Vision Board adjustments**:

- Include early traction metrics (even if small)
- Strategic pillars emerge but may still be fluid
- Milestone timeline shows what you have achieved post-launch
- North Star metric defined

**Culture Canvas adjustments**:

- Now essential — culture is being formed with every hire
- 3-4 values, focused on how the founding team works
- Anti-values clarify what the culture is NOT
- Rituals are forming (weekly demo, retro, etc.)

**Org Growth Plan adjustments**:

- Simple: Current team + next 5-10 hires
- Focus on first leadership hires
- Budget context: "We can hire X people with seed funding"

**Investor Update adjustments**:

- Monthly cadence, very concise
- Key metrics: users, revenue (even if small), burn, runway
- Emphasize learning and iteration speed

### Series A (15-50 people)

**Board focus**: We have PMF and a repeatable growth engine. Now we scale.

**Vision Board adjustments**:

- Full strategic cascade with 3-4 pillars
- Robust metrics dashboard with trends
- Team snapshot showing department structure
- Milestone timeline balanced between past and future

**Culture Canvas adjustments**:

- Critical — this is the "culture codification" stage
- 4-5 values with full behavioral definitions
- Decision-making framework formalized
- Onboarding integration (new hires review this board)

**Org Growth Plan adjustments**:

- Full timeline: now, +6mo, +12mo, +18mo
- Department-level planning with roles specified
- Leadership pipeline identified
- Team topology decisions documented

**Investor Update adjustments**:

- Quarterly cadence (or monthly for active investors)
- Full dashboard: ARR, NRR, churn, CAC, LTV, burn, runway
- Structured wins/challenges with root cause analysis
- Specific asks section

### Series B+ / Growth Stage (50-200 people)

**Board focus**: We are scaling the machine. Efficiency, governance, and culture preservation.

**Vision Board adjustments**:

- Multi-board approach: master vision board + departmental strategy boards
- Metrics are more granular (segment-level, cohort-level)
- Board of directors governance section added
- International expansion or multi-product strategy visible

**Culture Canvas adjustments**:

- Culture preservation is the main challenge
- Add "culture health" metrics (eNPS, retention, Glassdoor)
- Manager-specific behavioral guidelines
- Cross-functional collaboration norms

**Org Growth Plan adjustments**:

- Complex: multiple reporting layers, VPs, directors
- Team topology evolution documented (e.g., moving to squad model)
- Reorg planning visible
- Compensation philosophy section

**Investor Update adjustments**:

- Board-quality governance materials
- Detailed financial model integration
- Market analysis and competitive positioning
- Strategic option evaluation

### Late Stage / Pre-IPO (200+ people)

**Board focus**: Governance, operational excellence, and public-readiness.

**Vision Board adjustments**:

- Corporate strategy format (more formal)
- Multi-year strategic plan (3-5 year horizon)
- Market positioning and competitive landscape
- ESG / sustainability / diversity metrics

**Culture Canvas adjustments**:

- Company-wide principles + team-level charters
- Diversity, equity, and inclusion section
- Global culture considerations (multi-office, multi-country)
- Manager training and leadership development

## Variation by Industry

### SaaS / B2B Software

**Content emphasis**:

- ARR, NRR, and LTV:CAC as primary metrics
- Product-led growth indicators (self-serve signups, time to value)
- Enterprise vs. SMB customer mix
- Platform strategy and API ecosystem

**Visual style**: Clean, professional, data-driven. Blue/gray palette. Minimal decoration.

**Unique sections**:

- Customer segmentation (SMB/Mid-Market/Enterprise)
- Pricing model visual
- Integration ecosystem map

### Consumer / B2C

**Content emphasis**:

- DAU/MAU ratio, retention curves, virality coefficient
- User acquisition channels and CAC by channel
- Brand awareness and social metrics
- Community engagement

**Visual style**: Vibrant, brand-forward, engaging. Use the consumer brand's visual identity.

**Unique sections**:

- User persona showcase
- Growth loop visualization
- Community metrics (social followers, UGC volume)

### FinTech

**Content emphasis**:

- Regulatory compliance status
- Trust and security certifications
- Transaction volume and processing metrics
- Risk management framework

**Visual style**: Professional, trust-forward. Deep blue/navy palette. Conservative but modern.

**Unique sections**:

- Compliance and licensing status
- Security architecture overview
- Regulatory roadmap

### HealthTech

**Content emphasis**:

- Patient outcome metrics
- Regulatory pathway (FDA, CE, etc.)
- Clinical validation data
- Provider adoption metrics

**Visual style**: Clean, clinical, trustworthy. Blue/green palette. Balanced warmth and authority.

**Unique sections**:

- Clinical evidence summary
- Regulatory timeline
- Patient impact stories

### Marketplace / Platform

**Content emphasis**:

- Supply and demand metrics (both sides)
- Take rate and GMV
- Liquidity metrics (match rate, time to transaction)
- Network effects indicators

**Visual style**: Dynamic, balanced, showing both sides. Often uses split-view layouts.

**Unique sections**:

- Two-sided metric dashboard (supply + demand)
- Network effects visualization
- Market coverage map

### Climate / Impact

**Content emphasis**:

- Impact metrics alongside financial metrics
- Carbon reduction or environmental measurements
- ESG compliance
- Partnership with governments/NGOs

**Visual style**: Green-forward, mission-driven. Natural tones. Impact-first narrative.

**Unique sections**:

- Impact dashboard (CO2 saved, lives improved, etc.)
- Theory of change visualization
- SDG (Sustainable Development Goals) alignment

## Variation by Audience

### Internal Team (All-Hands)

**Adjustments**:

- Use informal, energetic language
- Include cultural references and inside jokes
- Show individual/team contributions and shoutouts
- Display employee engagement metrics
- Include upcoming events, celebrations, milestones
- Can use company-specific terminology without explanation

**Content additions**:

- "What's new this quarter" section
- Team member spotlights
- Hiring pipeline (so team can refer candidates)
- Fun stats (Slack messages sent, PRs merged, coffees consumed)

### Investors / Board of Directors

**Adjustments**:

- Professional, formal tone
- Confidentiality banner required
- Metrics-forward with business context
- Structured wins/challenges format
- Financial transparency (revenue, burn, runway)
- Always include the ask section

**Content additions**:

- Cap table summary (if fundraising)
- Competitive landscape update
- Key risk factors
- Board-requested follow-ups

### Candidates / Recruiting

**Adjustments**:

- Aspirational and opportunity-focused
- Emphasize growth, learning, and impact
- Show team culture and diversity
- Highlight unique perks and working style
- Remove sensitive financial details

**Content additions**:

- Open roles with growth path
- Employee testimonials or day-in-the-life
- Benefits and perks summary
- Company stage and trajectory (where you fit in the story)

### Customers / Partners

**Adjustments**:

- Customer-impact focused
- Show product roadmap and reliability
- Emphasize trust, stability, and growth
- Remove internal operations details

**Content additions**:

- Customer success stories
- Product reliability metrics (uptime, response time)
- Partnership model explanation
- Roadmap with customer-relevant features highlighted

## Variation by Culture Type

### Remote-First

**Culture Canvas emphasis**:

- Async communication norms (Loom, docs, 24hr response)
- Timezone overlap expectations
- Written decision-making (RFCs, PRDs)
- Virtual ritual schedule (social calls, virtual offsites)
- Home office setup support

**Visual style**: Digital-native, clean, high-contrast for screen readability.

### In-Office

**Culture Canvas emphasis**:

- Office environment and spaces
- In-person collaboration rituals
- Team lunches, happy hours, offsites
- Commute/location considerations
- Office-specific traditions

**Visual style**: Can include office photos, floor plans, physical space imagery.

### Hybrid

**Culture Canvas emphasis**:

- In-office days and expectations
- Parity between remote and in-office participants
- Meeting norms for hybrid (one person remote = all cameras on)
- Tool usage for bridging in-office and remote
- Flexibility parameters

**Visual style**: Balanced, showing both worlds.

## Variation by Funding Model

### Venture-Backed

**Investor Update emphasis**:

- Growth metrics (ARR, MRR, growth rate)
- Path to next round (metrics needed)
- Burn rate and runway
- Capital efficiency (burn multiple)

**Tone**: Growth-oriented, ambitious, competitive.

### Bootstrapped / Profitable

**Vision Board emphasis**:

- Profitability metrics (net income, profit margin)
- Customer-funded growth metrics
- Independence and sustainability narrative
- Long-term value creation over rapid growth

**Tone**: Sustainable, confident, customer-centric.

### Non-Profit / Social Enterprise

**Vision Board emphasis**:

- Impact metrics alongside operational metrics
- Donor/funder relationship management
- Grant pipeline and fundraising status
- Program outcome measurements

**Tone**: Mission-driven, impact-first, transparent about challenges.

## Combining Variations

A real board combines multiple variations simultaneously. For example:

**A Series A FinTech startup with remote-first culture updating investors**:

- **Stage**: Series A (full strategic cascade, 4-5 values, emerging org structure)
- **Industry**: FinTech (compliance status, trust metrics, regulatory roadmap)
- **Audience**: Investors (professional, metrics-forward, specific asks)
- **Culture**: Remote-first (async norms, written decision-making)
- **Funding**: Venture-backed (growth metrics, path to Series B)

The builder selects elements from each variation and synthesizes them into a coherent board. The knowledge files provide the menu; the builder creates the meal.

## Summary

No two companies are alike, and no two company building boards should be identical. The variations described here — by stage, industry, audience, culture type, and funding model — provide the adaptation layer that transforms generic templates into specific, authentic organizational documents. Always start with the core template structure and apply the relevant variations for the company's unique context. The result should feel like it could only belong to one company — because it should.
